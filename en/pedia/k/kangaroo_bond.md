# Kangaroo Bond

## Introduction to Kangaroo Bond

A Kangaroo [bond](../b/bond.md) is a type of foreign [bond](../b/bond.md) issued in the Australian [market](../m/market.md) by non-Australian entities, denominated in Australian dollars (AUD). These instruments [offer](../o/offer.md) firms and governments outside Australia the opportunity to raise [capital](../c/capital.md) in the expansive and [liquid](../l/liquid.md) Australian [capital markets](../c/capital_markets.md).

## Characteristics of Kangaroo Bonds

### Denomination

Kangaroo bonds are denominated in Australian dollars (AUD). The [issuer](../i/issuer.md) may benefit if the AUD appreciates against their [home](../h/home.md) [currency](../c/currency.md), as they would repay the [debt](../d/debt.md) in strong [currency](../c/currency.md) terms. However, the reverse is also true, making the [issuer](../i/issuer.md) susceptible to [currency](../c/currency.md) [risk](../r/risk.md).

### Issuers

The issuers of Kangaroo bonds are often foreign governments, multinational corporations, and large financial institutions. These bonds allow these entities to diversify their borrowing sources and tap into the Australian [investor](../i/investor.md) base for more funding [options](../o/options.md).

### Market

The Australian [bond market](../b/bond_market.md) is considered relatively stable and [liquid](../l/liquid.md), providing an attractive platform for foreign issuers. It has stringent regulatory frameworks ensuring [transparency](../t/transparency.md) and credibility. The Australian [market](../m/market.md)'s depth offers sizable issuance capacity, making it appealing for large borrowing needs.

### Maturity

Kangaroo bonds can have varying maturities, typically ranging from short-term (1-3 years) to long-term (10 years or more). Depending on the [issuer](../i/issuer.md)â€™s requirements and [market](../m/market.md) conditions, different maturities can be chosen.

### Interest Rates

[Interest](../i/interest.md) rates on Kangaroo bonds are influenced by several factors, including the [credit rating](../c/credit_rating.md) of the [issuer](../i/issuer.md), prevailing [market](../m/market.md) [interest](../i/interest.md) rates, [economic conditions](../e/economic_conditions.md) in Australia, and [investor](../i/investor.md) appetite for foreign bonds. These bonds can [offer](../o/offer.md) either fixed or floating [interest](../i/interest.md) rates.

## Advantages of Kangaroo Bonds

### Diversification for Investors

For investors, Kangaroo bonds provide an opportunity to diversify their portfolios with non-Australian issuers while remaining in a constrained [risk](../r/risk.md) environment offered by the Australian [market](../m/market.md) structure.

### Access to Capital

For issuers, Kangaroo bonds allow access to Australian [capital](../c/capital.md), providing another source of funding outside their domestic [market](../m/market.md). This can be particularly beneficial when domestic markets are tight or when wanting to mitigate exposure to one [market](../m/market.md).

### Jurisdictional Benefits

Australia's [robust](../r/robust.md) financial regulation enhances the credibility of these instruments. The bonds are subject to Australian laws and regulatory frameworks, protecting the rights of investors and ensuring compliance.

## Risks Associated with Kangaroo Bonds

### Currency Risk

Foreign issuers face [currency](../c/currency.md) [risk](../r/risk.md) due to the AUD [denomination](../d/denomination.md). An unfavorable [exchange rate](../e/exchange_rate.md) movement could increase the cost of repaying the [debt](../d/debt.md).

### Interest Rate Risk

Both investors and issuers face [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) risks, particularly in volatile [economic conditions](../e/economic_conditions.md). A rise in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates could lead to a [capital](../c/capital.md) loss for investors and increased borrowing costs for issuers.

### Credit Risk

[Credit risk](../c/credit_risk.md) pertains to the likelihood that the [issuer](../i/issuer.md) might be unable to meet their [debt](../d/debt.md) [obligations](../o/obligation.md). However, this [risk](../r/risk.md) varies significantly with the [creditworthiness](../c/creditworthiness.md) of the [issuer](../i/issuer.md).

### Market Liquidity Risk

Although the Australian [market](../m/market.md) is relatively [liquid](../l/liquid.md), extraordinary conditions or lack of [demand](../d/demand.md) can lead to [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) risks, affecting the ease with which these bonds can be bought or sold without a substantial price concession.

## Prominent Issuers of Kangaroo Bonds

Several prominent international entities have issued Kangaroo bonds, including:

1. **[World Bank](../w/world_bank.md)**
   - The [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) has frequently accessed the Australian [bond market](../b/bond_market.md), considering it a reliable source for funding various global projects.
     - [World Bank Bonds](https://treasury.worldbank.org/en/about/unit/treasury/ibrd/funding-program)
  
2. **[International Finance Corporation](../i/international_finance_corporation.md) (IFC)**
   - Part of the [World Bank Group](../w/world_bank_group.md), the IFC issues Kangaroo bonds to [finance](../f/finance.md) [private sector](../p/private_sector.md) projects in developing countries.
     - [IFC Bonds](https://www.ifc.org/wps/wcm/connect/corp_ext_content/ifc_external_corporate_site/home)
  
3. **European Investment [Bank](../b/bank.md) (EIB)**
   - The EIB utilizes Kangaroo bonds to support sustainable development projects within and outside the [European Union](../e/european_union_(eu).md).
     - [EIB Bonds](https://www.eib.org/en/investor_relations/funding/index.htm)
  
4. **[Asian Development Bank](../a/asian_development_bank.md) (ADB)**
   - ADB raises funds through Kangaroo bonds to support its development projects across Asia and the Pacific.
     - [ADB Bonds](https://www.adb.org/work-with-us/investors)

## Regulatory and Tax Aspects

### Regulatory Environment

The Kangaroo [bond market](../b/bond_market.md) is regulated by the Australian Securities and Investments [Commission](../c/commission.md) (ASIC) and the Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA). Issuers must comply with local regulations, ensuring [transparency](../t/transparency.md) and protecting [investor](../i/investor.md) interests.

### Taxation

[Interest](../i/interest.md) [income](../i/income.md) from Kangaroo bonds is subject to Australian [withholding tax](../w/withholding_tax.md), which can affect the net [return](../r/return.md) to foreign investors. However, tax treaties between Australia and other countries may [offer](../o/offer.md) [tax relief](../t/tax_relief.md) or reduced [withholding tax](../w/withholding_tax.md) rates.

## Conclusion

Kangaroo bonds represent a strategic [financial instrument](../f/financial_instrument.md) for both investors seeking [diversification](../d/diversification.md) in high-quality foreign bonds and for issuers looking for alternative funding sources. However, like any investment, they come with inherent risks that need to be carefully managed. The blend of regulatory integrity, [market](../m/market.md) [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md), and potential returns makes Kangaroo bonds a notable feature of the global [capital markets](../c/capital_markets.md) sphere.
