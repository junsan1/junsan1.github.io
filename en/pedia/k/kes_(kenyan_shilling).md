# Kenyan Shilling (KES)

The Kenyan Shilling, abbreviated as KES, is the official [currency](../c/currency.md) of Kenya. It is subdivided into 100 cents. Introduced in 1966 to replace the East African Shilling, the Kenyan Shilling is issued and regulated by the Central [Bank](../b/bank.md) of Kenya (CBK). The [currency](../c/currency.md) plays a crucial role in Kenya's [economy](../e/economy.md), facilitating domestic transactions, international [trade](../t/trade.md), and investment. This document aims to provide an extensive overview of the Kenyan Shilling, including its history, regulatory framework, forex [market dynamics](../m/market_dynamics.md), and significance in [algorithmic trading](../a/accountability.md).

## History and Evolution

The Kenyan Shilling replaced the East African Shilling in 1966. Initially pegged [at par](../a/at_par.md) with the British Pound and later to the U.S. Dollar, the KES has undergone several changes in its [valuation](../v/valuation.md). Over the years, the Kenyan Shilling has evolved to reflect Kenya's [economic conditions](../e/economic_conditions.md), including periods of [devaluation](../d/devaluation.md), [inflation](../i/inflation.md), and economic reforms.

### Introduction and Early Years

- **1966**: Kenya introduced the Kenyan Shilling to replace the East African Shilling. 
- **1974**: The KES was pegged to the U.S. Dollar at a [fixed exchange rate](../f/fixed_exchange_rate.md).
- **1982**: The peg was abandoned, and the [currency](../c/currency.md) started to [float](../f/float.md) against the U.S. Dollar.
- **1993**: Kenya liberalized its forex [market](../m/market.md), allowing the KES to be fully determined by [market](../m/market.md) forces.

### Recent Developments

- **2010s**: Introduction of new banknotes and coins to enhance [security](../s/security.md) features.
- **2020**: Central [Bank](../b/bank.md) of Kenya introduced new [currency](../c/currency.md) designs to combat counterfeiting and promote a cashless [economy](../e/economy.md).

## Regulatory Framework

The Central [Bank](../b/bank.md) of Kenya (CBK) is the [principal](../p/principal.md) regulatory authority overseeing the issuance and management of the Kenyan Shilling. CBK's monetary policies, regulations, and interventions significantly impact the [value](../v/value.md) and stability of the KES.

### Central Bank of Kenya (CBK)

- **[Monetary Policy](../m/monetary_policy.md) Committee (MPC)**: The CBK's MPC is responsible for formulating [monetary policy](../m/monetary_policy.md) aimed at achieving price stability.
- **[Foreign Exchange Reserves](../f/foreign_exchange_reserves.md)**: The Central [Bank](../b/bank.md) maintains [foreign exchange reserves](../f/foreign_exchange_reserves.md) to stabilize the KES.
- **Regulatory Measures**: CBK issues guidelines and regulations to control [inflation](../i/inflation.md), manage [interest](../i/interest.md) rates, and ensure the stability of the banking sector.

For more information, visit the [Central Bank of Kenya](https://www.centralbank.go.ke/).
