# Margin Trading

[Margin](../m/margin.md) trading is a financial [investment strategy](../i/investment_strategy.md) where investors [trade](../t/trade.md) assets using borrowed funds from a [broker](../b/broker.md), allowing them to [leverage](../l/leverage.md) their positions. This means that investors can purchase more securities than they could with just their available [capital](../c/capital.md), amplifying both potential gains and potential losses. Here's a comprehensive overview of [margin](../m/margin.md) trading, its mechanisms, risks, benefits, and regulations.

### 1. Understanding Margin Trading

[Margin](../m/margin.md) trading involves borrowing [money](../m/money.md) from a [broker](../b/broker.md) to purchase securities. The [investor](../i/investor.md)'s account acts as [collateral](../c/collateral.md) for the [loan](../l/loan.md). The amount of [money](../m/money.md) that can be borrowed and the terms of [repayment](../r/repayment.md) are typically set by the brokerage [firm](../f/firm.md), subject to regulatory requirements.

#### 1.1. Margin Account
To engage in [margin](../m/margin.md) trading, an [investor](../i/investor.md) needs to [open](../o/open.md) a [margin account](../m/margin_account.md) with a brokerage [firm](../f/firm.md). This is different from a standard cash account, where all transactions must be conducted with the available funds.

#### 1.2. Initial Margin
When you [open](../o/open.md) a [margin](../m/margin.md) position, you're required to [deposit](../d/deposit.md) a minimum amount of cash or securities, known as the [initial margin](../i/initial_margin.md). This is typically a percentage of the total purchase price of the securities you wish to buy.

#### 1.3. Maintenance Margin
The [maintenance margin](../m/maintenance_margin.md) is the minimum [account balance](../a/account_balance.md) you must maintain, typically expressed as a percentage of the total [value](../v/value.md) of your [margin](../m/margin.md) positions. If your [account balance](../a/account_balance.md) falls below this level due to adverse price movements, you'll receive a [margin call](../m/margin_call.md).

### 2. Mechanics of Margin Trading

[Margin](../m/margin.md) trading can be leveraged for both buying securities (long positions) and selling securities short (short positions). 

#### 2.1. Long Positions
When taking a long position, you're betting that the price of the [security](../s/security.md) [will](../w/will.md) rise. For example, if you have $10,000 and your [broker](../b/broker.md) has a [margin](../m/margin.md) requirement of 50%, you can borrow an additional $10,000 to purchase $20,000 worth of securities.

#### 2.2. Short Positions
In [short selling](../s/short_selling.md), you're betting that the price of the [security](../s/security.md) [will](../w/will.md) fall. You borrow [shares](../s/shares.md) from your [broker](../b/broker.md) and sell them at the current [market price](../m/market_price.md), hoping to buy them back at a lower price in the future, [return](../r/return.md) the borrowed [shares](../s/shares.md), and pocket the difference.

### 3. Risks of Margin Trading

#### 3.1. Amplified Losses
Just as [margin](../m/margin.md) trading can amplify gains, it can also amplify losses. If the [value](../v/value.md) of your securities falls, the losses are magnified because you're losing both your own invested funds and the borrowed funds.

#### 3.2. Margin Calls
A [margin call](../m/margin_call.md) occurs when the [equity](../e/equity.md) in your [margin account](../m/margin_account.md) falls below the [maintenance margin](../m/maintenance_margin.md) requirement. You must then [deposit](../d/deposit.md) more funds or sell some securities to bring your [account balance](../a/account_balance.md) back up. Failure to meet a [margin call](../m/margin_call.md) can result in your [broker](../b/broker.md) liquidating your assets without your consent.

#### 3.3. Interest Costs
Borrowing funds incurs [interest](../i/interest.md), which can significantly eat into your profits. The [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) is typically a variable rate set by the [broker](../b/broker.md) and is subject to change.

### 4. Benefits of Margin Trading

#### 4.1. Increased Purchasing Power
[Margin](../m/margin.md) trading increases your [purchasing power](../p/purchasing_power.md), allowing you to take larger positions than you could with just your own [capital](../c/capital.md).

#### 4.2. Portfolio Diversification
With more [capital](../c/capital.md) available, you can diversify your investments across a wider [range](../r/range.md) of securities, potentially spreading [risk](../r/risk.md).

#### 4.3. Short Selling
[Margin](../m/margin.md) accounts enable [short selling](../s/short_selling.md), which isn't possible in a standard cash account. This allows for strategies that [profit](../p/profit.md) from declining markets.

### 5. Regulations and Requirements

#### 5.1. FINRA and the SEC
In the United States, [margin](../m/margin.md) trading is regulated by the Financial [Industry](../i/industry.md) Regulatory Authority ([FINRA](../f/finra.md)) and the Securities and [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) [Commission](../c/commission.md) (SEC). There are strict rules governing initial and maintenance margins.

#### 5.2. Reg T
Under Regulation T, the Federal Reserve Board has set [margin](../m/margin.md) requirements for securities transactions. This regulation allows investors to borrow up to 50% of the purchase price of securities.

#### 5.3. Broker-Specific Rules
Brokerage firms may have their own additional [margin](../m/margin.md) requirements and policies. For example, [Interactive Brokers](https://www.interactivebrokers.com/en/index.php?f=4985) has specific [margin](../m/margin.md) rates and requirements.

### 6. Margin Trading Strategies

#### 6.1. Leveraged Investing
This involves using [margin](../m/margin.md) to increase the size of your investment, thus potentially increasing returns. However, this strategy also increases [risk](../r/risk.md).

#### 6.2. Hedging
[Margin](../m/margin.md) trading can be used to [hedge](../h/hedge.md) against potential losses in your portfolio. For instance, [short selling](../s/short_selling.md) can [offset](../o/offset.md) long positions that may be declining in [value](../v/value.md).

#### 6.3. Arbitrage
Traders often use [margin](../m/margin.md) to take advantage of price discrepancies between markets or securities, buying low and selling high simultaneously.
