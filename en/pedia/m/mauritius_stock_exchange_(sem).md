# Mauritius Stock Exchange (SEM)

The Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) of Mauritius (SEM) is the [principal](../p/principal.md) stock [exchange](../e/exchange.md) in Mauritius. It was established in 1989 and has since evolved into a reputable and well-regulated platform for trading securities in the Indian Ocean region. The SEM has played a pivotal role in the economic development of Mauritius, providing a marketplace where investors can buy and sell a variety of financial instruments, including equities, bonds, and other securities. 

## History and Development

Since its inception, the Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) of Mauritius has made significant progress. It started with five [listed](../l/listed.md) companies and has grown steadily to encompass a diverse [range](../r/range.md) of financial products. Over the years, the [exchange](../e/exchange.md) has adopted modern technology and regulatory standards to ensure [efficiency](../e/efficiency.md) and [transparency](../t/transparency.md) in its operations. 

The SEM has also been recognized internationally for its standards and practices. It is a member of the World Federation of Exchanges (WFE) and the African Securities Exchanges Association (ASEA). It has incorporated international [best practices](../b/best_practices.md) in its regulatory framework, aligning itself with global standards to attract [foreign investment](../f/foreign_investment.md).

## Market Segments

The SEM operates two main [market](../m/market.md) segments:

- **Official [Market](../m/market.md)**: This is the [principal](../p/principal.md) [market](../m/market.md) where most of the large and [mid-cap](../m/mid-cap.md) companies are [listed](../l/listed.md). It acts as the primary [trading platform](../t/trading_platform.md) and includes equities, [debt](../d/debt.md) securities, and other financial instruments.
  
- **Development & Enterprise [Market](../m/market.md)**: This [market segment](../m/market_segment.md) targets small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and newly established companies with high growth potential. It offers a less stringent regulatory environment compared to the Official [Market](../m/market.md), encouraging more SMEs to go public.

## Financial Instruments

The SEM supports the trading of various financial instruments:

- **Equities**: [Shares](../s/shares.md) of companies [listed](../l/listed.md) on the [exchange](../e/exchange.md). These are the most commonly traded instruments.
  
- **Bonds**: Government and corporate bonds, providing a fixed-[income](../i/income.md) investment option.
  
- **[Exchange](../e/exchange.md)-Traded Funds (ETFs)**: These are [marketable securities](../m/marketable_securities.md) that track an [index](../i/index.md), [commodity](../c/commodity.md), or basket of assets like an [index fund](../i/index_fund.md) but [trade](../t/trade.md) like a stock on the [exchange](../e/exchange.md).
  
- **Structured Products**: These are pre-packaged investment strategies based on [derivatives](../d/derivatives.md).

## Technological Framework

The SEM employs advanced technology to facilitate trading and ensure [market](../m/market.md) integrity. Some of the key technological components include:

- **Automated Trading System (ATS)**: The SEM uses an electronic [trading platform](../t/trading_platform.md) that supports straight-through processing and efficient [trade](../t/trade.md) [execution](../e/execution.md). This system ensures [transparency](../t/transparency.md) and fairness in the trading process.
  
- **Central [Depository](../d/depository.md) & Settlement System (CDS)**: This system handles the [clearing and settlement](../c/clearing_and_settlement.md) of all trades executed on the SEM. It operates on a T+3 settlement cycle, ensuring that transactions are completed within three [business](../b/business.md) days.

## Regulatory Environment

The Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) of Mauritius operates under the supervision of the Financial Services [Commission](../c/commission.md) (FSC) of Mauritius. The FSC is responsible for ensuring the integrity of the [market](../m/market.md), protecting investors, and promoting [transparency](../t/transparency.md). The SEM itself has established strict [listing requirements](../l/listing_requirements.md) and continuous [disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) [obligations](../o/obligation.md) to maintain [market](../m/market.md) confidence.

## Performance and Indices

The performance of the SEM is tracked using various indices:

- **SEMDEX**: The SEMDEX is a [market capitalization](../m/market_capitalization.md)-[weighted](../w/weighted.md) [index](../i/index.md) that includes all [listed](../l/listed.md) companies. It serves as a barometer of the overall [market](../m/market.md) performance.
  
- **SEM-10**: The SEM-10 is a tradable [index](../i/index.md) consisting of the top 10 largest and most [liquid](../l/liquid.md) [stocks](../s/stock.md) on the SEM.
  
- **DEMEX**: This [index](../i/index.md) tracks the performance of companies [listed](../l/listed.md) on the Development & Enterprise [Market](../m/market.md).

## International Recognition

The SEM has garnered international recognition for its standards and practices. It has forged strategic partnerships with other major exchanges and financial institutions around the world. This global collaboration has facilitated cross-listings and enhanced [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) in the Mauritian [market](../m/market.md).

### Global Business Sector

Mauritius is a well-regarded jurisdiction for global [business](../b/business.md), and the SEM has played a crucial role in this aspect. The [exchange](../e/exchange.md) provides a platform for Global [Business](../b/business.md) Companies (GBCs) and other international entities to list their securities. This has attracted significant [foreign investment](../f/foreign_investment.md) and promoted Mauritius as a financial hub in the Indian Ocean region.

## Investment Opportunities

For investors, the SEM offers a [range](../r/range.md) of opportunities:

- **Domestic Investors**: Local investors can diversify their portfolios by [investing](../i/investing.md) in a variety of [listed](../l/listed.md) companies and financial instruments.
  
- **Foreign Investors**: The SEM provides a gateway for foreign investors to access the Mauritian [market](../m/market.md) and other African markets through cross-listings and strategic partnerships.
  
- **Institutional Investors**: Institutional investors, such as mutual funds, pension funds, and [insurance](../i/insurance.md) companies, can [leverage](../l/leverage.md) the [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) and [transparency](../t/transparency.md) of the SEM to achieve their investment objectives.

## Key Figures and Statistics

As of the latest available data, the SEM boasts the following key figures:

- **Number of [Listed](../l/listed.md) Companies**: Approximately 90.
  
- **[Market Capitalization](../m/market_capitalization.md)**: Over USD 10 billion.
  
- **Average Daily [Turnover](../t/turnover.md)**: Around USD 1 million.
  
- **Indices**: Several key indices, including SEMDEX, SEM-10, and DEMEX.

### Examples of Major Listed Companies

- **New Mauritius Hotels**: A leading leisure and hospitality company.

To remain competitive and relevant, the SEM has undertaken several strategic initiatives:

- **Technological Upgrades**: Continuous investment in technology to enhance [trading systems](../t/trading_systems.md) and cybersecurity measures.
  
- **Regulatory Enhancements**: Ongoing updates to the regulatory framework to align with international standards and address emerging risks.
