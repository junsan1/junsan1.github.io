# Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)

Modified Adjusted [Gross Income](../g/gross_income.md) (MAGI) is a critical financial measure often used in the context of U.S. federal tax purposes. It modifies the Adjusted [Gross Income](../g/gross_income.md) (AGI) by factoring in several types of [income](../i/income.md) that are otherwise excluded from the AGI calculation. MAGI is especially consequential because it's used to determine eligibility for various tax benefits, credits, and sometimes program eligibility, including contributions to Roth IRAs and eligibility for [health insurance](../h/health_insurance.md) subsidies through the [Affordable Care Act](../a/affordable_care_act.md) (ACA). Understanding how to calculate and utilize MAGI is essential for optimizing one's financial and tax situation.

## Definition of Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

Before delving into MAGI, it's essential to understand what AGI is since MAGI is derived from AGI. Adjusted [Gross Income](../g/gross_income.md) (AGI) is your total [gross income](../g/gross_income.md) for the year, including wages, dividends, [capital](../c/capital.md) gains, [business](../b/business.md) [income](../i/income.md), and other sources of [income](../i/income.md), minus specific deductions. These deductions can include things like retirement plan contributions, student [loan](../l/loan.md) [interest](../i/interest.md), or tuition and fees.

### Key Components of AGI:
- **[Gross Income](../g/gross_income.md)**: This encompasses all [income](../i/income.md) received in the form of [money](../m/money.md), goods, property, and services that are not exempt from tax.
- **Adjustments to [Income](../i/income.md)**: These include deductions such as contributions to a [traditional IRA](../t/traditional_ira.md), student [loan](../l/loan.md) [interest](../i/interest.md) paid, and certain tuition payments.

## Calculating AGI:
The formula for AGI is straightforward:
```
AGI = [Gross Income](../g/gross_income.md) - Adjustments to [Income](../i/income.md) 
```

## Definition of Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)

MAGI modifies the AGI by adding back certain deductions and exclusions, effectively increasing the base [income](../i/income.md) measure for eligibility purposes for various tax benefits and credits. The adjustments added back to AGI can vary based on the specific [tax benefit](../t/tax_benefit.md) or program in question.

### Typical Add-backs for MAGI Calculation:
- **Non-taxable [Social Security](../s/social_security.md) Benefits**: Any part of [Social Security](../s/social_security.md) benefits not included in your [gross income](../g/gross_income.md).
- **[Tax-exempt Interest](../t/tax-exempt_interest.md)**: [Interest](../i/interest.md) [income](../i/income.md) not subject to federal tax, such as [interest](../i/interest.md) from [municipal bonds](../m/municipal_bonds.md).
- **Foreign [Earned Income](../e/earned_income.md) and Housing Exclusions**: Amounts excluded from [income](../i/income.md) under the [foreign earned income exclusion](../f/foreign_earned_income_exclusion.md) or the foreign housing exclusion.
- **Student [Loan](../l/loan.md) [Interest](../i/interest.md) [Deduction](../d/deduction.md)**: Amounts deducted for student [loan](../l/loan.md) [interest](../i/interest.md).
- **Retirement Contributions**: Deductions for contributions to certain retirement plans.

### Sample Formula for MAGI:
```
MAGI = AGI + Non-taxable [Social Security](../s/social_security.md) Benefits + [Tax-exempt Interest](../t/tax-exempt_interest.md) + Excluded Foreign [Income](../i/income.md) + Other Specific Deductions
```

## Importance of MAGI

### Eligibility for Roth IRA Contributions:
MAGI is used to determine eligibility for making contributions to a [Roth IRA](../r/roth_ira.md). There are specific MAGI thresholds set by the IRS, and these thresholds determine how much, if anything, you can contribute to a [Roth IRA](../r/roth_ira.md) for a given year.

For instance, in 2023:
- **Single Filers**: Contributions begin to phase out at a MAGI of $138,000 and are completely phased out at $153,000.
- **[Married Filing Jointly](../m/married_filing_jointly.md)**: Contributions begin to phase out at a MAGI of $218,000 and are completely phased out at $228,000.

### Deduction of Traditional IRA Contributions:
For those covered by a retirement plan at work, MAGI also affects the deductibility of contributions to a [traditional IRA](../t/traditional_ira.md).

### Premium Tax Credits for Health Insurance:
MAGI is used to determine eligibility for [premium](../p/premium.md) tax credits under the [Affordable Care Act](../a/affordable_care_act.md) (ACA), which can help lower the cost of [health insurance](../h/health_insurance.md) premiums for plans purchased through the [Health Insurance Marketplace](../h/health_insurance_marketplace.md).

### Other Tax Credits:
Several other tax benefits and credits, such as the American Opportunity [Tax Credit](../t/tax_credit.md) (AOTC) and the [Lifetime Learning Credit](../l/lifetime_learning_credit.md), rely on MAGI as a determining [factor](../f/factor.md) for eligibility.

## Steps to Calculate MAGI

### Step-by-Step Breakdown:
1. **Start with your AGI**: Begin by calculating your Adjusted [Gross Income](../g/gross_income.md) using IRS Form 1040.
2. **Add Back Non-[taxable Income](../t/taxable_income.md)**: Include any non-taxable [Social Security](../s/social_security.md) benefits.
3. **Add Back [Tax-exempt Interest](../t/tax-exempt_interest.md)**: Include any [tax-exempt interest](../t/tax-exempt_interest.md) [income](../i/income.md).
4. **Include Foreign [Earned Income](../e/earned_income.md)**: Add any excluded foreign [earned income](../e/earned_income.md).
5. **Include Other Deductions**: Add back any specific deductions relevant to the [tax benefit](../t/tax_benefit.md) or [credit](../c/credit.md) you're evaluating, such as student [loan](../l/loan.md) [interest](../i/interest.md) or retirement contributions.

### Example Calculation:
Assume you have the following [income](../i/income.md) and deductions:
- AGI: $60,000
- Non-taxable [Social Security](../s/social_security.md) Benefits: $5,000
- [Tax-exempt Interest](../t/tax-exempt_interest.md): $1,000
- [Foreign Earned Income Exclusion](../f/foreign_earned_income_exclusion.md): $2,000

```
MAGI = $60,000 (AGI) + $5,000 (Non-taxable [Social Security](../s/social_security.md)) + $1,000 ([Tax-exempt Interest](../t/tax-exempt_interest.md)) + $2,000 (Foreign [Earned Income](../e/earned_income.md))
MAGI = $68,000
```

## Common Mistakes and Tips

### Common Mistakes:
1. **Ignoring Specific Add-backs**: Not including relevant add-backs can result in an incorrect MAGI.
2. **Using Wrong AGI**: Using an estimated AGI instead of the actual AGI from your [tax return](../t/tax_return.md).
3. **Misunderstanding [Deduction](../d/deduction.md) Limits**: Ignoring phase-out ranges for deductions can lead to errors in MAGI calculations.

### Tips for Accurate Calculation:
1. **Consult IRS Guidelines**: Always refer to the latest IRS guidelines for the specific tax year.
2. **Use Reliable Software**: Tax software can streamline the calculation process.
3. **Keep Detailed Records**: Maintain thorough records of all [income](../i/income.md) sources and deductions to ensure nothing is missed in your MAGI calculation.
