# Market Profile Trading

[Market](../m/market.md) Profile Trading is a [technical analysis](../t/technical_analysis.md) tool used by traders to understand the [distribution](../d/distribution.md) of price over a specific time period. Developed by Peter Steidlmayer, a [trader](../t/trader.md) at the Chicago Board of [Trade](../t/trade.md) (CBOT), [Market](../m/market.md) Profile Trading provides a unique perspective on the [market dynamics](../m/market_dynamics.md) and helps traders identify [key price levels](../k/key_price_levels.md), [market](../m/market.md) trends, and potential trading opportunities. The methodology revolves around the concept of organizing price and time data into a [distribution](../d/distribution.md) curve, allowing traders to analyze the behavior of [market](../m/market.md) participants.

## Historical Background and Development

Peter Steidlmayer introduced [Market](../m/market.md) Profile in the early 1980s to [offer](../o/offer.md) a better way to visualize and understand [market](../m/market.md) activity. The innovation came at a time when traditional bar charts and [candlestick](../c/candlestick.md) charts were the main tools for traders. The [Market](../m/market.md) Profile methodology represented a significant advancement by providing a three-dimensional view of [market](../m/market.md) data, considering both price and time.

CBOT endorsed this new methodology and started providing [Market](../m/market.md) Profile charts to its members. Over time, the concept has been integrated into various trading platforms, and its application has expanded to [multiple](../m/multiple.md) [asset](../a/asset.md) classes, including [stocks](../s/stock.md), [futures](../f/futures.md), and forex.

## Core Components of Market Profile

[Market](../m/market.md) Profile is built on several key components, each serving a specific role in analyzing [market](../m/market.md) behavior:

### Time Price Opportunity (TPO)

A Time Price Opportunity (TPO) is the basic building block of a [Market](../m/market.md) Profile. TPOs represent the amount of time the [market](../m/market.md) spends at a particular [price level](../p/price_level.md). Typically, TPOs are represented by letters or blocks, and each letter/block corresponds to a specific time interval (e.g., 30 minutes).

### Initial Balance (IB)

The Initial Balance (IB) represents the [range](../r/range.md) of prices traded during the first hour of the regular [trading session](../t/trading_session.md). It acts as a critical reference point as it helps to identify the boundaries within which the majority of the trading activity is expected to occur.

### Value Area (VA)

The [Value](../v/value.md) Area (VA) is the price [range](../r/range.md) within which a specified percentage (commonly 70%) of all trading activity took place during the day. It highlights the most accepted prices and is considered a vital area for [price discovery](../p/price_discovery.md).

### Point of Control (POC)

The Point of Control (POC) is the [price level](../p/price_level.md) at which the most TPOs were recorded during the [trading session](../t/trading_session.md). It represents the price with the highest [market](../m/market.md) acceptance and is often regarded as an [equilibrium](../e/equilibrium.md) point.

## Construction of a Market Profile Chart

Creating a [Market](../m/market.md) Profile chart involves several steps and understanding different representations:

### Step 1: Collecting Price and Time Data

To construct a [Market](../m/market.md) Profile chart, collect intraday price data and segment it into specific time intervals (e.g., 30-minute segments). This data is essential for [distribution](../d/distribution.md) analysis.

### Step 2: Identifying TPOs

Assign a unique identifier (letter or block) to each time interval and record the price levels at which trades occurred within that period. This organizes the data into a time-price format.

### Step 3: Developing the Profile

Stack the TPOs vertically to create a [histogram](../h/histogram.md)-like structure. This vertical stacking provides a visual representation of the price [distribution](../d/distribution.md) over time, highlighting areas with high and low trading activity.

### Step 4: Analyzing Key Levels

Identify the Initial Balance (IB), [Value](../v/value.md) Area (VA), Point of Control (POC), and other significant price levels. These levels serve as reference points for analyzing [market dynamics](../m/market_dynamics.md) and potential trading opportunities.

## Benefits of Market Profile Trading

[Market](../m/market.md) Profile Trading offers several advantages for traders, helping them make informed decisions in the [market](../m/market.md):

### Enhanced Market Transparency

[Market](../m/market.md) Profile provides a clear and transparent view of [market](../m/market.md) activity, revealing price levels with high acceptance and areas of low participation. This [transparency](../t/transparency.md) aids traders in understanding [market sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md) and potential turning points.

### Identification of Key Price Levels

By highlighting the Initial Balance, [Value](../v/value.md) Area, and Point of Control, [Market](../m/market.md) Profile identifies critical price levels that influence [market](../m/market.md) behavior. Traders can use these levels as reference points for setting entry, exit, and [stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md).

### Improved Trade Management

[Market](../m/market.md) Profile’s structured approach helps traders manage their trades more effectively. By understanding price acceptance and rejection areas, traders can adjust their strategies and manage [risk](../r/risk.md) more efficiently.

### Insights into Market Behavior

The visualization of time and price data provides valuable insights into [market](../m/market.md) participants' behavior. Traders can gauge the strength of trends, identify [consolidation patterns](../c/consolidation_patterns.md), and anticipate potential breakouts or reversals.

## Market Profile Trading Strategies

Several [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md) [leverage](../l/leverage.md) [Market](../m/market.md) Profile’s insights to [capitalize](../c/capitalize.md) on [market](../m/market.md) opportunities:

### Trend Following

Traders using a [trend](../t/trend.md)-following strategy follow the direction established by the Initial Balance and the [Value](../v/value.md) Area. If the price breaks above the [Value](../v/value.md) Area, it indicates a potential upward [trend](../t/trend.md), while a break below suggests a potential downward [trend](../t/trend.md).

### Value Area Reversion

In this strategy, traders anticipate price reversion towards the [Value](../v/value.md) Area. If the price moves away from the [Value](../v/value.md) Area temporarily, it might revert back as the [market](../m/market.md) seeks [equilibrium](../e/equilibrium.md). Trades are placed to exploit these reversion opportunities.

### Point of Control Breakouts

Breakouts from the Point of Control (POC) are significant events. Traders look for strong price moves away from the POC, signalling a shift in [market sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md). Positions are taken in the direction of the [breakout](../b/breakout.md), with tight stop-losses to manage [risk](../r/risk.md).

### Auction Market Theory

[Auction Market](../a/auction_market.md) Theory is the foundation of [Market](../m/market.md) Profile trading, focusing on [market](../m/market.md) auction processes. Traders analyze how buyers and sellers establish [value](../v/value.md) through the auction process, using this understanding to predict price movements.

By embracing [Market](../m/market.md) Profile, traders can enhance their decision-making process, manage trades more effectively, and identify profitable trading opportunities in diverse [market](../m/market.md) conditions.
