# Michael Bloomberg

Michael [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) is a prominent figure in the world of [finance](../f/finance.md), media, and politics. He is best known as the co-founder, CEO, and majority owner of [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) L.P., a global financial services, software, and media company that he established in 1981. Beyond his [business](../b/business.md) achievements, Michael [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) has also made significant strides in public service, having served as the Mayor of New York City from 2002 to 2013, and actively engaging in philanthropic endeavors.

## Early Life and Education

Michael Rubens [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) was born on February 14, 1942, in Boston, Massachusetts. He grew up in a middle-class family and attended Johns Hopkins University, where he earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering. He went on to receive a Master of [Business](../b/business.md) Administration (MBA) from [Harvard Business School](../h/harvard_business_school.md).

## Career Beginnings

[Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) began his career on [Wall Street](../w/wall_street.md) with Salomon Brothers, a significant investment [bank](../b/bank.md) at the time. He started in an entry-level position and eventually climbed the ranks to become a partner. However, in 1981, Salomon Brothers was acquired, and [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) was laid off. This event became a turning point in his career, leading him to conceive the idea for [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) L.P.

## Founding Bloomberg L.P.

In 1981, with a vision to create a data and analytics service for the financial community, [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) took his severance from Salomon Brothers and founded Innovative [Market](../m/market.md) Systems (IMS), which was later renamed [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) L.P. The flagship product, known as the [Bloomberg Terminal](../b/bloomberg_terminal.md), revolutionized the [industry](../i/industry.md) by providing [real-time market data](../r/real-time_market_data.md), financial analytics, and trading tools to subscribers.

The [Bloomberg Terminal](../b/bloomberg_terminal.md) rapidly gained traction among [Wall Street](../w/wall_street.md) firms, owing to its comprehensive data offerings and user-friendly interface. Over time, the terminal became indispensable for traders, analysts, and portfolio managers, establishing [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) L.P. as a leader in financial information services.

## Expansion and Diversification

Under [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md)'s [leadership](../l/leadership.md), the company diversified its services and expanded its global footprint. [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) L.P. ventured into various segments, including:

### Bloomberg News

Launched in 1990, [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) News provides extensive coverage of [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md), [economics](../e/economics.md), and politics. The news service operates across [multiple](../m/multiple.md) media platforms, including television, radio, internet, and print, delivering timely and insightful news to a global audience.

### Bloomberg TV

[Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) Television is a 24-hour global network delivering [business](../b/business.md) and financial news. It features a [range](../r/range.md) of programs, including [market](../m/market.md) analysis, interviews with [industry](../i/industry.md) leaders, and live coverage of major financial events.

### Bloomberg Businessweek

In 2009, [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) L.P. acquired Businessweek magazine from McGraw-Hill and rebranded it as [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) Businessweek. The magazine provides in-depth coverage of [business](../b/business.md) trends, [economics](../e/economics.md), and technology, [offering](../o/offering.md) a blend of news and feature articles.

### Bloomberg BNEF (Bloomberg New Energy Finance)

[Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) New Energy [Finance](../f/finance.md) specializes in research and analysis of the [energy sector](../e/energy_sector.md), focusing on clean energy, advanced transport, and emerging technologies. BNEF provides data, analytics, and insights to help stakeholders navigate the evolving energy landscape.

### Bloomberg Law, Bloomberg Government, and Bloomberg Environment

These specialized services [offer](../o/offer.md) comprehensive legal, government, and environmental news and analysis, catering to professionals in these sectors with tailored information and tools.

## Political Career

In 2001, Michael [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) entered the political arena and successfully ran for Mayor of New York City as a Republican. He served three terms from 2002 to 2013, implementing policies that impacted education, public health, and urban development. His tenure as mayor was marked by efforts to improve the city's public schools, reduce crime rates, and promote [economic growth](../e/economic_growth.md).

### Key Initiatives as Mayor

- **Public Health**: [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) championed several public health initiatives, including a smoking ban in indoor workplaces and restaurants, calorie labeling in chain restaurants, and promoting healthier food [options](../o/options.md).
- **Education Reform**: He pursued reforms aimed at improving the quality of public education in New York City, such as increasing accountability and expanding charter schools.
- **Economic Development**: [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) focused on attracting businesses to the city and revitalizing neighborhoods. His administration worked on large-scale projects like the redevelopment of the waterfront and the construction of new housing units.

## Philanthropy

Michael [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) is a committed philanthropist through his foundation, [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) Philanthropies. The foundation supports initiatives in public health, education, the environment, the arts, and government innovation. It has made substantial donations to various causes, such as combating smoking, reducing traffic fatalities, and addressing climate change.

## Presidential Campaign

In 2020, Michael [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) entered the race for the Democratic nomination for President of the United States. He self-funded his campaign, emphasizing issues like gun control, climate change, and economic inequality. Despite a strong start, he withdrew from the race after the Super Tuesday primaries and endorsed Joe Biden.

## Legacy and Influence

Michael [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md)'s influence extends beyond his [business](../b/business.md) empire and political career. His work has significantly impacted the financial [industry](../i/industry.md) through the innovations introduced by [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) L.P. His public health policies and philanthropic efforts have also created lasting changes in society.

Throughout his diverse career, [Bloomberg](../b/bloomberg.md) has demonstrated a blend of innovative [business](../b/business.md) acumen, effective public service, and generative [philanthropy](../p/philanthropy.md), making him a notable figure in modern history.
