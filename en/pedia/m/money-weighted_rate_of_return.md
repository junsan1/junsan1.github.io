# Money-Weighted Rate of Return

The [Money](../m/money.md)-[Weighted](../w/weighted.md) [Rate of Return](../r/rate_of_return.md) (MWRR) is a measure of the performance of an investment portfolio that accounts for the timing and amount of cash flows. Unlike the Time-[Weighted](../w/weighted.md) [Rate of Return](../r/rate_of_return.md) (TWRR), which focuses on the performance of the portfolio itself devoid of external cash flows such as deposits and withdrawals, the MWRR directly incorporates the effects of these cash flows. This metric is particularly valuable for individual investors and financial advisors who need to evaluate the performance of portfolios that experience varying contributions and withdrawals over time.

## Key Concepts and Definitions

### Rate of Return

The [rate of return](../r/rate_of_return.md) refers to the [percentage change](../p/percentage_change.md) in the [value](../v/value.md) of an investment over a specified period. It is a fundamental concept in [finance](../f/finance.md) and [investing](../i/investing.md), used to measure the [gain](../g/gain.md) or loss generated on an investment. Typically, it is expressed as a percentage of the initial investment amount.

### Cash Flows

Cash flows are the [money](../m/money.md) transfers into or out of an investment portfolio. They can include deposits (additions), withdrawals (subtractions), dividends, and [interest](../i/interest.md) payments. In the context of MWRR, cash flows are crucial as they affect the calculation of the overall [return](../r/return.md), taking into account their timing and magnitude.

### Internal Rate of Return (IRR)

The [Money](../m/money.md)-[Weighted](../w/weighted.md) [Rate of Return](../r/rate_of_return.md) is essentially the Internal [Rate of Return](../r/rate_of_return.md) (IRR) of a portfolio. The IRR is the [discount rate](../d/discount_rate.md) that makes the net [present value](../p/present_value.md) (NPV) of all cash flows (both positive and negative) from a particular investment equal to zero. This means that the MWRR, or IRR, represents the annualized effective compounded [return](../r/return.md) rate that can be earned on the [invested capital](../i/invested_capital.md), considering the specific timing of cash flows.

## Calculation of Money-Weighted Rate of Return

To calculate the MWRR, the following steps are generally undertaken:

1. **List All Cash Flows:** Identify and list all cash flows associated with the investment over the given period. Cash inflows (deposits) are considered positive, while cash outflows (withdrawals) are considered negative.
   
2. **Determine the Time Periods:** Specify the timing of each [cash flow](../c/cash_flow.md). This involves noting the exact dates of each contribution and [withdrawal](../w/withdrawal.md).

3. **Formula Application:** Apply the IRR formula, which is based on solving for the [discount rate](../d/discount_rate.md) (r) in the following equation:
   
   \[
   \sum_{t=0}^{N} \frac{C_t}{(1 + r)^{t}} = 0
   \]
   
   Here, \(C_t\) represents the [cash flow](../c/cash_flow.md) at time t, r is the IRR (MWRR), and N is the total number of periods.

Given that solving the IRR may involve iterative [numerical methods](../n/numerical_methods_in_trading.md), financial calculators, spreadsheet software like Microsoft Excel, or specialized financial software are often used.

## Example

Consider an investment scenario involving the following cash flows:

- Initial investment of $10,000 on January 1, 2021.
- [Deposit](../d/deposit.md) of $2,000 on July 1, 2021.
- [Withdrawal](../w/withdrawal.md) of $1,500 on January 1, 2022.
- Ending portfolio [value](../v/value.md) of $12,500 on December 31, 2022.

We need to solve for r (MWRR):

\[
2000 \times (1 + r)^{-1.5} - 1500 \times (1 + r)^{-1} + 12500 \times (1 + r)^{-2} - 10000 = 0
\]

The solution of this equation would give us the MWRR.

## Importance and Applications of MWRR

### Individual Investors

For individual investors managing their personal portfolios, understanding the MWRR helps in assessing how well their investment decisions and the timing of their contributions and withdrawals have impacted their overall returns.

### Financial Advisors

Financial advisors use MWRR to provide their clients with a clear picture of their investment performance considering real cash flows, which is crucial for personalized [financial planning](../f/financial_planning.md) and advising.

### Portfolio Managers

Portfolio managers who [handle](../h/handle.md) accounts with frequent cash flows can [leverage](../l/leverage.md) MWRR to [offer](../o/offer.md) more customized performance evaluations to their clients. It helps in identifying the effectiveness of managing investments in the face of variable cash flows.

### Financial Planning

MWRR is valuable for [financial planning](../f/financial_planning.md) and [forecasting](../f/forecasting.md). It allows planners to incorporate expected future cash flows into their models, giving a realistic view of potential future performance.

### Performance Metrics Comparison

MWRR can be used in conjunction with other [performance metrics](../p/performance_metrics.md) like TWRR for a comprehensive analysis. While MWRR reflects the [investor](../i/investor.md)'s specific experience, TWRR can be useful for comparing the performance of different managers or investment strategies on an even footing.

## Limitations of MWRR

### Sensitivity to Cash Flow Timing

The MWRR is highly sensitive to the timing and size of cash flows. Large contributions or withdrawals near the start or end of the measurement period can disproportionately affect the [return](../r/return.md), which may not always be indicative of the portfolioâ€™s performance.

### Complexity

Calculating MWRR can be complex, requiring [numerical methods](../n/numerical_methods_in_trading.md) and financial software, especially when there are frequent and irregular cash flows. This complexity may deter some investors from using this metric.

### Not Ideal for Short Time Frames

For very short time frames, MWRR may produce misleading results due to its sensitivity to cash flows. In such cases, other measures such as the Simple [Rate of Return](../r/rate_of_return.md) might be more appropriate.

## Tools for Calculating MWRR

### Financial Calculators

Many financial calculators have built-in functions to compute IRR, which can be used to find the MWRR by inputting the sequence of cash flows.

### Spreadsheet Software

Software like Microsoft Excel offers the IRR() function, which simplifies the computation of MWRR. By entering the cash flows into a [range](../r/range.md) of cells, the IRR() function can be used to solve for the MWRR.
