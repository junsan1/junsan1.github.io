# Momentum Trading

[Momentum](../m/momentum.md) trading is a strategy in which traders buy securities that are rising in price and sell them in the future at a higher price. The core principle behind [momentum](../m/momentum.md) trading is that securities which have shown recent strong performance [will](../w/will.md) continue to perform in the same manner until the [momentum](../m/momentum.md) weakens. Essentially, it leverages the idea that "the [trend](../t/trend.md) is your friend." 

This approach is based on the assumption that prices [will](../w/will.md) continue to move in the same direction as they currently are. [Momentum](../m/momentum.md) traders look for assets that are moving strongly in one direction on high [volume](../v/volume.md) and believe that a strong [trend](../t/trend.md) [will](../w/will.md) continue in that direction for a while, long enough for them to capture profits.

#### Key Components of Momentum Trading

1. **[Trend Analysis](../t/trend_analysis.md):**
   [Momentum](../m/momentum.md) traders emphasize the importance of identifying trends. There are several methods to analyze trends, including moving averages, [Relative Strength](../r/relative_strength.md) [Index](../i/index_instrument.md) (RSI), MACD (Moving Average Convergence [Divergence](../d/divergence.md)), and [price action analysis](../p/price_action_analysis.md).

2. **[Relative Strength](../r/relative_strength.md):**
   The concept of [relative strength](../r/relative_strength.md) measures how an [asset](../a/asset.md) is performing in relation to the [market](../m/market.md) or its [peer group](../p/peer_group.md). Traders use this to pinpoint strong-performing securities.

3. **[Volume](../v/volume.md):**
   Trading [volume](../v/volume.md) is crucial in [momentum](../m/momentum.md) trading. High [volume](../v/volume.md) indicates strong [interest](../i/interest.md) and activity in a [security](../s/security.md), which typically accompanies strong price movements.

4. **Entry and Exit Points:**
   [Momentum](../m/momentum.md) trading requires precise timing for buying and selling. Entry points are often identified through [breakout](../b/breakout.md) signals, while exit points are determined by trailing stops or [profit](../p/profit.md) targets.

5. **[Risk Management](../r/risk_management.md):**
   Effective [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) strategies are vital. This includes [stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md) to protect against significant losses, [position sizing](../p/position_sizing.md), and [portfolio diversification](../p/portfolio_diversification.md).

#### Popular Momentum Indicators

1. **Moving Averages:**
   - **Simple Moving Average (SMA):** The average of a [security](../s/security.md)'s price over a specific time period.
   - **Exponential Moving Average (EMA):** Gives more weight to recent prices and reacts faster to price changes.

2. **[Relative Strength](../r/relative_strength.md) [Index](../i/index_instrument.md) (RSI):**
   - Measures the speed and change of price movements. RSI values above 70 indicate that a [security](../s/security.md) may be [overbought](../o/overbought.md), while values below 30 suggest it may be [oversold](../o/oversold.md).

3. **MACD:**
   - Utilizes two moving averages to identify [momentum](../m/momentum.md) changes. It consists of the MACD line, the signal line, and the [histogram](../h/histogram.md).

4. **On-Balance [Volume](../v/volume.md) (OBV):**
   - A [momentum](../m/momentum.md) [indicator](../i/indicator.md) that uses [volume](../v/volume.md) flow to predict changes in stock price. It accumulates [volume](../v/volume.md) to identify buying and selling pressure.

5. **[Bollinger Bands](../b/bollinger_bands.md):**
   - A [volatility](../v/volatility.md) [indicator](../i/indicator.md) that consists of a middle band (SMA) and two outer bands set at a [standard deviation](../s/standard_deviation.md) above and below the middle band.

#### Momentum Trading Strategies

1. **[Breakout Trading](../b/breakout_trading.md):**
   Traders look for [stocks](../s/stock.md) that are breaking out of a [range](../r/range.md) or are forming new highs, assuming that the price [will](../w/will.md) continue to rise. This often involves buying [stocks](../s/stock.md) that break above resistance levels.

2. **[Pullback](../p/pullback.md) Strategy:**
   During an [uptrend](../u/uptrend.md), prices often pull back to a previous support level. Traders use these pullbacks as opportunities to enter long positions before the price continues its upward trajectory.

3. **[Reversal](../r/reversal.md) Strategy:**
   Although riskier, some traders look for signs that a strong [trend](../t/trend.md) is about to reverse. This strategy often involves [short selling](../s/short_selling.md) [stocks](../s/stock.md) that have had a significant upward run but show signs of weakness.

4. **Pair Trading:**
   Involves buying a strong stock and selling a weak stock within the same sector, capturing the spread between the two.

5. **Seasonal Patterns:**
   Certain [stocks](../s/stock.md) exhibit seasonal behaviors. For example, retail [stocks](../s/stock.md) may perform well during the holiday season. Traders [leverage](../l/leverage.md) these patterns to make trades.

#### Challenges and Risks

1. **[Market](../m/market.md) [Volatility](../v/volatility.md):**
   The [market](../m/market.md) can change direction swiftly, and [momentum](../m/momentum.md) traders can face significant losses if caught on the wrong side of a [trade](../t/trade.md).

2. **Whipsaws:**
   Rapid price movements can trigger [false signals](../f/false_signals_in_trading.md), leading to [whipsaw](../w/whipsaw.md) trades where trades enter and exit positions frequently with no [profit](../p/profit.md).

3. **[Slippage](../s/slippage.md):**
   The difference between the expected price of a [trade](../t/trade.md) and the actual executed price can impact profits, especially in fast-moving markets.

4. **[Overtrading](../o/overtrading.md):**
   Constantly entering and exiting positions can lead to high [transaction costs](../t/transaction_costs.md) and reduced overall profitability.

#### Academic Support

[Momentum](../m/momentum.md) trading is supported by academic research, such as the work by Jegadeesh and Titman (1993) which demonstrates that [stocks](../s/stock.md) with high returns over the past three to twelve months continue to [outperform](../o/outperform.md) [stocks](../s/stock.md) with low past returns over the next three to twelve months. This concept, known as "[momentum](../m/momentum.md) effect," is considered a [market](../m/market.md) [anomaly](../a/anomaly.md) and has been widely cited in financial literature.
