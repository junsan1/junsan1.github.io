# Volume Indicators

[Volume indicators](../v/volume_indicators.md) play a critical role in trading, [offering](../o/offering.md) insights into the strength and sustainability of a [trend](../t/trend.md), the intensity of buying and selling pressure, and potential reversals. These indicators are built upon the [volume](../v/volume.md) of trading transactions over a certain period and provide additional context to price movements. Traders and investors use [volume indicators](../v/volume_indicators.md) to validate trends, predict reversals, and make informed decisions. This comprehensive guide explores the most widely used [volume indicators](../v/volume_indicators.md), their interpretation, and their application in trading.

## 1. On-Balance Volume (OBV)

On-Balance [Volume](../v/volume.md) (OBV) is a straightforward yet powerful [indicator](../i/indicator.md) that measures cumulative [volume](../v/volume.md) by adding the day's [volume](../v/volume.md) when the price closes up and subtracting it when the price closes down. OBV helps traders gauge whether [volume](../v/volume.md) is increasing or decreasing relative to price movements.

### Calculation:
\[ OBV = OBV_{\text{previous}} + \begin{cases} 
      \text{[Volume](../v/volume.md),} & \text{if } \text{Close}_{\text{today}} > \text{Close}_{\text{yesterday}} \\
      -\text{[Volume](../v/volume.md),} & \text{if } \text{Close}_{\text{today}} < \text{Close}_{\text{yesterday}} \\
      0, & \text{if } \text{Close}_{\text{today}} = \text{Close}_{\text{yesterday}} 
   \end{cases}
\]

### Interpretation:
- **Bullish Signal:** OBV rises when the price rises, indicating that [volume](../v/volume.md) is confirming the [uptrend](../u/uptrend.md).
- **Bearish Signal:** OBV falls when the price falls, indicating that [volume](../v/volume.md) is confirming the [downtrend](../d/downtrend.md).
- **[Divergence](../d/divergence.md):** If OBV moves in the opposite direction of the price, it may indicate that the current [trend](../t/trend.md) is weakening and a [reversal](../r/reversal.md) could occur.

## 2. Volume Price Trend (VPT)

The [Volume](../v/volume.md) Price [Trend](../t/trend.md) (VPT) [indicator](../i/indicator.md) combines price and [volume](../v/volume.md) to determine the strength of a price [trend](../t/trend.md). It calculates the [percentage change](../p/percentage_change.md) in price and multiplies it by the current [volume](../v/volume.md), adding this [value](../v/value.md) to a running total.

### Calculation:
\[ VPT = VPT_{\text{previous}} + \left( \frac{\text{Close}_{\text{today}} - \text{Close}_{\text{yesterday}}}{\text{Close}_{\text{yesterday}}} \right) \times \text{[Volume](../v/volume.md)} \]

### Interpretation:
- **[Uptrend Confirmation](../u/uptrend_confirmation.md):** VPT rises with increasing price and [volume](../v/volume.md).
- **[Downtrend](../d/downtrend.md) Confirmation:** VPT falls with decreasing price and [volume](../v/volume.md).
- **[Divergence](../d/divergence.md):** Similar to OBV, [divergence](../d/divergence.md) between VPT and price can signal potential reversals.

## 3. Accumulation/Distribution Line (A/D Line)

The Accumulation/[Distribution](../d/distribution.md) Line (A/D Line) is built upon the relationship between price and [volume](../v/volume.md). It emphasizes where the closing price is within the [range](../r/range.md) for the day and adjusts based on [volume](../v/volume.md), [offering](../o/offering.md) insight into the buying or selling pressure.

### Calculation:
\[ \text{A/D} = ((\text{Close} - \text{Low}) - (\text{High} - \text{Close})) / (\text{High} - \text{Low}) \times \text{[Volume](../v/volume.md)} \]
\[ \text{A/D Line} = \text{A/D Line}_{\text{previous}} + \text{A/D} \]

### Interpretation:
- **Buying Pressure:** When the A/D line is rising, it suggests more accumulation (buying) than [distribution](../d/distribution.md) (selling).
- **Selling Pressure:** When the A/D line is falling, it indicates more [distribution](../d/distribution.md) (selling) than accumulation (buying).
- **[Divergence](../d/divergence.md):** As with other indicators, [divergence](../d/divergence.md) between the A/D line and price can indicate potential [trend](../t/trend.md) reversals.

## 4. Chaikin Money Flow (CMF)

Developed by Marc Chaikin, the Chaikin [Money Flow](../m/money_flow.md) (CMF) measures the [money flow](../m/money_flow.md) [volume](../v/volume.md) over a specific period, usually 21 days. It is a [momentum](../m/momentum.md) [indicator](../i/indicator.md) that assesses the buying and selling pressure.

### Calculation:
\[ \text{CMF} = \frac{\text{Sum of } \left[ \left( \left( \text{Close} - \text{Low} \right) - (\text{High} - \text{Close}) \right) / (\text{High} - \text{Low}) \times \text{[Volume](../v/volume.md)} \right]}{\text{Sum of [Volume](../v/volume.md) over period}} \]

### Interpretation:
- **Positive CMF:** Indicates strong buying pressure.
- **Negative CMF:** Indicates strong selling pressure.
- **Crossing the Zero Line:** When CMF passes above 0, it suggests a bullish [trend](../t/trend.md), and below 0 indicates a bearish [trend](../t/trend.md).

## 5. Money Flow Index (MFI)

The [Money Flow](../m/money_flow.md) [Index](../i/index.md) (MFI) is a [volume](../v/volume.md)-[weighted](../w/weighted.md) version of the [Relative Strength](../r/relative_strength.md) [Index](../i/index.md) (RSI), combining price and [volume](../v/volume.md) data. It ranges from 0 to 100 and is termed as a [momentum](../m/momentum.md) [indicator](../i/indicator.md).

### Calculation:
\[ \text{Typical Price (TP)} = \frac{(\text{High} + \text{Low} + \text{Close})}{3} \]
\[ \text{Raw [Money Flow](../m/money_flow.md)} = \text{TP} \times \text{[Volume](../v/volume.md)} \]
\[ \text{[Money Flow](../m/money_flow.md) Ratio} = \frac{\text{Positive [Money Flow](../m/money_flow.md) (14-period)}}{\text{Negative [Money Flow](../m/money_flow.md) (14-period)}} \]
\[ \text{MFI} = 100 - \left( \frac{100}{1 + \text{[Money Flow](../m/money_flow.md) Ratio}} \right) \]

### Interpretation:
- **[Overbought](../o/overbought.md) (>80):** Indicates a potentially [overbought](../o/overbought.md) [market](../m/market.md), possible sell signal.
- **[Oversold](../o/oversold.md) (<20):** Indicates a potentially [oversold](../o/oversold.md) [market](../m/market.md), possible buy signal.
- **[Divergence](../d/divergence.md):** [Divergence](../d/divergence.md) between MFI and price can signal [trend](../t/trend.md) reversals.

## 6. Negative Volume Index (NVI)

The Negative [Volume](../v/volume.md) [Index](../i/index.md) (NVI) focuses on days when [volume](../v/volume.md) decreases from the previous day to indicate what [smart money](../s/smart_money.md) is doing. It's based on the idea that [smart money](../s/smart_money.md) is active on low-[volume](../v/volume.md) days.

### Calculation:
The NVI is calculated with adjustments only on days when the [volume](../v/volume.md) drops from the previous day:
\[ \text{NVI}_{\text{today}} = \begin{cases} 
      \text{NVI}_{\text{previous}} + \left( \frac{\text{Close}_{\text{today}} - \text{Close}_{\text{previous}}}{\text{Close}_{\text{previous}}} \times \text{NVI}_{\text{previous}} \right) & \text{if [volume](../v/volume.md) decreases} \\
      \text{NVI}_{\text{previous}} & \text{if [volume](../v/volume.md) does not decrease} 
   \end{cases}
\]

### Interpretation:
- **Rising NVI:** Indicates that price increases are being driven on low-[volume](../v/volume.md) days, suggesting the involvement of [smart money](../s/smart_money.md).
- **[Divergence](../d/divergence.md):** As with the other indicators, [divergence](../d/divergence.md) can be a signal of a potential change in [trend](../t/trend.md).

## 7. Positive Volume Index (PVI)

The Positive [Volume](../v/volume.md) [Index](../i/index.md) (PVI) is the counterpart to the Negative [Volume](../v/volume.md) [Index](../i/index.md), focusing on days with increased [volume](../v/volume.md). It represents the behavior of retail investors who are thought to be more active on high-[volume](../v/volume.md) days.

### Calculation:
The PVI adjust only on days when the [volume](../v/volume.md) increases:
\[ \text{PVI}_{\text{today}} = \begin{cases} 
      \text{PVI}_{\text{previous}} + \left( \frac{\text{Close}_{\text{today}} - \text{Close}_{\text{previous}}}{\text{Close}_{\text{previous}}} \times \text{PVI}_{\text{previous}} \right) & \text{if [volume](../v/volume.md) increases} \\
      \text{PVI}_{\text{previous}} & \text{if [volume](../v/volume.md) does not increase} 
   \end{cases}
\]

### Interpretation:
- **Rising PVI:** Suggests that price movements are strong and supported by high volumes.
- **[Divergence](../d/divergence.md):** Similar to NVI, [divergence](../d/divergence.md) can signal potential shifts in [trend](../t/trend.md).

## 8. Volume Oscillator

The [Volume Oscillator](../v/volume_oscillator.md) employs the relationship between two moving averages to determine the level of [volume](../v/volume.md) and discern [trend](../t/trend.md) strength.

### Calculation:
\[ \text{[Volume Oscillator](../v/volume_oscillator.md)} = \text{Short-term [Volume](../v/volume.md) MA} - \text{Long-term [Volume](../v/volume.md) MA} \]

### Interpretation:
- **Positive [Oscillator](../o/oscillator.md):** Indicates an increase in [volume](../v/volume.md), suggesting stronger trends.
- **Negative [Oscillator](../o/oscillator.md):** Indicates a decrease in [volume](../v/volume.md), suggesting weakening trends.
- **Crossovers:** When the short-term MA crosses above the long-term MA, it indicates increasing [volume](../v/volume.md) and vice versa.

## 9. Klinger Oscillator

Developed by Stephen Klinger, the [Klinger Oscillator](../k/klinger_oscillator.md) aims to show long-term [money flow](../m/money_flow.md) trends while remaining sensitive enough to detect short-term fluctuations.

### Calculation:
\[ KO = EMA_{34}(\text{[Volume](../v/volume.md)} \times \text{T}) - EMA_{55}(\text{[Volume](../v/volume.md)} \times \text{T}) \]

Where:
\[ \text{T} = \left( 2 \times (\text{Close} - \text{Low} - \text{High})) / (\text{High} - \text{Low}) \right) \]

### Interpretation:
- **Bullish Klinger:** When the [Klinger Oscillator](../k/klinger_oscillator.md) is positive and rising, it indicates [money flow](../m/money_flow.md) is entering the [market](../m/market.md).
- **Bearish Klinger:** When the [Klinger Oscillator](../k/klinger_oscillator.md) is negative and falling, it indicates [money flow](../m/money_flow.md) is leaving the [market](../m/market.md).
- **[Divergence](../d/divergence.md):** As usual, [divergence](../d/divergence.md) between the [Klinger Oscillator](../k/klinger_oscillator.md) and price movements can indicate potential reversals.

## Conclusion

[Volume indicators](../v/volume_indicators.md) are essential tools in trading, providing insights that price movements alone cannot. By incorporating [volume analysis](../v/volume_analysis.md) into their [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md), traders can [gain](../g/gain.md) a deeper understanding of [market dynamics](../m/market_dynamics.md), confirm trends, and anticipate potential reversals. Each [volume](../v/volume.md) [indicator](../i/indicator.md) has its unique strengths and usages, and traders often use a combination of [multiple](../m/multiple.md) indicators to increase the robustness of their analysis.
