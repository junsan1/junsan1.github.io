# Viatical Settlement

A viatical settlement is a financial arrangement where a terminally ill policyholder sells their [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policy to a [third party](../t/third_party.md) at a discounted rate from its [face value](../f/face_value.md). This arrangement allows the policyholder to access a portion of the [death benefit](../d/death_benefit.md) while they are still alive, providing them with immediate financial support for their medical bills, living expenses, and other costs associated with their terminal illness. The purchaser of the policy, often referred to as the viatical settlement provider, then becomes the new [beneficiary](../b/beneficiary.md) of the policy and assumes responsibility for paying the remaining premiums. Upon the policyholder's death, the purchaser collects the full [death benefit](../d/death_benefit.md) from the [insurance](../i/insurance.md) company.

## Historical Background

Viatical settlements emerged in the United States during the 1980s, primarily as a response to the AIDS epidemic. Many individuals with AIDS faced significant financial challenges due to their inability to work and the high cost of medical treatments. To alleviate some of these burdens, they began selling their [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policies for a percentage of the [death benefit](../d/death_benefit.md), providing them with necessary funds to cover expenses and improve their [quality of life](../q/quality_of_life.md) during their remaining time.

Regulations and oversight of the viatical settlement [industry](../i/industry.md) have evolved significantly since its inception. Initially, the [industry](../i/industry.md) was relatively unregulated, which led to potential risks for both policyholders and investors. However, in response to concerns about consumer protection and fraudulent practices, many states in the U.S. have since implemented regulations to govern viatical settlements and ensure fair practices.

## Key Players

1. **Policyholders**: These are individuals with a [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policy who have been diagnosed with a terminal illness and have a [life expectancy](../l/life_expectancy.md) of typically two years or less. They seek to access the [value](../v/value.md) of their policy to manage medical and living expenses.

2. **Viatical Settlement Providers**: These are companies or individuals who purchase [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policies from terminally ill policyholders. They typically [offer](../o/offer.md) a percentage of the policy's [face value](../f/face_value.md), customarily ranging from 50% to 80%, depending on various factors including the insured's [life expectancy](../l/life_expectancy.md) and the policy details.

3. **Investors**: In some cases, the viatical settlement [market](../m/market.md) includes investors who purchase these policies from the providers. These investors anticipate earning a [profit](../p/profit.md) when the insured individual passes away, and they receive the full [death benefit](../d/death_benefit.md) minus any remaining [premium](../p/premium.md) payments and costs.

4. **[Insurance](../i/insurance.md) Companies**: These entities initially [issue](../i/issue.md) the [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policies and are responsible for paying out the death benefits to the policyholder's beneficiaries, or in the case of a viatical settlement, to the viatical settlement provider or [investor](../i/investor.md) who purchases the policy.

## Process of a Viatical Settlement

1. **Evaluation**: The policyholder contacts a viatical settlement provider to express their [interest](../i/interest.md) in selling their [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policy. The provider evaluates the policy, including [face value](../f/face_value.md), type, and the insured individual's medical prognosis to determine the [offer](../o/offer.md).

2. **[Offer](../o/offer.md)**: If the provider decides to proceed, they make an [offer](../o/offer.md) to the policyholder. This [offer](../o/offer.md) is often a lump sum [payment](../p/payment.md) that represents a percentage of the policy's [face value](../f/face_value.md).

3. **Acceptance**: The policyholder reviews the [offer](../o/offer.md), and if agreeable, they accept the terms. This acceptance typically involves signing a viatical settlement contract.

4. **Transfer**: Upon acceptance, the ownership of the policy is transferred from the policyholder to the viatical settlement provider. The provider assumes responsibility for paying any future premiums.

5. **[Payout](../p/payout.md)**: The policyholder receives the agreed lump sum [payment](../p/payment.md). 

6. **[Death Benefit](../d/death_benefit.md) Collection**: Upon the death of the insured, the viatical settlement provider or [investor](../i/investor.md) contacts the [insurance](../i/insurance.md) company to collect the full [death benefit](../d/death_benefit.md).

## Regulatory Framework

In the United States, the regulation of viatical settlements is primarily handled at the state level. Some of the key regulatory measures include:
- **Licensing Requirements**: Viatical settlement providers and brokers are often required to obtain a license to operate in many states. This ensures that they meet certain standards of practice and ethics.
  
- **[Disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) Requirements**: States typically require full [disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) to the policyholder about the terms of the viatical settlement, including the amount they [will](../w/will.md) receive, any fees involved, and the process of transferring policy ownership.
  
- **Consumer Protections**: Many states impose consumer protection regulations to safeguard policyholders, such as ensuring confidentiality, providing rescission periods, and requiring [transparency](../t/transparency.md) in the [transaction](../t/transaction.md).

- **[Fraud](../f/fraud.md) Prevention**: To mitigate fraudulent activities, regulations often include measures to verify the authenticity of medical records, the legitimacy of [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policies, and the good faith of viatical settlement providers and brokers.

The National Association of [Insurance](../i/insurance.md) Commissioners (NAIC) has also developed a model Viatical Settlements Act to standardize and guide state regulations.

## Financial Implications

For policyholders, a viatical settlement offers several financial advantages:
- **Immediate Funds**: Accessing the [value](../v/value.md) of a [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policy while still alive can provide crucial financial support, easing the burden of medical and daily living expenses.
- **No Future Premiums**: After selling the policy, the policyholder is relieved of the obligation to pay future premiums, which can be significant especially if financial resources are limited.

For viatical settlement providers and investors, the key financial incentives include:
- **Potential Profits**: By purchasing policies at a [discount](../d/discount.md) and collecting the full [death benefit](../d/death_benefit.md) upon the insured's death, providers and investors can realize substantial returns. 

However, there are also risks to consider:
- **Longevity [Risk](../r/risk.md)**: If the policyholder lives longer than expected, the purchaser must continue paying premiums, reducing the net [profit](../p/profit.md).
- **Legal and Regulatory Risks**: Changes in regulation or legal challenges can impact the ease of doing [business](../b/business.md) and the profitability of viatical settlements.

## Ethical Considerations

The viatical settlement [industry](../i/industry.md) raises several ethical concerns:
- **Exploitative Practices**: Critics argue that these arrangements can be exploitative, taking advantage of vulnerable individuals in desperate financial situations.
- **Confidentiality**: Ensuring the confidentiality of the policyholderâ€™s medical and personal information is paramount. Breaches of confidentiality can cause additional stress and harm to the policyholder.

Despite these concerns, proponents argue that viatical settlements [offer](../o/offer.md) a necessary financial tool for those facing terminal illness, allowing them to maintain dignity and improve their [quality of life](../q/quality_of_life.md).

## Viatical Settlements vs. Life Settlements

It is important to distinguish between viatical settlements and life settlements. While both involve selling a [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policy, the primary differences lie in the condition of the policyholder and the expected [payout](../p/payout.md):
- **Viatical Settlements**: These are specifically for terminally ill individuals with a limited [life expectancy](../l/life_expectancy.md). The [payout](../p/payout.md) tends to be higher due to the shorter expected [duration](../d/duration.md) of [premium](../p/premium.md) payments.
  
- **Life Settlements**: Life settlements are for older individuals (usually seniors) who are not necessarily terminally ill but still wish to sell their [life insurance](../l/life_insurance.md) policies. The [life expectancy](../l/life_expectancy.md) of the insured is typically longer, resulting in a lower [payout](../p/payout.md) compared to viatical settlements.
