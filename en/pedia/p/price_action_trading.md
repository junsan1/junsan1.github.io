# Price Action Trading

[Price Action](../p/price_action.md) Trading (PAT) is a trading technique that utilizes historical [market](../m/market.md) prices, typically the [open](../o/open.md), high, low, and close (OHLC) of a price bar or [candlestick](../c/candlestick.md), to make trading decisions. Unlike other [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md) that rely on [lagging indicators](../l/lagging_indicators.md) or complex algorithms, [price action](../p/price_action.md) traders focus on the raw price data. This approach aims to make sense of the immediate [market](../m/market.md) behavior and predict future price movements based on patterns that repeat over time.

### Fundamentals of Price Action Trading
[Price action](../p/price_action.md) trading is based on the premise that all relevant information is already reflected in the price. Advocates of PAT believe that price is the ultimate [indicator](../i/indicator.md) and that it discounts the need for additional tools like moving averages, oscillators, or even [fundamental analysis](../f/fundamental_analysis.md) in some cases. The core idea is to 'read' the [market](../m/market.md) by looking at the patterns and formations that prices create over periods, whether intraday, daily, weekly, or monthly.

### Historical Context
The concept of [price action](../p/price_action.md) trading dates back to the early days of stock and [commodity](../c/commodity.md) trading. Before the advent of modern technology, traders used to chart prices by hand to identify trends and patterns. Over time, these observations evolved into a more systematic approach, leading to the development of various techniques and strategies that are collectively known as [price action](../p/price_action.md) trading.

### Key Elements of Price Action Trading

#### **Candlestick Patterns**
[Candlestick](../c/candlestick.md) charts are one of the most popular tools used by [price action](../p/price_action.md) traders. These charts provide a visual representation of price movements within a specified time frame and are composed of individual 'candles' that show the [open](../o/open.md), high, low, and close prices.

- **Bullish Patterns**: Patterns like the [Bullish Engulfing Pattern](../b/bullish_engulfing_pattern.md), Hammer, and [Morning Star](../m/morning_star.md) indicate potential upward movements.
- **Bearish Patterns**: Patterns such as the [Bearish Engulfing Pattern](../b/bearish_engulfing_pattern.md), [Shooting Star](../s/shooting_star.md), and [Evening Star](../e/evening_star.md) suggest possible downward trends.

#### **Support and Resistance Levels**
[Support and resistance](../s/support_and_resistance.md) are critical concepts in [price action](../p/price_action.md) trading. [Support levels](../s/support_levels.md) act as a floor below which prices tend not to fall, while resistance levels are ceilings where prices often struggle to rise above.

- **Identification**: Traders look for [multiple](../m/multiple.md) touches of these levels without price breaking through, which reinforces their strength.
- **Role [Reversal](../r/reversal.md)**: If a support level is broken, it can act as a new resistance level and vice versa.

#### **Trend Analysis**
[Trend analysis](../t/trend_analysis.md) involves identifying the general direction in which the [market](../m/market.md) is moving. This can be an [uptrend](../u/uptrend.md) (higher highs and higher lows), a [downtrend](../d/downtrend.md) (lower highs and lower lows), or a sideways [trend](../t/trend.md) ([range](../r/range.md)-bound movement).

- **Trendlines**: Drawing lines connecting key highs or lows helps in visual identification of the [trend](../t/trend.md).
- **Channels**: Parallel lines that encapsulate the price movement enhance the understanding of the [trend](../t/trend.md)'s strength and possible continuation or [reversal](../r/reversal.md).

#### **Price Patterns**
[Price patterns](../p/price_patterns.md) are specific formations created by price movements that can indicate potential future [price action](../p/price_action.md).

- **[Continuation Patterns](../c/continuation_patterns.md)**: Patterns like flags, pennants, and rectangles suggest that the current [trend](../t/trend.md) is likely to continue.
- **[Reversal Patterns](../r/reversal_patterns.md)**: Patterns such as head and shoulders, double tops, and double bottoms indicate that the current [trend](../t/trend.md) may reverse.

### Psychological Aspects
[Trading psychology](../t/trading_psychology.md) plays a significant role in [price action](../p/price_action.md) trading. Traders must develop a keen awareness of [market sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md) and crowd behavior, which can often be inferred from the [price action](../p/price_action.md) itself. 

- **Discipline**: Sticking to the trading plan and not being swayed by emotions is essential.
- **Patience**: Waiting for high-probability setups and not forcing trades is crucial for long-term success.

### Strategies in Price Action Trading

#### **Breakout Trading**
[Breakout trading](../b/breakout_trading.md) involves entering a [trade](../t/trade.md) when the price moves beyond a defined support or resistance level. The idea is that the price [will](../w/will.md) continue moving in that direction with increased [momentum](../m/momentum.md).

- **Entry Points**: Traders look for strong breakouts with high [volume](../v/volume.md) as confirmation of the move.
- **Stop Loss**: Placed just below the [breakout](../b/breakout.md) point in case of a [false breakout](../f/false_breakout.md).

#### **Pullback Trading**
[Pullback](../p/pullback.md) trading is about entering a [trade](../t/trade.md) after a temporary [reversal](../r/reversal.md) in the direction of the [trend](../t/trend.md). This strategy aims to [capitalize](../c/capitalize.md) on minor price corrections.

- **Entry Points**: Waiting for a [pullback](../p/pullback.md) to a support level in an [uptrend](../u/uptrend.md) or to a resistance level in a [downtrend](../d/downtrend.md) before entering.
- **Stop Loss**: Placed below the [pullback](../p/pullback.md) low in an [uptrend](../u/uptrend.md) or above the [pullback](../p/pullback.md) high in a [downtrend](../d/downtrend.md).

#### **Reversal Trading**
[Reversal](../r/reversal.md) trading entails identifying and trading potential [market](../m/market.md) reversals. This is a higher-[risk](../r/risk.md) strategy but can [offer](../o/offer.md) substantial rewards.

- **Entry Points**: Traders look for strong [reversal patterns](../r/reversal_patterns.md) like head and shoulders or double tops/bottoms.
- **Stop Loss**: Placed just beyond the high point of the [reversal](../r/reversal.md) pattern for bearish trades or the low point for bullish trades.
