# Price Action Analysis

## Introduction
Price action analysis is the study of price movement in the financial markets. Traders and analysts use price action to intuitively interpret and forecast market trends based on historical price movements, without relying on [technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md) or other external data. This approach is commonly employed in various markets including stocks, forex, commodities, and more.

## Fundamentals of Price Action
Price action refers to the movement of a security’s price plotted over time. Price action forms the basis for all [technical analysis](../t/technical_analysis.md) of a stock, commodity, or other asset chart. Many short-term traders rely on price action-based techniques, often with combined use of [technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md) and charts, to inform their trading decisions.

## Components of Price Action
Several key elements make up price action analysis, each of which provides valuable information about the market:

1. **[Candlestick Patterns](../c/candlestick_patterns.md)**: One of the most fundamental components, candlestick charts display the high, low, open, and close prices of a security. Key patterns include Doji, Hammer, Engulfing patterns, and more.

2. **[Support and Resistance](../s/support_and_resistance.md) Levels**: These are price levels at which a security tends to find support as it falls and resistance as it rises. Identifying these levels helps in making trading decisions.

3. **Trend Lines**: Drawing trend lines helps in identifying the direction of the market. They connect significant price points on a chart, showing overall trends and potential points of reversal.

4. **Volume**: Volume indicates the number of shares or contracts traded in a security. Patterns in volume can confirm or deny price movements, providing further [trading signals](../t/trading_signals.md).

## Tools and Strategies in Price Action Trading
Different tools and strategies are employed by price action traders to make informed trading decisions:

1. **[Chart Patterns](../c/chart_patterns.md)**: Patterns like Head and Shoulders, Double Top and Bottom, and Triangles are used to predict future price movements.

2. **Price Action Indicators**: While [price action trading](../p/price_action_trading.md) primarily avoids traditional indicators, some traders use them for confirmation, such as Moving Averages and the Relative Strength Index (RSI).

3. **Breakouts and Fakeouts**: Traders watch for breakouts from [support and resistance](../s/support_and_resistance.md) levels, and fakeouts (false breakouts) to identify potential trades.

4. **[Candlestick Patterns](../c/candlestick_patterns.md)**: Analysis of specific patterns like Bullish Engulfing, Bearish Harami, and Morning Star can provide signals for trading decisions.

## Benefits of Price Action Trading
1. **Simplicity**: [Price action trading](../p/price_action_trading.md) focuses on pure price movements, which can be simpler than using multiple [technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md).
2. **Versatility**: Applicable across different markets and timeframes.
3. **Reliability**: When mastered, [price action trading](../p/price_action_trading.md) can provide highly reliable [trading signals](../t/trading_signals.md).

## Limitations of Price Action Trading
1. **Subjectivity**: Interpretation can vary between traders.
2. **Experience-Dependent**: Requires substantial market knowledge and experience.
3. **Market Conditions**: Certain market conditions may render [price action signals](../p/price_action_signals.md) less effective.
