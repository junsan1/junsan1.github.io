# Price Value of a Basis Point (PVBP)

## Introduction
Price [Value](../v/value.md) of a [Basis](../b/basis.md) Point (PVBP), also known as Dollar [Value](../v/value.md) of a [Basis](../b/basis.md) Point (DV01), is a key metric in [bond](../b/bond.md) trading and [fixed income](../f/fixed_income.md) [portfolio management](../p/par.md). It measures the change in the price of a [bond](../b/bond.md) or a [bond](../b/bond.md) portfolio for a 1 [basis](../b/basis.md) point (0.01%) change in [yield](../y/yield.md). Understanding PVBP helps traders, portfolio managers, and [risk](../r/risk.md) managers to assess [interest rate risk](../i/interest_rate_risk.md) and the sensitivity of [bond](../b/bond.md) prices to changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates.

## Calculation of PVBP
PVBP can be calculated using the following formula:

\[ PVBP = \frac{\[Delta](../d/delta.md) P}{\[Delta](../d/delta.md) Y} \]

where:
- \( \[Delta](../d/delta.md) P \) is the change in the price of the [bond](../b/bond.md).
- \( \[Delta](../d/delta.md) Y \) is the change in the [yield](../y/yield.md), which is equal to 1 [basis](../b/basis.md) point or 0.0001.

Alternatively, PVBP can be approximated using the [bond](../b/bond.md)’s [duration](../d/duration.md):

\[ PVBP \approx \text{[Duration](../d/duration.md)} \times 0.0001 \times \text{[Bond](../b/bond.md) Price} \]

Here, [Duration](../d/duration.md) measures the [weighted average](../w/weighted_average.md) time until the [bond](../b/bond.md)’s cash flows are received and indicates the [bond](../b/bond.md)’s sensitivity to changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates.

## Importance in Bond Trading
PVBP is vital in [bond](../b/bond.md) trading for several reasons:

1. **[Interest Rate Risk Management](../i/interest_rate_risk_management.md)**: PVBP provides a standardized measure of [interest rate risk](../i/interest_rate_risk.md), allowing traders to compare the sensitivities of different bonds or portfolios.
2. **Hedging**: Traders use PVBP to construct [hedging strategies](../h/hedging_strategies.md) that neutralize [interest rate risk](../i/interest_rate_risk.md) by offsetting positions.
3. **Pricing and [Valuation](../v/valuation.md)**: PVBP helps in pricing [interest rate derivatives](../i/interest_rate_derivatives.md), such as swaps and [options](../o/options.md), by providing an estimate of how much the [bond](../b/bond.md)’s price [will](../w/will.md) change with small movements in [yield](../y/yield.md).

## Practical Example
Consider a [bond](../b/bond.md) with a [face value](../f/face_value.md) of $1000, a [coupon rate](../c/coupon_rate.md) of 5%, and a [yield to maturity](../y/yield_to_maturity.md) (YTM) of 4%. If the [bond](../b/bond.md)’s [duration](../d/duration.md) is 7 years and its current price is $1050, the PVBP can be calculated as follows:

\[ PVBP \approx 7 \times 0.0001 \times 1050 = 0.735 \]

Therefore, for a 1 [basis](../b/basis.md) point increase in [yield](../y/yield.md), the [bond](../b/bond.md)’s price would decrease by approximately $0.735.

## Applications in Portfolio Management
Portfolio managers use PVBP to:

1. **Assess Portfolio Sensitivity**: By calculating the PVBP for an entire [bond](../b/bond.md) portfolio, managers can assess its overall sensitivity to [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) changes.
2. **Rebalance Portfolios**: Managers may rebalance portfolios to maintain a desired level of [interest rate risk](../i/interest_rate_risk.md) exposure.
3. **Performance Measurement**: PVBP aids in measuring the performance of portfolio strategies by evaluating the impact of [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) changes.

## PVBP and Convexity
While PVBP provides a linear approximation of price changes for small [yield](../y/yield.md) movements, it does not account for [convexity](../c/convexity.md), the measure of curvature in the price-[yield](../y/yield.md) relationship. For larger [yield](../y/yield.md) changes, [convexity](../c/convexity.md) must be considered to improve accuracy.

\[ \Delta P = PVBP \times \Delta Y + \frac{1}{2} \times \text{[Convexity](../c/convexity.md)} \times \[Delta](../d/delta.md) Y^2 \]

Including [convexity](../c/convexity.md) ensures more accurate price estimates, especially for bonds with significant price-[yield](../y/yield.md) curvature.
