# Portfolio Duration

Portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) is a financial metric used to assess the sensitivity of a portfolio's [value](../v/value.md) to changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates. This metric is especially relevant in [fixed income](../f/fixed_income.md) [investing](../i/investing.md), where it helps investors understand and manage [interest rate risk](../i/interest_rate_risk.md). While [duration](../d/duration.md) is commonly associated with individual bonds, portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) extends this concept to diversified portfolios that may include various fixed-[income](../i/income.md) instruments such as bonds, debentures, and other [debt](../d/debt.md) securities.

## Understanding Portfolio Duration

### Definition

[Duration](../d/duration.md) measures the [weighted average](../w/weighted_average.md) time it takes to receive the cash flows (both [interest](../i/interest.md) and [principal](../p/principal.md) payments) from an investment. It is expressed in years. The concept of [duration](../d/duration.md) is pivotal because it provides a measure of the [price sensitivity](../p/price_sensitivity.md) of a [bond](../b/bond.md) or a [bond](../b/bond.md) portfolio to changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates. When applied at the portfolio level, it helps manage the overall [interest rate sensitivity](../i/interest_rate_sensitivity.md) of the portfolio.

There are different types of [duration](../d/duration.md):
1. **[Macaulay Duration](../m/macaulay_duration.md)**: This is the [weighted average](../w/weighted_average.md) time until the cash flows are received and is used primarily for academic purposes.
2. **[Modified Duration](../m/modified_duration.md)**: Derived from [Macaulay Duration](../m/macaulay_duration.md), this measure indicates the [percentage change](../p/percentage_change.md) in price for a 1% change in [yield](../y/yield.md).
3. **[Effective Duration](../e/effective_duration.md)**: Used for instruments with embedded [options](../o/options.md), this measure accounts for changes in cash flows due to changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates.

### Calculation of Portfolio Duration

The portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) is generally the [weighted average](../w/weighted_average.md) of the durations of the individual bonds in the portfolio, [weighted](../w/weighted.md) by their [market](../m/market.md) values. The formula is:

\[ \text{Portfolio [Duration](../d/duration.md)} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left( \frac{MV_i}{MV_p} \times D_i \right) \]

Where:
- \( \sum \) denotes the sum for all securities \( N \) in the portfolio.
- \( MV_i \) is the [market value](../m/market_value.md) of each individual [bond](../b/bond.md) \( i \).
- \( MV_p \) is the total [market value](../m/market_value.md) of the portfolio.
- \( D_i \) is the [duration](../d/duration.md) of each individual [bond](../b/bond.md) \( i \).

### Example

Assume a portfolio consisting of three bonds with the following characteristics:

- [Bond](../b/bond.md) A: [Market Value](../m/market_value.md) \( \text{\$100,000} \), [Duration](../d/duration.md) \( 5 \) years.
- [Bond](../b/bond.md) B: [Market Value](../m/market_value.md) \( \text{\$200,000} \), [Duration](../d/duration.md) \( 3 \) years.
- [Bond](../b/bond.md) C: [Market Value](../m/market_value.md) \( \text{\$300,000} \), [Duration](../d/duration.md) \( 7 \) years.

First, calculate the total [market value](../m/market_value.md) of the portfolio:

\[ MV_p = 100,000 + 200,000 + 300,000 = 600,000 \]

Next, compute the [weighted](../w/weighted.md) [duration](../d/duration.md) for each [bond](../b/bond.md):

- For [Bond](../b/bond.md) A: \( \frac{100,000}{600,000} \times 5 = 0.833 \)
- For [Bond](../b/bond.md) B: \( \frac{200,000}{600,000} \times 3 = 1.000 \)
- For [Bond](../b/bond.md) C: \( \frac{300,000}{600,000} \times 7 = 3.500 \)

Finally, sum these [weighted](../w/weighted.md) durations to get the portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md):

\[ \text{Portfolio [Duration](../d/duration.md)} = 0.833 + 1.000 + 3.500 = 5.333 \text{ years} \]

## Importance and Applications

### Risk Management

Portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) is a critical measure in [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) as it helps investors understand how changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates [will](../w/will.md) affect the overall [value](../v/value.md) of their [bond](../b/bond.md) [holdings](../h/holdings.md). A higher [duration](../d/duration.md) implies greater sensitivity to [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) changes, meaning the portfolio's [value](../v/value.md) [will](../w/will.md) fluctuate more with [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) movements.

### Asset Liability Matching

Financial institutions such as banks, pension funds, and [insurance](../i/insurance.md) companies use portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) to match the durations of their assets and liabilities. This ensures that they have sufficient funds to meet future [obligations](../o/obligation.md) and minimizes the [risk](../r/risk.md) of funding mismatches.

### Yield Curve Positioning

Investors can use [duration](../d/duration.md) to position their portfolios along the [yield curve](../y/yield_curve.md). By adjusting the [duration](../d/duration.md), an [investor](../i/investor.md) can take advantage of expectations about future [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) movements. For example, if rates are expected to rise, an [investor](../i/investor.md) might shorten the [duration](../d/duration.md) to reduce [price sensitivity](../p/price_sensitivity.md).

### Performance Measurement

Portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) is also a key [factor](../f/factor.md) in performance measurement and [attribution analysis](../a/attribution_analysis.md). By comparing the [duration](../d/duration.md) of the portfolio to the [duration](../d/duration.md) of a [benchmark](../b/benchmark.md) [index](../i/index.md), investors can assess how much of the performance is attributed to [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) movements versus other factors such as [credit](../c/credit.md) selection or sector allocation.

## Practical Considerations

### Limitations

While portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) is a useful tool, it has limitations. [Duration](../d/duration.md) assumes a [linear relationship](../l/linear_relationship.md) between price changes and [yield](../y/yield.md) changes, which may not [hold](../h/hold.md) true for large [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) movements. Additionally, [duration](../d/duration.md) does not account for changes in the shape of the [yield curve](../y/yield_curve.md) (non-parallel shifts).

### Duration Targeting

Investors often set a [duration](../d/duration.md) target for their portfolios based on their [risk](../r/risk.md) and [return](../r/return.md) objectives. Implementing a [duration](../d/duration.md) target involves selecting bonds and other fixed-[income](../i/income.md) securities whose durations collectively align with the desired portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md).

## Advanced Concepts

### Convexity

[Convexity](../c/convexity.md) is a measure of the sensitivity of [duration](../d/duration.md) to changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates. It accounts for the curvature in the relationship between [bond](../b/bond.md) prices and yields. High [convexity](../c/convexity.md) indicates that a [bond](../b/bond.md)'s [duration](../d/duration.md) [will](../w/will.md) change more as [interest](../i/interest.md) rates change. While [duration](../d/duration.md) provides a first-[order](../o/order.md) approximation of [interest rate risk](../i/interest_rate_risk.md), [convexity](../c/convexity.md) offers a second-[order](../o/order.md) adjustment, making the [risk](../r/risk.md) assessment more accurate.

### Immunization

Immunization is a strategy aimed at minimizing the impact of [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) changes on a portfolio's [value](../v/value.md). By matching the [duration](../d/duration.md) of assets and liabilities, or by managing the portfolio [duration](../d/duration.md) actively, investors can ensure that the portfolio is shielded from adverse [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) movements over a specified [investment horizon](../i/investment_horizon.md).
