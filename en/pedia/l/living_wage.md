# Living Wage

**Living wage** is the minimum [income](../i/income.md) necessary for a worker to meet their basic needs. This is different from the [minimum wage](../m/minimum_wage.md), which is set by law and may not necessarily cover the [cost of living](../c/cost_of_living.md). The living wage considers factors such as housing, food, healthcare, transportation, and other essential costs to ensure that workers and their families can live with dignity and participate fully in society. 

### Components of a Living Wage

1. **Housing Costs**
    - Rent or [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md)
    - Utilities (electricity, water, gas)
    - Maintenance and repairs

2. **Food**
   - Cost of a balanced diet
   - Consideration for dietary needs and regional price differences

3. **Healthcare**
   - Medical [insurance](../i/insurance.md)
   - [Out-of-pocket expenses](../o/out-of-pocket_expenses.md)
   - Regular [check](../c/check.md)-ups and emergency care

4. **Transportation**
   - Public transportation costs
   - Maintenance and fuel for personal vehicles
   - [Insurance](../i/insurance.md)

5. **Childcare and Education**
   - Fees for daycare or babysitters
   - School fees and materials
   - After-school programs

6. **Miscellaneous**
   - Clothing and footwear
   - Communication (phone, internet)
   - Personal care items

### Methodologies for Calculating Living Wage

1. **[Market](../m/market.md)-Based Approach**
   - Uses local prices to estimate the [cost of living](../c/cost_of_living.md)
   - Commonly used by organizations like MIT's Living Wage Calculator

    [MIT Living Wage Calculator](https://livingwage.mit.edu)

2. **[Budget](../b/budget.md) Standards Approach**
   - Involves creating a detailed [budget](../b/budget.md) for a typical household
   - Can be more accurate but is resource-intensive to maintain

3. **Relative [Poverty](../p/poverty.md) Line**
   - Uses a percentage of [median](../m/median.md) [income](../i/income.md) as a [benchmark](../b/benchmark.md)
   - Focuses on inequality rather than absolute costs

### Significance in Business and Economy

1. **Employee [Productivity](../p/productivity.md)**
   - Higher wages can lead to increased [productivity](../p/productivity.md) and job satisfaction
   - Low wages are often linked to higher [turnover](../t/turnover.md) and absenteeism

2. **Consumer Spending**
   - Workers earning a living wage have more [disposable income](../d/disposable_income.md)
   - This can stimulate local economies through increased spending

3. **Corporate Responsibility**
   - Firms that pay a living wage often have better public perceptions
   - Can be part of an ethical [business](../b/business.md) strategy

### Challenges and Criticisms

1. **Implementation Complexity**
   - Differing costs of living between regions make standardization difficult
   - Often requires continuous updates to reflect [inflation](../i/inflation.md) and changes in cost

2. **Economic [Trade](../t/trade.md)-offs**
   - Small businesses may struggle to pay a living wage without increasing prices
   - Could potentially lead to reduced hiring or layoffs in certain industries

3. **Policy and Enforcement**
   - Governments may face opposition from lobbyists and industries
   - Ensuring compliance can be resource-intensive

### Global Perspective

1. **United Kingdom**
   - The Living Wage Foundation advocates for higher wages than the statutory minimum

    [Living Wage Foundation](https://www.livingwage.org.uk/)

2. **United States**
   - Various states and cities have enacted higher local minimum wages to approximate a living wage
   - Federal [minimum wage](../m/minimum_wage.md) remains a contentious [issue](../i/issue.md)

3. **[European Union](../e/european_union_(eu).md)**
   - Countries like Germany have implemented policies to ensure fair wages across different regions
   - The EU supports coordinated efforts to reduce wage inequality

### Case Studies and Examples

1. **Costco**
    - Consistently pays above the legal [minimum wage](../m/minimum_wage.md)
    - Has reported higher employee satisfaction and lower [turnover](../t/turnover.md) rates

    [Costco Careers](https://www.costco.com/jobs.html)

2. **IKEA**
    - Committed to paying living wages across all their locations globally
    - Emphasizes the importance of fair wages in their corporate responsibility

    [IKEA Group Sustainability](https://www.ikea.com/gb/en/this-is-ikea/sustainable-everyday)

### Conclusion
  
The concept of the living wage is essential for creating a just and equitable society. It assures that all workers can meet their basic needs and contribute to their communities. While there are challenges in implementation and widespread adaptation, the benefits often outweigh the drawbacks, setting a foundation for long-term economic stability and social well-being. Whether from a policy perspective or a [business](../b/business.md) strategy, promoting living wages can lead to positive outcomes for workers, employers, and the broader [economy](../e/economy.md).
