# Overhead Supply

In the world of [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md), "overhead [supply](../s/supply.md)" is a term frequently used by technical analysts to describe a situation where a stock or [security](../s/security.md) faces resistance above its current [price level](../p/price_level.md). This resistance occurs due to substantial [supply](../s/supply.md) from sellers who are willing to part with their [shares](../s/shares.md) at a particular price point. This phenomenon is pivotal in understanding [market dynamics](../m/market_dynamics.md), particularly in the context of price movements and [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md).

## Understanding Overhead Supply

To grasp the concept of overhead [supply](../s/supply.md), it's essential to first understand how [market](../m/market.md) [supply](../s/supply.md) and [demand](../d/demand.md) impact stock prices. When the [demand](../d/demand.md) for a stock exceeds its [supply](../s/supply.md), prices tend to rise. Conversely, when the [supply](../s/supply.md) of a stock exceeds [demand](../d/demand.md), prices fall. Overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) defines a scenario where past trading activity creates a pool of potential sellers at higher price levels, which hampers the stock's ability to advance beyond those levels.

## Causes of Overhead Supply

Overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) generally forms as a result of historical trading patterns. Here are a few common scenarios that can lead to overhead [supply](../s/supply.md):

### 1. Prior Highs and Resistance Levels
When a stock reaches a previous high, investors who bought at or near those levels might be waiting to sell at breakeven once the stock approaches those prices again. This creates an abundance of sell orders and establishes resistance.

### 2. Long Positions from Higher Prices
Investors who held long positions in a stock bought at higher levels may be looking to minimize losses by selling their [shares](../s/shares.md) when the price rises again. The influx of sell orders can create significant resistance.

### 3. Psychological Price Points
Psychological factors also play a role. Round numbers, such as $100 or $50, tend to act as psychological resistance levels where investors may place their sell orders, contributing to overhead [supply](../s/supply.md).

### 4. Fundamental Changes
Any fundamental changes in a company's operations, [market](../m/market.md) conditions, or external factors that lead investors to reconsider their valuations may cause overhead [supply](../s/supply.md). For instance, if a company's outlook becomes less favorable, former buyers at higher prices may become sellers.

## How Overhead Supply Impacts Trading

The presence of overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) can significantly impact [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md) and [price action](../p/price_action.md):

### 1. Resistance Levels
[Stocks](../s/stock.md) encountering overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) often face resistance that slows or halts upward [momentum](../m/momentum.md). Traders use these resistance levels to make informed decisions about entering or exiting positions.

### 2. Breakout Patterns
Technical analysts look for [breakout](../b/breakout.md) patterns where a stock manages to surpass overhead [supply](../s/supply.md). A break above the resistance level often indicates strong buying [interest](../i/interest.md) and may signal further upwards movement.

### 3. Price Patterns
Overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) often contributes to the formation of specific [price patterns](../p/price_patterns.md), such as Head and Shoulders, Double Tops, or Rectangle patterns. Recognizing these patterns helps traders predict potential price movements.

### 4. Risk Management
Understanding overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) is crucial for [risk management](../r/risk_management.md). Traders may place [stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md) just below resistance levels to limit potential losses if the stock fails to break through the overhead [supply](../s/supply.md).

Tesla, Inc. has experienced significant [volatility](../v/volatility.md) and overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) at various points due to its high profile and speculative nature. When TSLA stock approaches previous highs, the overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) from bearish investors looking to sell can create noticeable resistance. [Tesla](https://www.tesla.com/)

### Amazon.com, Inc. (NASDAQ: AMZN)
Amazon.com has faced overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) during periods of rapid price appreciation. Investors who bought [shares](../s/shares.md) during previous peaks often sell when the stock revisits those levels, establishing resistance. [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/)

## Strategies for Dealing with Overhead Supply

### 1. Waiting for Confirmation
Rather than buying near resistance levels, traders may wait for confirmation of a [breakout](../b/breakout.md) above overhead [supply](../s/supply.md). This reduces the [risk](../r/risk.md) of entering a position prematurely.

### 2. Volume Analysis
Analyzing trading volumes can provide insights into how [robust](../r/robust.md) the overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) is. High trading volumes during a [breakout](../b/breakout.md) often indicate strong buying [interest](../i/interest.md) capable of overcoming overhead [supply](../s/supply.md).

### 3. Using Indicators
[Technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md) such as Moving Averages, [Relative Strength](../r/relative_strength.md) [Index](../i/index_instrument.md) (RSI), and Moving Average Convergence [Divergence](../d/divergence.md) (MACD) can help traders gauge the strength of resistance and potential for a [breakout](../b/breakout.md).

### 4. Diversifying Positions
To mitigate the [risk](../r/risk.md) associated with overhead [supply](../s/supply.md), traders may diversify their positions across different assets or sectors.

## Conclusion

Overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) is a critical concept in [technical analysis](../t/technical_analysis.md) that sheds light on price resistance and [market](../m/market.md) behavior. By understanding its causes and impacts, traders can develop more effective strategies for navigating the [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md). Recognizing overhead [supply](../s/supply.md) patterns, utilizing indicators, and employing [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) techniques are essential components of successful trading in the face of this phenomenon.
