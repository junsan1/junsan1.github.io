# Option Pool

## Introduction
An option pool, also known as an [equity](../e/equity.md) pool or employee option pool, is a set number of company [shares](../s/shares.md) reserved for future issuance to employees, board members, advisors, and other stakeholders as part of an option plan. Option pools are essential in startups and growing companies to attract and retain top talent by [offering](../o/offering.md) [equity](../e/equity.md) incentives. This discussion [will](../w/will.md) cover the mechanics, importance, and strategic considerations of creating and managing an option pool.

## Mechanics of an Option Pool

### Creation of an Option Pool
Creating an option pool typically involves amending the company's charter to authorize a specific number of [shares](../s/shares.md) set aside for the pool. This requires approval from the company's board of directors and potentially its shareholders.

### Size of the Option Pool
The size of the option pool can vary, but a common [range](../r/range.md) is 10-20% of the company's outstanding [shares](../s/shares.md). The size usually depends on the company's stage of development and hiring plans. For instance, earlier-stage startups might set aside a larger percentage to attract initial key hires.

### Issuing Options
[Options](../o/options.md) are usually granted with a [vesting](../v/vesting.md) schedule, commonly over a period of 4 years with a 1-year cliff. This means the employee earns the right to a portion of the [options](../o/options.md) over time, encouraging longer-term commitment to the company.

### Exercise Price
The [exercise price](../e/excersise_price.md), or [strike price](../s/strike_price.md), is the cost at which an employee can purchase the [shares](../s/shares.md). It's usually set at the fair [market value](../m/market_value.md) of the [shares](../s/shares.md) at the time of the [grant](../g/grant.md).

### Vesting and Exercising
Employees need to stay with the company for a certain period ([vesting](../v/vesting.md) period) to earn the [options](../o/options.md). Once vested, employees can [exercise](../e/exercise.md) these [options](../o/options.md), meaning they buy the [shares](../s/shares.md) at the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md). Upon exercising, they become shareholders of the company.

## Importance of an Option Pool

### Attracting Talent
Startups often cannot compete with established companies on salaries, so they use [equity compensation](../e/equity_compensation.md) to attract high-caliber talent. Stock [options](../o/options.md) can provide significant [upside potential](../u/upside_potential_in_trading.md) if the company succeeds.

### Aligning Interests
Stock [options](../o/options.md) align the interests of employees with those of the company's shareholders. Employees' rewards become tied to the company's success, driving them to work towards increasing the company's [value](../v/value.md).

### Retaining Employees
The [vesting](../v/vesting.md) schedule ensures that employees have a financial incentive to stay with the company for a longer period. This helps reduce [turnover](../t/turnover.md) and retain valuable talent within the company.

## Strategic Considerations

### Dilution
When setting up an option pool, it's critical to consider [dilution](../d/dilution.md). Creating an option pool dilutes the ownership of existing shareholders because it increases the total number of outstanding [shares](../s/shares.md). It's important to communicate the benefits of the option pool to current shareholders to [gain](../g/gain.md) their approval.

### Replenishing the Pool
As [options](../o/options.md) are granted and exercised, the option pool depletes. Companies may need to replenish the pool periodically, which might necessitate further approval from the board and shareholders.

### Market Competitiveness
The size and structure of the option pool should be competitive within the [industry](../i/industry.md). Companies should analyze peer [equity](../e/equity.md) offerings to ensure they provide attractive and [market](../m/market.md)-consistent option plans.

### Legal and Tax Considerations
Establishing and managing an option pool involves legal and tax considerations that vary by jurisdiction. Companies need to navigate these complexities with the help of legal and financial advisors to ensure compliance and optimize tax outcomes for the company and its employees.

### Communicating with Employees
[Transparency](../t/transparency.md) with employees regarding the terms, potential benefits, and risks of stock [options](../o/options.md) is crucial. Employees should fully understand how [options](../o/options.md) fit into their overall compensation and the potential for financial [gain](../g/gain.md).

### Timing Issues
Deciding when to [grant](../g/grant.md) [options](../o/options.md) can also be a strategic decision. Companies may choose to [grant](../g/grant.md) [options](../o/options.md) when their [valuation](../v/valuation.md) is lower to benefit from a low [exercise price](../e/excersise_price.md) for employees. ## Conclusion
An option pool is a vital tool for startups and growing companies to attract, retain, and incentivize key employees by [offering](../o/offering.md) them a stake in the company's future success. While it involves considerations of [dilution](../d/dilution.md), [market](../m/market.md) competitiveness, legal, and fiscal implications, the strategic use of an option pool can be instrumental in building a motivated and committed workforce, ultimately driving the company towards its long-term goals.
