# Open Offer

An [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) is a [financing](../f/financing.md) mechanism in the [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md) where a company offers its existing shareholders the chance to purchase additional [shares](../s/shares.md) at a predetermined price. This method is frequently utilized as a means of raising new [equity](../e/equity.md) [capital](../c/capital.md). As such, an [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) is non-dilutive in the sense that only existing shareholders have the right to purchase more [shares](../s/shares.md), preserving their proportional ownership in the company.

## Key Characteristics of an Open Offer

### 1. **Pre-determined Price**
An [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) is set at a pre-determined price, typically at a [discount](../d/discount.md) to the current [market price](../m/market_price.md). This [discount](../d/discount.md) acts as an incentive for existing shareholders to partake in the [offer](../o/offer.md).

### 2. **Non-transferable Rights**
The rights contained in an [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) are non-transferable. This is in contrast to a rights [issue](../i/issue.md), where shareholders may have the option to sell their rights to another party.

### 3. **Period of Subscription**
There is a limited period during which shareholders can subscribe to an [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md). Failing to participate within this time frame results in the loss of the opportunity to buy the additional [shares](../s/shares.md) at the discounted rate.

### 4. **Pro-rata Allocation**
Existing shareholders are given the opportunity to purchase [shares](../s/shares.md) on a pro-rata [basis](../b/basis.md). This means the number of additional [shares](../s/shares.md) they can buy is proportional to their current [holdings](../h/holdings.md).

## Advantages of an Open Offer

### 1. **Preservation of Ownership**
Shareholders who participate in the [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) can maintain their proportional ownership by buying more [shares](../s/shares.md). This aspect is especially appealing to those looking to avoid [dilution](../d/dilution.md) of their [holdings](../h/holdings.md).

### 2. **Fundraising**
For the issuing company, [open](../o/open.md) offers are an effective way to raise [capital](../c/capital.md) without broadening the ownership base or incurring high transactional costs. This method often incurs lower administrative costs compared to public offerings.

### 3. **Market Perception**
Opting for an [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) can have a positive impact on the [market](../m/market.md)’s perception of the company. It signals that the company is providing existing shareholders with an exclusive opportunity, indirectly indicating [trust](../t/trust.md) in long-term growth.

## Disadvantages of an Open Offer

### 1. **Limited Reach**
Since only existing shareholders are eligible to participate, the potential to raise [capital](../c/capital.md) is inherently limited. This can be a downside for companies needing to raise substantial amounts of [money](../m/money.md) quickly.

### 2. **Participation Uncertainty**
There is always [uncertainty](../u/uncertainty_in_trading.md) regarding how many shareholders [will](../w/will.md) take up the [offer](../o/offer.md). Low participation levels can lead to lower-than-desired [capital](../c/capital.md) inflow.

### 3. **Execution Complexity**
While simpler than some other funding mechanisms, executing an [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) still requires compliance with legal and regulatory frameworks, potentially involving significant administrative effort.

## Use Cases

### 1. **Debt Refinancing**
Companies may use [open](../o/open.md) offers to raise [capital](../c/capital.md) for repaying or refinancing existing debts. The proceeds from the [offer](../o/offer.md) can be utilized to improve the company's [balance sheet](../b/balance_sheet.md) by reducing its [leverage](../l/leverage.md).

### 2. **Growth Funding**
Firms looking to [fund](../f/fund.md) [expansion](../e/expansion.md), R&D, or other growth initiatives might lean towards an [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md). Providing existing shareholders with this opportunity shows the company’s confidence in its growth strategy.

### 3. **Operational Liquidity**
An [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) can also serve as a tool to bolster operational [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md), giving companies a buffer to manage day-to-day expenses or unanticipated financial commitments.

## Regulatory Considerations

[Open](../o/open.md) offers are subject to stringent regulatory mandates that vary by jurisdiction. Compliance typically involves adhering to securities laws, submitting detailed documentation, and receiving approvals from financial regulatory bodies.

### Case Study: TUI AG Open Offer

TUI AG, a Germany-based multinational travel and tourism company, announced an [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) in 2020 as part of its financial strategy to navigate through the economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. The [offer](../o/offer.md) aimed to raise significant [capital](../c/capital.md) to enhance [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) and strengthen its [balance sheet](../b/balance_sheet.md) during the crisis. Shareholders were offered the right to subscribe to new [shares](../s/shares.md) at a [discount](../d/discount.md), ensuring that those opting in could maintain their ownership levels. For more details, you can [check](../c/check.md) their official site: [TUI Group](https://www.tuigroup.com).

## Conclusion

An [open](../o/open.md) [offer](../o/offer.md) presents a viable mechanism for companies to raise [capital](../c/capital.md) while providing existing shareholders with the opportunity to maintain their ownership stakes. While it offers distinct benefits like preservation of ownership and lower costs, its limitations such as restricted reach and uncertain participation levels must be carefully considered. When executed thoughtfully, [open](../o/open.md) offers can be a strategic tool for managing financial stability and supporting growth initiatives.

By understanding the nuances and strategic implications of [open](../o/open.md) offers, companies can better navigate the complexities of [equity financing](../e/equity_financing.md), ensuring optimal outcomes for both the company and its shareholders.
