# Out-of-the-Money Options

#### Introduction
In the world of financial [derivatives](../d/derivatives.md), [options](../o/options.md) are a pivotal tool for investors. They provide the right, but not the obligation, to buy (in the case of a [call option](../c/call_option.md)) or sell (in the case of a [put option](../p/put.md)) an [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at a specified price on or before a particular date. Among these [options](../o/options.md), an important concept is that of "Out-of-the-[Money](../m/money.md)" (OTM) [options](../o/options.md). This term describes a specific situation where the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) of an option is not advantageous compared to the current [market price](../m/market_price.md) of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md).

#### Understanding Out-of-the-Money Options
An option is considered Out-of-the-[Money](../m/money.md) (OTM) when, for call [options](../o/options.md), the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) is higher than the current [market price](../m/market_price.md) of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md), and for [put options](../p/put_options.md), the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) is lower than the current [market price](../m/market_price.md). These [options](../o/options.md), at the moment, do not [hold](../h/hold.md) [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md) and would expire worthless if the [market price](../m/market_price.md) remained [unchanged](../u/unchanged.md). OTM [options](../o/options.md) are primarily valued for their [time value](../t/time_value.md) and [volatility](../v/volatility.md) rather than [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md).

#### Example of Call and Put Options
1. **[Call Option](../c/call_option.md) Example**: Assume the current price of XYZ stock is $50. A [call option](../c/call_option.md) with a [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) of $60 is considered OTM because the stock would need to rise above $60 for the option to be exercised profitably.
2. **[Put Option](../p/put.md) Example**: If the current price of XYZ stock is $50, a [put option](../p/put.md) with a [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) of $40 is OTM because the stock would need to drop below $40 for the option to [gain](../g/gain.md) [value](../v/value.md).

#### Characteristics of Out-of-the-Money Options
- **[Intrinsic Value](../i/intrinsic_value.md)**: OTM [options](../o/options.md) have an [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md) of zero, as exercising them would not be beneficial based on current [market](../m/market.md) conditions.
- **[Time Value](../t/time_value.md)**: The [value](../v/value.md) of an OTM option is entirely composed of [time value](../t/time_value.md). This reflects the potential for the option to become profitable before expiration due to changes in the [market price](../m/market_price.md).
- **[Risk](../r/risk.md) and [Return](../r/return.md) Profile**: OTM [options](../o/options.md) provide a high-[risk](../r/risk.md), high-reward opportunity. They are less expensive than In-the-[Money](../m/money.md) (ITM) [options](../o/options.md), potentially [offering](../o/offering.md) significant [leverage](../l/leverage.md) should the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s price move significantly in the right direction.

#### Factors Influencing OTM Option Prices
The price of OTM [options](../o/options.md) is influenced by several factors:
- **Time to Expiration**: The more time remaining until the option expires, the higher the [time value](../t/time_value.md), as there's more opportunity for the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)â€™s price to move favorably.
- **[Volatility](../v/volatility.md)**: Increased [volatility](../v/volatility.md) in the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) can lead to higher option prices, as it increases the likelihood of the option moving ITM before expiration.
- **[Interest](../i/interest.md) Rates**: Changes in [interest](../i/interest.md) rates can impact option prices, especially for longer-term [options](../o/options.md).
- **[Underlying Asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) Price**: The closer the current [market price](../m/market_price.md) of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) is to the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md), the higher the option price, reflecting an increased probability of moving ITM.

#### Strategies Involving OTM Options
Investors and traders employ various strategies using OTM [options](../o/options.md), primarily focusing on their potential for significant returns:

1. **Long OTM Call/Put**: Buying OTM calls or puts is a speculative strategy aiming to benefit from large price movements. The initial cost is lower, but the probability of expiring worthless is higher.

2. **Spread Strategies**: Involving buying and selling [options](../o/options.md) with different strike prices or expiration dates to [hedge](../h/hedge.md) or speculate. Common spread strategies using OTM [options](../o/options.md) include vertical [spreads](../s/spreads.md), butterfly [spreads](../s/spreads.md), and condors.

3. **Strangles and Straddles**: These strategies involve purchasing both call and [put options](../p/put_options.md) with the same expiration but different (strangles) or the same (straddles) strike prices. This is particularly useful in high [volatility](../v/volatility.md) situations where significant price movement is expected, but the direction is uncertain.

#### Pros and Cons of Trading OTM Options
- **Pros**:
  - Lower Costs: OTM [options](../o/options.md) are generally cheaper than ITM [options](../o/options.md), allowing traders to obtain larger positions with less [capital](../c/capital.md).
  - [Leverage](../l/leverage.md): They [offer](../o/offer.md) high [leverage](../l/leverage.md), potentially amplifying returns if the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) moves significantly.
  - Potential for High Returns: The asymmetry of [risk](../r/risk.md) (limited loss potential, high reward potential) appeals to traders seeking substantial gains.

- **Cons**:
  - High [Risk](../r/risk.md): A high likelihood of expiring worthless, leading to a total loss of the [premium](../p/premium.md) paid.
  - Sensitivity to [Market](../m/market.md) Factors: High sensitivity to changes in [volatility](../v/volatility.md), [interest](../i/interest.md) rates, and [time decay](../t/time_decay.md) can adversely affect [value](../v/value.md).
  - Less Predictable: The payoff depends on considerable, unpredictable movements in the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) price.
