# Years Certain Annuity

A "Years Certain Annuity" is a financial product that guarantees payments to the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md) for a specified number of years, known as the "term" or "period certain." Unlike lifetime annuities that provide [income](../i/income.md) for the [duration](../d/duration.md) of the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md)'s life, a years certain annuity pays out for a set period, regardless of whether the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md) lives or dies. If the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md) passes away before the term ends, the remaining payments [will](../w/will.md) be made to a designated [beneficiary](../b/beneficiary.md) or the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md)'s [estate](../e/estate.md).

## Key Features of a Years Certain Annuity

1. **Fixed Term**: Payments are made for a fixed number of years, often ranging from 5 to 30 years. The term is determined at the inception of the annuity contract.
2. **Consistent Payments**: The payments can be structured as either fixed or variable, depending on the annuity contract. Fixed payments provide predictable, consistent [income](../i/income.md) while variable payments can fluctuate based on the performance of the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) assets.
3. **[Death Benefit](../d/death_benefit.md)**: If the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md) dies before the term ends, the remaining payments are made to a [beneficiary](../b/beneficiary.md) or the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md)’s [estate](../e/estate.md). This feature provides a safeguard to ensure the annuity’s [value](../v/value.md) is fully realized.
4. **Tax Treatment**: The tax treatment of a years certain annuity varies by jurisdiction but usually involves [ordinary income](../o/ordinary_income.md) tax on the [earnings](../e/earnings.md) portion of each [payment](../p/payment.md). Contributions made with pre-tax dollars typically result in the entire [payment](../p/payment.md) being subject to [income tax](../i/income_tax.md).
5. **Low [Market Risk](../m/market_risk.md)**: Because payments are guaranteed for a specified period, years certain annuities carry lower [market risk](../m/market_risk.md) compared to other investment [options](../o/options.md) affected by [market](../m/market.md) [volatility](../v/volatility.md).

## Types of Years Certain Annuities

1. **Immediate Years Certain Annuities**: These annuities start making payments almost immediately after an initial lump-sum investment. They are ideal for individuals already in retirement who need a safe, predictable source of [income](../i/income.md).
2. **Deferred Years Certain Annuities**: Payments from these annuities begin at a future date, which allows the invested [principal](../p/principal.md) to grow during the deferral period. These are suitable for individuals looking to secure future [income](../i/income.md).

## Pros and Cons

### Pros:

- **Predictable [Income](../i/income.md)**: Provides a reliable source of [income](../i/income.md) for a specified period, which can be crucial for [financial planning](../f/financial_planning.md).
- **[Beneficiary](../b/beneficiary.md) Protection**: Ensures that if the [annuitant](../a/annuitant.md) dies before the term ends, beneficiaries still receive the remaining payments.
- **Low [Risk](../r/risk.md)**: Offers lower [market risk](../m/market_risk.md) than many other types of investments.
- **Simplicity**: Easier to understand and implement compared to more complex financial products like variable or indexed annuities.

### Cons:

- **Lack of Lifetime [Income](../i/income.md)**: Does not provide [income](../i/income.md) for life, so there's a [risk](../r/risk.md) of outliving the annuity payments if the term does not extend until death.
- **[Inflation](../i/inflation.md) [Risk](../r/risk.md)**: Fixed payments may lose [purchasing power](../p/purchasing_power.md) over time due to [inflation](../i/inflation.md).
- **Lower Returns**: Generally offers lower returns compared to [equity](../e/equity.md) investments due to the low-[risk](../r/risk.md) nature of the annuity.
- **Limited [Liquidity](../l/liquidity.md)**: Like most annuities, they typically have penalties for early [withdrawal](../w/withdrawal.md), reducing [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md).

## Case Study: Using Years Certain Annuity in Financial Planning

Imagine a retiree named John who is 65 years old. John has a moderate [risk tolerance](../r/risk_tolerance.md) and is looking for ways to supplement his existing [income](../i/income.md) from [Social Security](../s/social_security.md) and pensions. He has a lump sum of $300,000 to invest and wants to ensure steady [income](../i/income.md) for a specific period while preserving some [capital](../c/capital.md) for his heirs.

John decides to purchase a 20-year certain annuity with his $300,000. Based on the annuity's terms, he [will](../w/will.md) receive $18,000 annually for the next 20 years, providing him with a predictable [income](../i/income.md) stream. If John passes away after 10 years, the remaining $18,000 annual payments [will](../w/will.md) go to his designated [beneficiary](../b/beneficiary.md), ensuring that his investment continues to benefit his family.

Additionally, John appreciates the low [market risk](../m/market_risk.md) associated with this annuity, as he is not comfortable with the [volatility](../v/volatility.md) of the [stock market](../s/stock_market.md). However, John is also aware that this annuity [will](../w/will.md) not provide [income](../i/income.md) beyond the 20-year period, so he plans accordingly to ensure other [income](../i/income.md) sources are available if he lives beyond the guaranteed payments.

## Regulatory and Market Considerations

Years certain annuities are regulated financial products and must comply with both federal and state [insurance](../i/insurance.md) regulations. In the United States, these products are typically overseen by state [insurance](../i/insurance.md) commissioners who ensure that annuity providers maintain the [solvency](../s/solvency.md) and adhere to ethical sales practices. Regulatory bodies such as the National Association of [Insurance](../i/insurance.md) Commissioners (NAIC) play an essential role in standardizing practices and protecting consumers.

[Market](../m/market.md) conditions also affect annuity rates. [Interest](../i/interest.md) rates, for example, are a significant [factor](../f/factor.md) in determining the [payout](../p/payout.md) amounts of years certain annuities. When [interest](../i/interest.md) rates are high, annuity payments tend to be more generous; conversely, lower [interest](../i/interest.md) rates generally result in reduced payments. Therefore, prospective annuitants often seek expert financial advice to optimize the timing of their annuity purchase.
