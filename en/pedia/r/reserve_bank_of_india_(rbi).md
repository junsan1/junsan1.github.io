# Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

The Reserve [Bank](../b/bank.md) of India (RBI) is the central banking institution of India, which controls the issuance and [supply](../s/supply.md) of the Indian [currency](../c/currency.md), the rupee. Established on April 1, 1935, during British rule under the provisions of the Reserve [Bank](../b/bank.md) of India Act, 1934, it was nationalized post-independence in 1949. The main objectives of the RBI are to oversee [monetary policy](../m/monetary_policy.md), regulate and supervise the [financial system](../f/financial_system.md), manage [foreign exchange](../f/foreign_exchange.md), and ensure the country’s monetary stability. Headquartered in Mumbai, it has a significant impact on the Indian [economy](../e/economy.md).

## Historical Background

### Establishment and Evolution
Initially headquartered in Kolkata before permanently moving to Mumbai, the RBI was created based on the recommendations of the 1926 Royal [Commission](../c/commission.md) on Indian [Currency](../c/currency.md) and [Finance](../f/finance.md), also known as the Hilton-Young [Commission](../c/commission.md). Its primary function at the time was to manage the nation’s [currency](../c/currency.md) and [credit](../c/credit.md) system and stabilize internal and external [value](../v/value.md).

## Primary Functions of the RBI

### Monetary Authority
As the monetary authority of India, the RBI formulates, implements, and monitors the country's [monetary policy](../m/monetary_policy.md) with the aim of maintaining price stability while keeping in mind the objective of growth.

### Issuer of Currency
The RBI holds the sole authority to [issue](../i/issue.md) banknotes in India. It ensures the adequate [supply](../s/supply.md) of [currency](../c/currency.md) in the nation and oversees the removal and destruction of damaged or unfit notes.

### Regulator of Financial System
The Reserve [Bank](../b/bank.md) regulates and supervises the [financial system](../f/financial_system.md) by laying down detailed regulations covering banking operations. It aims to maintain public confidence in the system, protect depositors' interests, and provide cost-effective banking services to the public.

### Manager of Foreign Exchange
The RBI manages the [Foreign Exchange](../f/foreign_exchange.md) Management Act, 1999, which aims to facilitate external [trade](../t/trade.md) and payments and promote the orderly development and maintenance of the [foreign exchange](../f/foreign_exchange.md) [market](../m/market.md) in India.

### Developmental Role
In addition to its regulatory role, the RBI also plays a prominent developmental role in providing a variety of promotional functions to support national objectives.

### Banker to the Government
The central [bank](../b/bank.md) acts as the banker to both the central and state governments. This role involves handling governments' banking transactions, such as [receipts](../r/receipt.md) and payments.

### Banker to Banks
The RBI also acts as the banker to all scheduled commercial banks, whereby it maintains banking accounts of the banks and enables redressal of [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) mismatches through short-term [credit](../c/credit.md) facilities.

## Organizational Structure

### Central Board of Directors
The general superintendence and direction of the RBI's affairs are entrusted to its Central Board of Directors, which consists of the Governor, Deputy Governors, and other directors appointed by the Government of India.

### Regional Offices
Besides the central office located in Mumbai, the RBI has several regional offices throughout the country to efficiently manage its operations.

## Monetary Policy and Inflation Targeting

### Objectives
The primary objective of RBI's [monetary policy](../m/monetary_policy.md) is to maintain price stability while ensuring adequate flow of [credit](../c/credit.md) to productive sectors. Post 2016, the RBI adopted an [inflation](../i/inflation.md) targeting framework, establishing an [inflation](../i/inflation.md) target of 4% with a [variability](../v/variability.md) band of +/- 2%.

### Tools
- **Repo Rate:** The rate at which the RBI lends short-term funds to commercial banks. 
- **Reverse Repo Rate:** The rate at which the RBI borrows [money](../m/money.md) from commercial banks.
- **Cash [Reserve Ratio](../r/reserve_ratio.md) (CRR):** The percentage of a [bank](../b/bank.md)'s total deposits that must be held in reserve.
- **Statutory [Liquidity Ratio](../l/liquidity_ratio.md) (SLR):** A percentage of net [demand](../d/demand.md) and time liabilities that banks must maintain as [liquid](../l/liquid.md) assets.

## Supervision and Regulation

### Banking Supervision
The RBI supervises the commercial banks, development [finance](../f/finance.md) institutions, and other financial entities. It establishes prudential norms to ensure the financial stability and soundness of these entities.

### Financial Stability and Development Council (FSDC)
The FSDC, chaired by the [Finance](../f/finance.md) Minister, has been set up to strengthen and institutionalize the mechanism for maintaining financial stability and development. The RBI plays a crucial role in this council.

## Payment and Settlement Systems

### National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI)
The RBI has played a pivotal role in the establishment of the NPCI to consolidate and integrate [multiple](../m/multiple.md) systems with varying service levels into a nationwide uniform and standard [business](../b/business.md) process for all retail [payment](../p/payment.md) systems.

### Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS)
RTGS is a continuous (real-time) settlement system where [fund](../f/fund.md) transfers are settled individually on an [order](../o/order.md)-by-[order](../o/order.md) [basis](../b/basis.md) without [netting](../n/netting.md).

### National Electronic Funds Transfer (NEFT)
NEFT is an electronic funds transfer system that operates on a Deferred [Net Settlement](../n/net_settlement.md) (DNS) [basis](../b/basis.md) which settles transactions in batches.

## Foreign Exchange Management

### Exchange Rate Stability
The RBI manages the country's [foreign exchange reserves](../f/foreign_exchange_reserves.md) and intervenes in the [foreign exchange](../f/foreign_exchange.md) [market](../m/market.md) to stabilize the rupee's [value](../v/value.md) against other currencies.

### External Sector Policy
The central [bank](../b/bank.md) formulates policies related to the external sector to foster an environment conducive to boosting international [trade](../t/trade.md) and investment.

## Developmental Role

### Financial Inclusion
The RBI promotes [financial inclusion](../f/financial_inclusion.md) by expanding access to banking services and products to all sections of society. Various schemes and initiatives are aimed at bringing the [unbanked](../u/unbanked.md) population into the formal [financial sector](../f/financial_sector.md)'s fold.

### Agriculture and Rural Development
It has specific measures focusing on [credit](../c/credit.md) allocation to agriculture and rural development, providing [refinance](../r/refinance.md) facilities to lend support to these sectors.

### Research and Information Dissemination
The RBI also undertakes and supports significant economic research and disseminates data and relevant information to stakeholders and the general public to aid decision-making and policy formulation.

## Conclusion

The Reserve [Bank](../b/bank.md) of India, through its broad array of functions, plays a crucial role in the country’s economic stability and growth. By effectively regulating the [financial system](../f/financial_system.md), implementing [monetary policy](../m/monetary_policy.md), issuing [currency](../c/currency.md), and managing [foreign exchange](../f/foreign_exchange.md), the RBI ensures that India's financial [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md) remains [robust](../r/robust.md) and conducive to sustainable economic development.
