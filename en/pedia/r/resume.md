# Resume

## Definition
In trading and [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md), "resume" typically refers to the resumption or continuation of trading activity, often after a pause or halt. It can apply to individual securities, entire markets, or specific trading sessions.

## Key Aspects

### 1. Trading Halts
- Resumption of trading after a temporary suspension
- Can occur due to various reasons such as news announcements, technical issues, or extreme [volatility](../v/volatility.md)

### 2. Market Openings
- Resumption of trading at the start of a new session
- Includes daily openings and reopenings after holidays

### 3. Continuation of Trends
- Resumption of a previous price [trend](../t/trend.md) after a period of [consolidation](../c/consolidation.md) or interruption
- Important concept in [technical analysis](../t/technical_analysis.md)

## Applications in Trading

### 1. Stock-Specific Resumes
- Individual [stocks](../s/stock.md) may resume trading after being halted
- Often accompanied by significant price movements or increased [volatility](../v/volatility.md)

### 2. Market-Wide Resumes
- Entire exchanges or markets may resume operations after closures
- Can impact overall [market sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md) and [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md)

### 3. Trading Session Resumes
- Continuation of trading after scheduled breaks (e.g., lunch breaks in some Asian markets)
- Can be associated with changes in trading [volume](../v/volume.md) and [price action](../p/price_action.md)

### 4. Trend Analysis
- Traders look for [trend](../t/trend.md) resumptions after periods of [consolidation](../c/consolidation.md) or [reversal](../r/reversal.md)
- Used in various technical [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md)

## Importance in Trading Decisions

1. **[Volatility](../v/volatility.md) Management**
   - Trading resumes often coincide with increased [volatility](../v/volatility.md)
   - Requires adjusted [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) strategies

2. **[Information Processing](../i/information_processing_in_trading.md)**
   - Resumes after news-related halts allow time for [market](../m/market.md) participants to digest information
   - Can lead to more informed trading decisions

3. **[Liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) Considerations**
   - Trading volumes may be abnormal immediately following a resume
   - Impacts [execution](../e/execution.md) strategies and potential [slippage](../s/slippage.md)

4. **[Algorithmic Trading](../a/accountability.md) Adjustments**
   - Automated systems need to account for trading resumes in their logic
   - May require specific programming to [handle](../h/handle.md) these events

## Related Concepts

1. **Circuit Breakers**
   - Mechanisms that halt trading to prevent excessive [volatility](../v/volatility.md)
   - Trading resumes after predetermined conditions are met

2. **[Gap Analysis](../g/gap_analysis.md)**
   - Study of price differences between close and resume of trading
   - Important for overnight positions and multi-day strategies

3. **[Opening Range](../o/opening_range.md)**
   - The price [range](../r/range.md) established in the initial minutes after trading resumes
   - Used by some traders to set the tone for the [trading session](../t/trading_session.md)

## Best Practices for Traders

1. Be aware of scheduled [market](../m/market.md) openings and potential unscheduled halts
2. [Exercise](../e/exercise.md) caution when trading immediately after a resume, due to potential [volatility](../v/volatility.md)
3. Stay informed about the reasons behind any trading halts or unusual resumes
4. Adjust [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md) to account for abnormal conditions following a resume

## Limitations and Risks

- Initial prices after a resume may be volatile and not reflective of true [market sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md)
- [Liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) may be limited immediately following a resume
- News or events causing a halt may continue to impact prices after trading resumes
