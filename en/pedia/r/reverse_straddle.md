# Reverse Straddle

A reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) is a sophisticated [options](../o/options.md) [trading strategy](../t/trading_strategy.md) that involves selling or writing a [straddle](../s/straddle.md) position, as opposed to buying one. In simpler terms, a reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) is executed when an [investor](../i/investor.md) believes that the price of an [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) [will](../w/will.md) remain stable or within a specific [range](../r/range.md) over a certain period. This strategy can be quite [lucrative](../l/lucrative.md) if the [market](../m/market.md) behaves as anticipated. However, it's essential to understand the mechanics, risks, and potential rewards associated with reverse straddles thoroughly.

## How Reverse Straddle Works

A [straddle](../s/straddle.md) involves either buying or selling both a [call option](../c/call_option.md) and a [put option](../p/put.md) with the same [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) and [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md) on the same [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md). In a reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md), an [investor](../i/investor.md) sells a [call option](../c/call_option.md) and a [put option](../p/put.md) with identical strike prices and expiration dates. Essentially, you are betting against [volatility](../v/volatility.md); hence, this strategy is also known as a "[short straddle](../s/short_straddle.md)" or "sold [straddle](../s/straddle.md)."

### Breakdown of Reverse Straddle:

1. **Sell a [Call Option](../c/call_option.md):** The [investor](../i/investor.md) sells a [call option](../c/call_option.md) at a particular [strike price](../s/strike_price.md).
2. **Sell a [Put Option](../p/put.md):** Simultaneously, the [investor](../i/investor.md) sells a [put option](../p/put.md) at the same [strike price](../s/strike_price.md).

By selling these [options](../o/options.md), the [investor](../i/investor.md) collects the premiums from both transactions. The total [premium](../p/premium.md) received represents the maximum [profit](../p/profit.md) potential of the reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md).

## Profit and Loss Scenario

### Maximum Profit

The maximum [profit](../p/profit.md) is limited to the total premiums received from selling both the call and [put options](../p/put_options.md). This occurs if the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) remains exactly at the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) at expiration. In this case, both [options](../o/options.md) expire worthless, and the [investor](../i/investor.md) keeps the entire [premium](../p/premium.md).

### Break-Even Points

There are two break-even points in a reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md):

1. **Upper Break-Even Point:** Calculated as the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) plus the total [premium](../p/premium.md) received.
2. **Lower Break-Even Point:** Calculated as the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) minus the total [premium](../p/premium.md) received.

If the [market price](../m/market_price.md) of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) is within these break-even points at expiration, the [trader](../t/trader.md) still makes a [profit](../p/profit.md) but less than the maximum.

### Maximum Loss

The potential for loss in a reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) is theoretically unlimited. Losses occur if the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) moves significantly above or below the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md), leading to substantial losses that could exceed the [premium](../p/premium.md) collected.

## Example of a Reverse Straddle

Consider an [investor](../i/investor.md) who believes that Stock ABC, currently priced at $100, [will](../w/will.md) not experience significant price movement in the next month. The [investor](../i/investor.md) decides to sell a reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) with [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) $100.

1. **Sell a [Call Option](../c/call_option.md)** with a [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) of $100, receiving a [premium](../p/premium.md) of $5 per share.
2. **Sell a [Put Option](../p/put.md)** with a [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) of $100, receiving a [premium](../p/premium.md) of $5 per share.

Total [premium](../p/premium.md) collected = $5 (Call) + $5 (Put) = $10 per share.

- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md):** $10 per share (if the price stays at $100 at expiration).
- **Upper Break-Even Point:** $100 + $10 = $110.
- **Lower Break-Even Point:** $100 - $10 = $90.

If the stock price is between $90 and $110 at expiration, the [investor](../i/investor.md) makes a [profit](../p/profit.md). If the stock price is either below $90 or above $110, the [investor](../i/investor.md) incurs a loss.

## Advantages of Reverse Straddle

1. **[Premium](../p/premium.md) Collection:** The strategy allows for the immediate collection of premiums, providing upfront [income](../i/income.md).
2. **[Profit](../p/profit.md) in Stable Markets:** Ideal for markets that are expected to be less volatile, allowing the [investor](../i/investor.md) to [profit](../p/profit.md) from [time decay](../t/time_decay.md).
3. **[Neutral](../n/neutral.md) Strategy:** The reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) benefits from the lack of significant price movement, not requiring a perfect [market](../m/market.md) direction prediction.

## Disadvantages of Reverse Straddle

1. **Unlimited Loss Potential:** If the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) experiences high [volatility](../v/volatility.md), losses can be substantial and potentially unlimited.
2. **[Margin](../m/margin.md) Requirements:** Due to the [risk](../r/risk.md) profile, [margin](../m/margin.md) requirements can be significantly higher, necessitating substantial [capital](../c/capital.md) reserves.
3. **Complexity:** It's a relatively complex strategy needing meticulous [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) and [market](../m/market.md) analysis.

## Suitable Market Conditions

A reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) strategy is most effective in markets where there is minimal expected price movement. Typically, it is employed when an [investor](../i/investor.md) has a strong inclination that the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) [will](../w/will.md) [trade](../t/trade.md) within a narrow [range](../r/range.md), hence minimizing the chances of extreme price [volatility](../v/volatility.md).

## Practical Considerations

### Volatility

Understand the [asset](../a/asset.md)'s implied and [historical volatility](../h/historical_volatility.md). A reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) might be inappropriate for highly volatile assets as it could result in significant losses.

### Time Decay

The [value](../v/value.md) of [options](../o/options.md) decreases as they approach their expiration dates. A reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) benefits from this [time decay](../t/time_decay.md), particularly when the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) remains stagnant.

### Market Outlook

Only use the reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) when confident that the [market](../m/market.md) [will](../w/will.md) be stable. Economic reports, [earnings announcements](../e/earnings_announcements.md), and other events that could trigger significant price movements should be factored into the decision-making process.

## Risk Management

Effective [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) is crucial when employing a reverse [straddle](../s/straddle.md) strategy. Possible measures include:

1. **[Stop-Loss Orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md):** Implementing [stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md) to limit potential losses.
2. **Hedging:** Using other financial instruments or strategies to [offset](../o/offset.md) potential losses.
3. **[Capital Allocation](../c/capital_allocation.md):** Allocating only a portion of the trading [capital](../c/capital.md) to this high-[risk](../r/risk.md) strategy.
