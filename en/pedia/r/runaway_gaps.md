# A runaway gap is a concept in technical analysis used predominantly in stock trading, but it also applies to various financial markets, including forex, commodities, and cryptocurrencies. It is one of the types of gaps identified by technical analysts to make trading decisions based on price patterns. Understanding runaway gaps can significantly enhance an algorithmic trader's strategy by allowing them to better interpret and act on market movements.

### Understanding Runaway Gaps

A gap in trading is defined as a region on a price chart where the price of a stock (or other financial instruments) moves sharply up or down with little or no trading in between. Gaps usually occur because of an underlying fundamental or technical factor. They can be categorized into four types: common gaps, breakaway gaps, runaway (or continuation) gaps, and exhaustion gaps.

Runaway gaps, often referred to as continuation gaps, occur typically in the middle of a significant trend. Unlike breakaway gaps that signify the beginning of a new trend, runaway gaps occur within an existing trend and indicate that the trend will continue for a longer period with increased momentum.

#### Characteristics of Runaway Gaps

1. **Location within the Trend**: Runaway gaps usually appear in the middle of a strong trend—be it an uptrend or downtrend. They signify that the market participants have reevaluated the value of the stock, resulting in a new level of enthusiasm.

2. **Volume**: A significant volume often accompanies runaway gaps, which indicates strong interest and confirms the continuation of the trend. Traders often look for increased volume as a signal to validate the gap.

3. **Price Movement**: Unlike common gaps (which may fill quickly) or exhaustion gaps (which often signal the end of a trend), runaway gaps usually do not fill shortly after they appear; instead, the price continues in the direction of the trend.

4. **Duration**: Runaway gaps typically indicate a medium-term outlook of the current trend. Traders may observe the price continuing in the direction of the gap for several days, weeks, or even months.

### Identifying and Trading Runaway Gaps

For algorithmic traders, identifying a runaway gap involves a combination of [technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md) and price [pattern recognition](../p/pattern_recognition.md). Algorithms can be designed to scan for price movements that exhibit the characteristics of a runaway gap and execute trades based on predefined rules. 

#### Technical Indicators

Algorithms can utilize various [technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md) to identify and confirm runaway gaps:

- **Moving Averages**: A moving average crossover (e.g., the 50-day crossing above the [200-day moving average](../1/200-day_moving_average.md)) can strengthen the continuation signal.
- **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**: An RSI can help determine overbought or oversold conditions. A runaway gap in an uptrend with an RSI that is not overbought could be a strong continuation signal.
- **[Volume Indicators](../v/volume_indicators.md)**: High trading volume confirms the validity of the gap. Indicators like On-Balance Volume (OBV) or the Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP) are helpful.

#### Algorithmic Trading Strategies

1. **Breakout Strategy**: Algorithms can be programmed to enter a position in the direction of the trend immediately following the identification of a runaway gap. [Stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md) can be placed just below the gap to mitigate risk.
2. **[Trend Following](../t/trend_following.md) Strategy**: By identifying runaway gaps early, algorithms can follow the trend, adjusting their positions as the price continues to move.
3. **[Mean Reversion](../m/mean_reversion.md) Strategy**: Algorithms may look for temporary pullbacks after the gap occurs to enter positions at a better price. This strategy is riskier given the nature of runaway gaps but can yield significant returns if executed correctly.

### Risk Management

[Risk management](../r/risk_management.md) is crucial in trading runaway gaps due to the potential for price volatility. Implementing [stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md), setting position size limits, and using trailing stops can help manage risk effectively. Additionally, continuous monitoring of the algorithm’s performance and adaptability to changing market conditions is essential.
