# Runaway Gaps

A runaway gap is a concept in [technical analysis](../t/technical_analysis.md) used predominantly in stock trading, but it also applies to various [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md), including forex, commodities, and cryptocurrencies. It is one of the types of [gaps](../g/gap.md) identified by technical analysts to make trading decisions based on [price patterns](../p/price_patterns.md). Understanding runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) can significantly enhance an algorithmic [trader](../t/trader.md)'s strategy by allowing them to better interpret and act on [market](../m/market.md) movements.

### Understanding Runaway Gaps

A gap in trading is defined as a region on a price chart where the price of a stock (or other financial instruments) moves sharply up or down with little or no trading in between. [Gaps](../g/gap.md) usually occur because of an [underlying](../u/underlying.md) fundamental or technical [factor](../f/factor.md). They can be categorized into four types: common [gaps](../g/gap.md), breakaway [gaps](../g/gap.md), runaway (or continuation) [gaps](../g/gap.md), and exhaustion [gaps](../g/gap.md).

Runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md), often referred to as continuation [gaps](../g/gap.md), occur typically in the middle of a significant [trend](../t/trend.md). Unlike breakaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) that signify the beginning of a new [trend](../t/trend.md), runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) occur within an existing [trend](../t/trend.md) and indicate that the [trend](../t/trend.md) [will](../w/will.md) continue for a longer period with increased [momentum](../m/momentum.md).

#### Characteristics of Runaway Gaps

1. **Location within the [Trend](../t/trend.md)**: Runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) usually appear in the middle of a strong [trend](../t/trend.md)—be it an [uptrend](../u/uptrend.md) or [downtrend](../d/downtrend.md). They signify that the [market](../m/market.md) participants have reevaluated the [value](../v/value.md) of the stock, resulting in a new level of enthusiasm.

2. **[Volume](../v/volume.md)**: A significant [volume](../v/volume.md) often accompanies runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md), which indicates strong [interest](../i/interest.md) and confirms the continuation of the [trend](../t/trend.md). Traders often look for increased [volume](../v/volume.md) as a signal to validate the gap.

3. **Price Movement**: Unlike common [gaps](../g/gap.md) (which may fill quickly) or exhaustion [gaps](../g/gap.md) (which often signal the end of a [trend](../t/trend.md)), runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) usually do not fill shortly after they appear; instead, the price continues in the direction of the [trend](../t/trend.md).

4. **[Duration](../d/duration.md)**: Runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) typically indicate a medium-term outlook of the current [trend](../t/trend.md). Traders may observe the price continuing in the direction of the gap for several days, weeks, or even months.

### Identifying and Trading Runaway Gaps

For algorithmic traders, identifying a runaway gap involves a combination of [technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md) and price [pattern recognition](../p/pattern_recognition.md). Algorithms can be designed to scan for price movements that exhibit the characteristics of a runaway gap and execute trades based on predefined rules. 

#### Technical Indicators

Algorithms can utilize various [technical indicators](../t/technical_indicators.md) to identify and confirm runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md):

- **Moving Averages**: A moving average crossover (e.g., the 50-day crossing above the [200-day moving average](../1/200-day_moving_average.md)) can strengthen the continuation signal.
- **[Relative Strength](../r/relative_strength.md) [Index](../i/index_instrument.md) (RSI)**: An RSI can help determine [overbought](../o/overbought.md) or [oversold](../o/oversold.md) conditions. A runaway gap in an [uptrend](../u/uptrend.md) with an RSI that is not [overbought](../o/overbought.md) could be a strong continuation signal.
- **[Volume Indicators](../v/volume_indicators.md)**: High trading [volume](../v/volume.md) confirms the validity of the gap. Indicators like On-Balance [Volume](../v/volume.md) (OBV) or the [Volume](../v/volume.md)-[Weighted Average](../w/weighted_average.md) Price (VWAP) are helpful.

#### Algorithmic Trading Strategies

1. **[Breakout](../b/breakout.md) Strategy**: Algorithms can be programmed to enter a position in the direction of the [trend](../t/trend.md) immediately following the identification of a runaway gap. [Stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md) can be placed just below the gap to mitigate [risk](../r/risk.md).
2. **[Trend Following](../t/trend_following.md) Strategy**: By identifying runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) early, algorithms can follow the [trend](../t/trend.md), adjusting their positions as the price continues to move.
3. **[Mean Reversion](../m/mean_reversion.md) Strategy**: Algorithms may look for temporary pullbacks after the gap occurs to enter positions at a better price. This strategy is riskier given the nature of runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) but can [yield](../y/yield.md) significant returns if executed correctly.

### Risk Management

[Risk management](../r/risk_management.md) is crucial in trading runaway [gaps](../g/gap.md) due to the potential for price [volatility](../v/volatility.md). Implementing [stop-loss orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md), setting position size limits, and using trailing stops can help manage [risk](../r/risk.md) effectively. Additionally, continuous monitoring of the algorithm’s performance and adaptability to changing [market](../m/market.md) conditions is essential.
