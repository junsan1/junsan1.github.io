# Wisconsin School of Business

The Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md), located at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, is an integral part of one of the leading research universities in the United States. The school is renowned for [offering](../o/offering.md) a high-quality education in [business](../b/business.md), preparing students for [leadership](../l/leadership.md) roles in diverse industries. The school's [robust](../r/robust.md) curriculum, extensive research initiatives, and strong [industry](../i/industry.md) connections provide a rich educational experience for both undergraduate and graduate students.

## History and Legacy

The Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md) was founded in 1900, making it one of the oldest [business](../b/business.md) schools in America. Over the years, it has evolved significantly, adapting to the changing needs of the global [business](../b/business.md) landscape. The school has built a strong reputation for its thought [leadership](../l/leadership.md), rigorous academic programs, and commitment to transforming students into skilled professionals.

## Academic Programs

### Undergraduate Program

The undergraduate program at the Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md) offers a comprehensive curriculum that covers essential areas of [business](../b/business.md) such as [finance](../f/finance.md), [marketing](../m/marketing.md), management, and [accounting](../a/accounting.md). Students can choose from a variety of majors and specializations, allowing them to tailor their education to their career goals.

### MBA Program

The full-time MBA program is designed to provide a rigorous and immersive learning experience. It includes a mix of core courses, electives, and experiential learning opportunities through internships, consulting projects, and global study programs. The program offers various concentrations, including [supply chain](../s/supply_chain.md) management, [finance](../f/finance.md), [marketing](../m/marketing.md), and more.

### Specialized Master’s Programs

The school offers specialized master’s programs in areas such as [accounting](../a/accounting.md), [business](../b/business.md) analytics, and [real estate](../r/real_estate.md). These programs are designed to provide in-depth knowledge and [technical skills](../t/technical_skills.md) in specific fields, preparing students for specialized roles in their industries.

### PhD Program

The PhD program at the Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md) enables students to conduct high-level research in various [business](../b/business.md) disciplines. The program emphasizes collaboration, innovation, and academic excellence, preparing graduates for careers in academia and research institutions.

### Executive Education

The Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md) also provides executive education programs tailored for working professionals. These programs are designed to enhance [leadership](../l/leadership.md) skills, strategic thinking, and managerial capabilities, helping executives to drive organizational success.

## Research and Innovation

The Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md) is a hub for innovative research and thought [leadership](../l/leadership.md) in [business](../b/business.md). The faculty members are engaged in cutting-edge research in areas such as [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md), consumer behavior, and organizational strategy. The school’s research centers and institutes, such as the Grainger Center for [Supply Chain](../s/supply_chain.md) Management and the Applied Securities Analysis Program, foster collaboration and thought [leadership](../l/leadership.md).

## Industry Connections

Strong [industry](../i/industry.md) connections are a hallmark of the Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md). The school actively collaborates with leading companies and organizations to provide students with real-world insights and opportunities. Corporate partnerships, guest lectures by [industry](../i/industry.md) leaders, and [networking](../n/networking.md) events help bridge the gap between academic learning and practical application.

## Facilities and Campus Life

The Wisconsin School of [Business](../b/business.md) is housed in modern facilities that support learning and collaboration.
