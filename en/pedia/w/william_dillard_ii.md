# William Dillard II

William T. Dillard II is the CEO of Dillard's Inc., a leading American department store chain with a rich history in retail. Under his [leadership](../l/leadership.md), Dillard's has navigated the complexities of the modern retail landscape, blending traditional in-store shopping experiences with digital innovations. This piece explores William Dillard II's career, his strategies for managing Dillard's, and the company's impact on the retail [industry](../i/industry.md).

## Early Life and Education

William T. Dillard II was born into the world of retail. He is the son of the founder, William T. Dillard, who established Dillard's Inc. in 1938 with a single store in Arkansas. Growing up, William II was exposed to the intricacies of retail management and [business](../b/business.md) from an early age.

William Dillard II attended the University of Arkansas, where he earned a bachelor's degree in [business](../b/business.md) administration. This educational background provided him with a solid foundation in [business](../b/business.md) principles, which he would later apply to his role in the family [business](../b/business.md).

## Career at Dillard's Inc.

William T. Dillard II officially joined Dillard's Inc. in a professional capacity in the late 1960s. This hands-on approach allowed him to understand the operations, challenges, and opportunities at every level of the organization.

### Leadership and Vision

William Dillard II took over as CEO of Dillard's Inc. in 1998, following in his father's footsteps. His [leadership](../l/leadership.md) style is often described as pragmatic and conservative, with a strong focus on maintaining the company's [financial health](../f/financial_health.md) and profitability.

One of his key strategies has been to control the company's growth carefully. Unlike some competitors who rapidly expanded their store count, William Dillard II opted for a more measured approach. This careful [expansion](../e/expansion.md) strategy enabled Dillard's to avoid overleveraging and to maintain flexibility in an ever-changing retail environment.
