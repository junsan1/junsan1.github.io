# Wrap-Around Loan

A wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md), also known as a wrap-around [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md), is a type of secondary [financing](../f/financing.md) for the purchase of [real property](../r/real_property.md). This kind of [loan](../l/loan.md) “wraps” or includes the current [note](../n/note.md) on the property. The wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md) is a unique form of [financing](../f/financing.md) that can benefit both the buyer and the seller in a [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) [transaction](../t/transaction.md). Here, we [will](../w/will.md) delve into various aspects of wrap-around loans ranging from their structure, advantages, risks, and real-world applications.

## Structure of a Wrap-Around Loan

### How It Works
A wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md) involves a secondary form of seller-[financing](../f/financing.md) where the seller retains the original [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md) on the property and offers a secondary [loan](../l/loan.md) that includes the remaining balance on the existing [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md) plus an additional amount to cover the new purchase price. 

### Interest Rates and Payments
Typically, the buyer makes payments to the seller who, in turn, continues to make payments on the existing [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md). The [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) on the wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md) is usually higher than the [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) on the existing [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md), which can benefit the seller with additional [interest](../i/interest.md) [income](../i/income.md). [Payment](../p/payment.md) terms are often [negotiable](../n/negotiable.md) but generally align with standard [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md) schedules.

### Example
For instance, suppose an existing [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md) has a balance of $100,000 at an [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) of 4%, and the seller agrees to sell the property for $150,000 with a new [loan](../l/loan.md) at 6%. The wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md) would be for $150,000 but would include the $100,000 balance of the original [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md). The buyer makes monthly payments based on the new [loan](../l/loan.md) amount and [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) while the seller continues to pay the original [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md).

## Advantages of Wrap-Around Loans

### Benefits to Sellers
1. **Higher [Interest](../i/interest.md) [Income](../i/income.md):** Sellers often charge higher [interest](../i/interest.md) than that on the existing [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md), resulting in additional [profit](../p/profit.md).
2. **Easier [Sale](../s/sale.md) of Property:** [Offering](../o/offering.md) [seller financing](../s/seller_financing.md) may help expedite the property [sale](../s/sale.md) by attracting buyers who may not qualify for traditional mortgages.
3. **Retained Legal Title:** The seller typically retains the legal title until the new [loan](../l/loan.md) is paid off, [offering](../o/offering.md) a layer of [security](../s/security.md).

### Benefits to Buyers
1. **Less Stringent Requirements:** This method can be beneficial for buyers who cannot secure traditional [financing](../f/financing.md) due to poor [credit](../c/credit.md) history or other financial hurdles.
2. **Potentially Lower [Down Payment](../d/down_payment.md):** Depending on the agreement, a wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md) may require a lower [down payment](../d/down_payment.md) compared to traditional [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md) [options](../o/options.md).
3. **Speed:** The [transaction](../t/transaction.md) can often proceed faster than it would through a conventional [lender](../l/lender.md).

## Risks Involved

### Risks to Sellers
1. **[Default](../d/default.md) by Buyer:** If the buyer defaults, the seller must continue paying the original [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md), or face [foreclosure](../f/foreclosure.md).
2. **Enforcement Challenges:** Recovering the property or enforcing [payment](../p/payment.md) terms can be legally complex and costly.

### Risks to Buyers
1. **Title Issues:** As the legal title remains with the seller, the buyer may face complications in securing clear ownership until the [loan](../l/loan.md) is paid off.
2. **Higher Costs:** While beneficial in many respects, wrap-around loans might involve higher [interest](../i/interest.md) rates and payments.

## Legal and Financial Considerations

### Due-on-Sale Clause
Most original mortgages contain a due-on-[sale](../s/sale.md) clause that allows the [lender](../l/lender.md) to [demand](../d/demand.md) full [repayment](../r/repayment.md) of the [loan](../l/loan.md) if the property is sold. It's crucial for both parties to understand that initiating a wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md) might trigger this clause.

### Documentation and Contracts
Comprehensive documentation is essential to outline the terms of the [loan](../l/loan.md), [interest](../i/interest.md) rates, [payment](../p/payment.md) schedules, and [default](../d/default.md) procedures. Engaging legal counsel to draft a solid agreement can mitigate potential risks.

### Regulatory Environment
Depending on the jurisdiction, wrap-around loans may be subject to specific regulatory requirements or restrictions. Both parties should ensure they understand these local laws to avoid legal pitfalls.

## Real-World Applications

### Investment Properties
Investors might use wrap-around loans as a strategy to acquire [multiple](../m/multiple.md) properties with less upfront [capital](../c/capital.md). By leveraging the existing [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md), they can invest in additional properties and potentially generate rental [income](../i/income.md) exceeding the wrap-around [loan](../l/loan.md) payments.

### Sellers in a Buyer's Market
When the [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) [market](../m/market.md) favors buyers, sellers might [offer](../o/offer.md) wrap-around loans to make their properties more attractive and facilitate quicker sales. This method can be particularly effective when conventional [financing](../f/financing.md) [options](../o/options.md) are limited or unattractive.

## Conclusion

Wrap-around loans [offer](../o/offer.md) a viable alternative to traditional [financing](../f/financing.md), particularly when [credit](../c/credit.md) is tight or buyers face challenges securing conventional loans. By understanding the benefits, risks, and [best practices](../b/best_practices.md) associated with this type of [financing](../f/financing.md), both buyers and sellers can effectively utilize wrap-around loans to achieve their [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) goals. However, it is paramount for both parties to undertake [due diligence](../d/due_diligence.md), secure proper documentation, and consult legal and financial advisors to navigate the complexities involved.
