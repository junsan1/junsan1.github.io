# University of Miami School of Business Administration

The University of Miami School of [Business](../b/business.md) Administration, now known as the Miami Herbert [Business](../b/business.md) School, is a leading institution dedicated to educating the next generation of global [business](../b/business.md) leaders. The school provides a [range](../r/range.md) of undergraduate, graduate, and executive education programs accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of [Business](../b/business.md) (AACSB).

## History and Overview

Founded in 1929, the Miami Herbert [Business](../b/business.md) School has established itself as an integral part of the University of Miami, which was itself founded in 1925. The [business](../b/business.md) school offers a broad array of programs that include Bachelor of [Business](../b/business.md) Administration (BBA), Master of [Business](../b/business.md) Administration (MBA), Master of Science (MS) in various disciplines, and Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degrees. Located in Coral Gables, Florida, the school benefits from a vibrant [business](../b/business.md) community and close proximity to Latin American markets, providing unique opportunities for international [business](../b/business.md) education.

## Programs and Curriculum

The undergraduate programs are designed to provide a solid foundation in core [business](../b/business.md) concepts. Typically, the curriculum includes courses in:

- [Marketing](../m/marketing.md)
- Management
- [Finance](../f/finance.md)

- [Business](../b/business.md) Technology

### Graduate Programs

#### MBA Programs

The MBA programs are some of the most popular graduate-level offerings and come in various formats to accommodate different types of students:

- **Full-Time MBA**: A traditional two-year program designed for individuals looking to advance their careers.
- **Executive MBA (EMBA)**: Tailored for working professionals with significant experience.
- **Online MBA**: For students who require flexibility in their learning schedules.

## Partnerships and Corporate Relations

The school actively engages with the [business](../b/business.md) community through various partnerships and advisory boards. These relationships facilitate real-world applications of classroom concepts, [offering](../o/offering.md) students internships, mentorships, and [networking](../n/networking.md) opportunities. Notable partnerships include collaborations with multinational corporations, financial institutions, and non-profits.

## Facilities and Resources

The [business](../b/business.md) school is housed in a modern [facility](../f/facility.md) that includes state-of-the-art classrooms, computer labs, and collaborative spaces. The Ziff Graduate Career Services Center provides comprehensive career support to students and alumni, including job placement services and career counseling.

## Global Reach

Miami Herbert [Business](../b/business.md) School has a strong focus on international [business](../b/business.md), leveraging its geographic position as a gateway to Latin America. Students have the opportunity to participate in study abroad programs, international internships, and global consulting projects. The school's alumni network is also globally dispersed, providing a powerful platform for career advancement.

## Alumni Network

The alumni network is extensive, with graduates holding influential positions across various sectors, such as [finance](../f/finance.md), technology, healthcare, and non-profits. Alumni events, [networking](../n/networking.md) opportunities, and lifelong learning resources are regularly offered to keep graduates connected to the school and each other.
