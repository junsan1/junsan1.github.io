# Unfunded Pension Plan

An unfunded [pension plan](../p/pension_plan.md) is a retirement plan where no funds are set aside in advance by the employer or sponsoring entity to meet future pension [obligations](../o/obligation.md). Under this type of plan, the pension benefits are paid directly out of the current [revenue](../r/revenue.md) or general assets of the employer as they become due. This approach contrasts with funded pension plans, where assets are accumulated in advance to cover future pension liabilities.

## Types of Unfunded Pension Plans

### Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) Plans

In a PAYG plan, current employees' contributions are used to pay the pensions of retired employees. This is a common method for many [social security](../s/social_security.md) systems globally, including the United States [Social Security](../s/social_security.md) system and public pension schemes in various countries.

### Book Reserve Plans

Book reserve plans, commonly found in Europe and Japan, rely on the employer merely setting aside a certain amount as a [liability](../l/liability.md) on its [balance sheet](../b/balance_sheet.md), rather than actually creating a separate [fund](../f/fund.md). The pensions are paid out of the employer's operational cash flows.

## Working Mechanism of Unfunded Pension Plans

In an unfunded [pension plan](../p/pension_plan.md), when an employee retires, the employer pays the retiree’s pension from its current [revenue](../r/revenue.md) or profits. This can involve significant [risk](../r/risk.md), as it depends on the employer's continued ability to generate sufficient [revenue](../r/revenue.md) or maintain sufficient financial stability to meet these [obligations](../o/obligation.md).

### Advantages

1. **Simplicity**: These plans are easier to administer, as they don’t require the maintenance of a pension [fund](../f/fund.md) with associated investment strategies.
2. **Flexibility**: Employers have more flexibility and direct control over the use of cash flows.

### Disadvantages

1. **[Risk](../r/risk.md) of [Insolvency](../i/insolvency.md)**: There is a significant [risk](../r/risk.md) that the company might be unable to meet pension [obligations](../o/obligation.md) in the future if their financial condition deteriorates.
2. **Lack of [Security](../s/security.md) for Employees**: Employees might be at [risk](../r/risk.md) of receiving reduced or no pension benefits if the employer faces financial difficulties.

## Regulatory Environment

Regulations surrounding unfunded pension plans vary from country to country. In the United States, unfunded pension plans are often subject to the Employee Retirement [Income](../i/income.md) [Security](../s/security.md) Act (ERISA) but they might be exempt from certain funding requirements applicable to other plans.

### United States

- **[Social Security](../s/social_security.md) System**: Managed on a pay-as-you-go [basis](../b/basis.md), funded primarily through [payroll](../p/payroll.md) [taxes](../t/taxes.md).
- **Nonprofit and Government Entities**: Often employ unfunded plans, relying on future revenues to meet [obligations](../o/obligation.md).

### Europe

- **Germany**: Predominantly uses the book reserve system for unfunded pension [obligations](../o/obligation.md), with well-defined regulations.
- **United Kingdom**: Primarily uses funded schemes but has a significant portion of public sector pensions that operate on a PAYG [basis](../b/basis.md).

## Corporate Governance and Unfunded Pension Plans

Effective oversight and governance are crucial for ensuring that companies can meet their commitments under unfunded pension plans. Key practices include:

1. **[Transparency](../t/transparency.md)**: Clear reporting of pension liabilities in the [financial statements](../f/financial_statements.md).
2. **[Risk Management](../r/risk_management.md)**: Regular assessment of the company’s ability to meet future [obligations](../o/obligation.md) under different economic scenarios.

## Examples of Unfunded Pension Plans

### Government Pension Plans

Government pension plans are frequently unfunded, relying on [taxation](../t/taxation.md) or other [revenue](../r/revenue.md) sources to pay pensions as they become due. Examples include public employee pensions at the local, state, and federal levels.

- **United States [Social Security](../s/social_security.md)**: [Social Security Administration](https://www.ssa.gov)
- **Public Service Pension Plans in the UK**: [UK Government Pensions](https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/civil-service-pensions)

### Corporate Unfunded Plans

Certain large corporations may also have unfunded pension liabilities, although this is becoming less common due to shifting regulatory standards and the associated risks.

## Conclusion

Unfunded pension plans [offer](../o/offer.md) a simpler, more flexible approach to providing retirement benefits. However, they carry significant risks for both the employer and employees, including potential [insolvency](../i/insolvency.md) and lack of [security](../s/security.md). Effective governance, [transparency](../t/transparency.md), and [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) are essential to mitigating these risks and ensuring that pension [obligations](../o/obligation.md) can be met in the long term.
