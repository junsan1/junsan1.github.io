# Underlying Option Security

In the realm of [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md), especially when discussing [options](../o/options.md) trading, the term **"[underlying](../u/underlying.md) option [security](../s/security.md)"** is frequently encountered. This term refers to the [financial instrument](../f/financial_instrument.md) upon which an [options contract](../o/options_contract.md) is based. Understanding [underlying](../u/underlying.md) option securities is fundamental for [options](../o/options.md) traders, as these securities dictate the price and behavior of the [options](../o/options.md) contracts written on them. This detailed discussion aims to thoroughly explore the concept of [underlying](../u/underlying.md) option securities, their types, and their impact on the [options](../o/options.md) [market](../m/market.md).

## Understanding the Basics

An option is a [derivative](../d/derivative.md) contract that grants the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an [asset](../a/asset.md) at a predetermined price before or on a specific date. This [asset](../a/asset.md), which the contract refers to, is the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) option [security](../s/security.md). The [value](../v/value.md) and price movements of the [options contract](../o/options_contract.md) are inherently tied to this [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md).

There are primarily two types of [options](../o/options.md):
1. **Call [Options](../o/options.md)** - Grants the holder the right to buy the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md).
2. **[Put Options](../p/put_options.md)** - Grants the holder the right to sell the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md).

## Types of Underlying Option Securities

[Underlying](../u/underlying.md) option securities can vary widely, spanning [multiple](../m/multiple.md) [asset](../a/asset.md) classes. Here, we categorize and explain several common types:

### 1. **Equities (Stocks)**
Equities or [stocks](../s/stock.md) are the most prevalent [underlying](../u/underlying.md) option securities. [Options](../o/options.md) on individual [stocks](../s/stock.md) allow traders to speculate on the future price movements of specific companies. For example, a [call option](../c/call_option.md) on Apple Inc. (AAPL) allows the holder to purchase AAPL [shares](../s/shares.md) at a [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) within a defined period.

### 2. **Indices**
An [index](../i/index.md) is a statistical measure that reflects the composite [value](../v/value.md) of a selected group of [stocks](../s/stock.md). [Options](../o/options.md) on indices, such as the S&P 500 (SPX) or the [NASDAQ](../n/nasdaq.md)-100 (NDX), permit traders to [gain](../g/gain.md) exposure to a broader [market](../m/market.md) or [industry](../i/industry.md) [sector performance](../s/sector_performance.md).

### 3. **Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)**
ETFs are investment funds traded on stock exchanges. They [hold](../h/hold.md) assets such as [stocks](../s/stock.md), commodities, or bonds and generally operate with an [arbitrage](../a/arbitrage.md) mechanism designed to keep trading close to its net [asset](../a/asset.md) [value](../v/value.md). [Options](../o/options.md) on ETFs, like the SPDR S&P 500 ETF (SPY), provide a way to [trade](../t/trade.md) broad [market](../m/market.md) indices or specific sectors.

### 4. **Commodities**
These are physical goods like gold, silver, oil, or agricultural products. [Options](../o/options.md) contracts on commodities like Gold (GLD) allow traders to [hedge](../h/hedge.md) against or speculate on [commodity](../c/commodity.md) price movements.

### 5. **Currencies**
[Currency](../c/currency.md) [options](../o/options.md) give the holder the right to purchase or sell a foreign [currency](../c/currency.md) at a specified [exchange rate](../e/exchange_rate.md) on or before a certain date. They are essential tools in forex markets, facilitating hedging against [currency](../c/currency.md) [risk](../r/risk.md).

### 6. **Interest Rates**
[Interest rate options](../i/interest_rate_options.md) provide traders with the ability to speculate on the direction of [interest](../i/interest.md) rates or [hedge](../h/hedge.md) against rate changes. The most common [interest rate options](../i/interest_rate_options.md) are those on Treasury bonds and notes.

### 7. **Cryptocurrencies**
With the rise of digital currencies, [options](../o/options.md) on cryptocurrencies such as [Bitcoin](../b/bitcoin.md) (BTC) and [Ethereum](../e/ethereum_.md) (ETH) have also emerged. These [options](../o/options.md) provide new avenues for [speculation](../s/speculation.md) and [risk management](../r/risk_management.md) in the volatile crypto [market](../m/market.md).

## Pricing and Valuation

The price of an option, also known as the [premium](../p/premium.md), is influenced by various factors related to the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md). The primary determinants include:

### 1. **Underlying Asset Price**
The current price of the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md) is the most significant [factor](../f/factor.md). For a [call option](../c/call_option.md), as the price of the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md) increases, the [value](../v/value.md) of the option typically increases. Conversely, for a [put option](../p/put.md), the [value](../v/value.md) increases as the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md) price decreases.

### 2. **Strike Price**
The [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) is the price at which the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) can be bought or sold. [Options](../o/options.md) with strike prices closer to the current price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) usually have higher premiums.

### 3. **Time to Expiration**
Also known as the [time value](../t/time_value.md), the [duration](../d/duration.md) until the option's [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md) affects its price. Generally, the longer the time until expiration, the higher the [premium](../p/premium.md), as there is more time for the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md)’s price to move favorably.

### 4. **Volatility**
[Volatility](../v/volatility.md) refers to the extent of price fluctuations in the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md). Higher [volatility](../v/volatility.md) often leads to higher option premiums because the probability of substantial price movement increases, benefiting option holders.

### 5. **Dividends**
For [underlying](../u/underlying.md) securities that pay dividends, the expected dividends can impact option pricing. This is because dividends can influence the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md)’s price, especially around the [ex-dividend](../e/ex-dividend.md) date.

### 6. **Interest Rates**
Prevailing [interest](../i/interest.md) rates impact [options](../o/options.md) pricing through the cost of carrying the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md). Higher [interest](../i/interest.md) rates can increase [call option](../c/call_option.md) premiums and decrease [put option](../p/put.md) premiums.

## Strategic Uses of Underlying Option Securities

[Options trading strategies](../o/options_trading_strategies.md) are built upon predictions about the movements of [underlying](../u/underlying.md) option securities. Here are a few strategic uses:

### 1. **Hedging**
Investors use [options](../o/options.md) to [hedge](../h/hedge.md) against potential losses in their portfolios. For example, a [put option](../p/put.md) on a stock can protect against a decline in that stock’s price.

### 2. **Speculation**
Traders might buy [options](../o/options.md) to speculate on the future direction of a [security](../s/security.md)’s price. This can allow them to [leverage](../l/leverage.md) their position with less [capital](../c/capital.md) compared to buying the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md) outright.

### 3. **Income Generation**
Strategies like writing covered calls involve holding the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md) and selling a [call option](../c/call_option.md) on it. This can generate [income](../i/income.md) through the [premium](../p/premium.md) received from writing the option.

### 4. **Risk Management**
[Options](../o/options.md) can be used to manage [risk](../r/risk.md) by setting predefined potential losses. For instance, buying protective puts provides a safety net against potential downturns in the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md)’s price.

### 5. **Arbitrage**
Some traders [leverage](../l/leverage.md) [options](../o/options.md) as part of [arbitrage](../a/arbitrage.md) strategies, capitalizing on price discrepancies between the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md) and [derivatives](../d/derivatives.md) markets.

## Key Market Participants

The [market](../m/market.md) for [options](../o/options.md) involves various participants, each playing a pivotal role:

### 1. **Retail Investors**
Individual investors who engage in [options](../o/options.md) trading for personal accounts.

### 2. **Institutional Investors**
Entities such as mutual funds, pension funds, and [hedge](../h/hedge.md) funds that utilize [options](../o/options.md) to manage large portfolios and [hedge](../h/hedge.md) significant positions.

### 3. **Market Makers**
Firms that provide [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) in [options](../o/options.md) markets by being ready to buy and sell [options](../o/options.md), facilitating smoother and more efficient trading.

### 4. **Exchanges**
Organizations such as the Chicago Board [Options](../o/options.md) [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (CBOE) where [options](../o/options.md) are traded. They provide a structured environment with regulations and oversight.

### 5. **Brokers**
Intermediaries that facilitate the trading of [options](../o/options.md) between buyers and sellers, [offering](../o/offering.md) platforms and tools for [execution](../e/execution.md) and analysis.

## Real-World Example: The VIX Index

An illustrative example of [underlying](../u/underlying.md) option [security](../s/security.md) is [options](../o/options.md) based on the Cboe [Volatility](../v/volatility.md) [Index](../i/index.md) (VIX), often referred to as the "fear gauge." The VIX measures [market](../m/market.md) expectations of near-term [volatility](../v/volatility.md) conveyed by S&P 500 stock [index option](../i/index_option.md) prices. When trading [VIX options](../v/vix_option.md), the [underlying security](../u/underlying_security.md) is the VIX [index](../i/index.md) itself, a [benchmark](../b/benchmark.md) for [market](../m/market.md) [volatility](../v/volatility.md).
