# Unrealized Gain

An **unrealized [gain](../g/gain.md)** refers to the increase in the [value](../v/value.md) of an [asset](../a/asset.md) or investment that an individual or [business](../b/business.md) holds but has not yet sold for cash. This type of [gain](../g/gain.md) exists only on paper and represents potential [profit](../p/profit.md) that could be realized if the [asset](../a/asset.md) were to be sold at its current [market value](../m/market_value.md). [Unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) are also known as "paper gains" because, unlike realized gains, they do not result in actual [cash flow](../c/cash_flow.md). The [gain](../g/gain.md) becomes realized only when the [asset](../a/asset.md) is sold.

## Key Components

### Asset Types

[Unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) can occur with various types of assets including:

- **[Stocks](../s/stock.md) and Bonds**: If the [market value](../m/market_value.md) of [stocks](../s/stock.md) or bonds increases compared to their purchase price.
- **[Real Estate](../r/real_estate.md)**: When the [market value](../m/market_value.md) of [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) properties appreciates without a [sale](../s/sale.md).
- **Commodities**: Appreciation in the [market value](../m/market_value.md) of commodities like gold or silver.
- **Cryptocurrencies**: Digital assets whose [market value](../m/market_value.md) increases before they are sold.

### Measurement

[Unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) are typically measured by the difference between the current [market value](../m/market_value.md) of the [asset](../a/asset.md) and its original purchase price or [cost basis](../c/cost_basis.md). For example, if you bought a share of stock at $50 and its current [market value](../m/market_value.md) is $80, you would have an unrealized [gain](../g/gain.md) of $30 per share.

### Financial Statements

In [financial accounting](../f/financial_accounting.md), [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) are recorded differently depending on the [accounting](../a/accounting.md) standards and the nature of the [asset](../a/asset.md):
- **Held-for-Trading Securities**: [Unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) for assets held for trading purposes are recorded on the [income statement](../i/income_statement.md), impacting net [income](../i/income.md).
- **Available-for-[Sale](../s/sale.md) Securities**: [Unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) for available-for-[sale](../s/sale.md) securities are recorded in other [comprehensive income](../c/comprehensive_income.md) (OCI), not directly impacting net [income](../i/income.md).

### Tax Implications

[Unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) are generally not subject to [taxation](../t/taxation.md) until they are realized. This means that you typically do not owe [taxes](../t/taxes.md) on the increase in [value](../v/value.md) until the [asset](../a/asset.md) is sold and the [gain](../g/gain.md) is realized. However, some jurisdictions may have specific tax rules regarding these gains.

## Importance in Investment Strategy

### Deferred Taxation

One significant benefit of [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) is the deferral of tax liabilities. Investors can avoid paying [taxes](../t/taxes.md) on the gains until they actually sell the [asset](../a/asset.md), allowing for potentially greater returns through [compound interest](../c/compound_interest_in_trading.md) or [reinvestment](../r/reinvestment.md).

### Performance Measurement

[Unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) are essential for assessing the performance of an investment portfolio. They provide a snapshot of how well the investments are performing over a specific period, even if the gains have not been realized.

### Risk Management

Managing [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) involves understanding [market](../m/market.md) conditions and making strategic decisions about when to realize the gains. Holding onto investments too long can result in losses if the [market](../m/market.md) conditions change unfavorably.

## Common Situations with Unrealized Gains

### Equity Investments

One of the most common scenarios involves stock investments. For instance, an individual purchases [shares](../s/shares.md) in a technology company at a low price. Over time, as the company grows, its share price increases significantly. The increase in [value](../v/value.md) is recorded as an unrealized [gain](../g/gain.md) until the [shares](../s/shares.md) are sold.

### Real Estate Investments

In [real estate](../r/real_estate.md), property values can appreciate due to various factors such as location development, [economic growth](../e/economic_growth.md), or [inflation](../i/inflation.md). Investors [hold](../h/hold.md) onto these properties with rising values, reflecting large [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md) in their [asset](../a/asset.md) portfolios.

### Cryptocurrencies

The volatile nature of cryptocurrencies like [Bitcoin](../b/bitcoin.md) and [Ethereum](../e/ethereum_.md) means that investors often see substantial [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md). The [value](../v/value.md) of these digital assets can change swiftly, providing opportunities for significant paper gains before they are realized.

## Managing Unrealized Gains

### Determining Time to Realize Gains

Investors often face the challenge of determining the optimal time to realize their gains. This involves analyzing [market](../m/market.md) trends, [asset](../a/asset.md) performance, and tax considerations. Holding assets for a longer period might lead to greater tax [efficiency](../e/efficiency.md) through long-term [capital gains tax](../c/capital_gains_tax.md) rates.

### Hedging Strategies

Investors may use [hedging strategies](../h/hedging_strategies.md) to protect their [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md). Techniques such as [options](../o/options.md) and [futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md) can provide a safety net against adverse [market](../m/market.md) movements.

### Rebalancing Portfolios

Regularly [rebalancing](../r/rebalancing.md) an investment portfolio helps in managing [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md). By selling over-performing assets and buying under-performing ones, investors can lock in some gains and maintain a balanced [risk](../r/risk.md) profile.

### Capital Gains Tax Planning

Effective [tax planning](../t/tax_planning.md) can maximize the after-tax returns on investments. This may involve strategies such as tax-loss harvesting or utilizing [tax-advantaged](../t/tax-advantaged.md) accounts to defer or minimize [taxes](../t/taxes.md) on [unrealized gains](../u/unrealized_gains.md).
