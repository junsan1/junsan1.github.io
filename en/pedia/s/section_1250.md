# Section 1250

Section 1250 refers to a specific [provision](../p/provision.md) of the United States Internal [Revenue](../r/revenue.md) Code (IRC) that deals with the [taxation](../t/taxation.md) of gains from the [disposition](../d/disposition.md) of certain types of depreciable [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) property. It primarily applies to buildings and improvements that have been depreciated under a system other than the straight-line [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) method. The significance of Section 1250 lies in its implications for investors, property owners, and businesses that manage portfolios of [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) assets.

## Overview of Depreciation

[Depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) is an [accounting method](../a/accounting_method.md) used to allocate the cost of [tangible assets](../t/tangible_asset.md) over their useful lives. It reflects the wear and tear, decay, or decline in usefulness that assets experience over time. For [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) properties, [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) can be claimed as an [expense](../e/expense.md), reducing the [taxable income](../t/taxable_income.md) of the owner.

When these properties are sold, the gains from the [sale](../s/sale.md) may be subject to [taxes](../t/taxes.md). Section 1250 is specifically concerned with the recapture of [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) for certain [real property](../r/real_property.md) and outlines how these recaptured amounts are taxed.

## Types of Depreciable Real Estate

Under Section 1250, the types of depreciable [real property](../r/real_property.md) include:

1. **Commercial Buildings**: These include office buildings, retail stores, and [manufacturing](../m/manufacturing.md) facilities.
2. **Residential Rental Properties**: [Home](../h/home.md) and apartment buildings used for renting to tenants.
3. **Improvements**: Structural improvements to land, such as roads, bridges, and landscaping that have been depreciated.

## Depreciation Methods

[Real estate](../r/real_estate.md) [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) can be computed using various methods. The two most common methods are:

1. **Straight-Line [Depreciation](../d/depreciation.md)**: This method [spreads](../s/spreads.md) the cost of the [asset](../a/asset.md) evenly over its [useful life](../u/useful_life.md). For tax purposes, the IRS prescribes a [useful life](../u/useful_life.md) for different types of property.
2. **[Accelerated Depreciation](../a/accelerated_depreciation.md)**: This method allows for larger [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) deductions in the earlier years of the [asset](../a/asset.md)'s life and smaller deductions later. Common [accelerated depreciation](../a/accelerated_depreciation.md) methods include the Double Declining Balance (DDB) and the Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS).

## Purpose of Section 1250

Section 1250 was enacted to address the tax treatment of gains from the [sale](../s/sale.md) of [real property](../r/real_property.md) that had been depreciated using accelerated methods. The rationale behind this [provision](../p/provision.md) is to recapture the "excess" [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) that exceeds what would have been allowed under the straight-line method.

## Tax Treatment under Section 1250

When a depreciable [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) property is sold, the [taxpayer](../t/taxpayer.md) must determine the amount of [gain](../g/gain.md) that is subject to recapture under Section 1250. The calculations involve several steps:

1. **Adjusted [Basis](../b/basis.md)**: This is the [original cost](../o/original_cost.md) of the property adjusted for [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) deductions taken over time.
2. **[Realized Gain](../r/realized_gain.md)**: The difference between the selling price of the property and its adjusted [basis](../b/basis.md).
3. **Section 1250 Recapture**: This portion of the [gain](../g/gain.md) represents the [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) that exceeds what would have been claimed under the straight-line method. This recaptured amount is taxed as [ordinary income](../o/ordinary_income.md), up to a maximum rate of 25%.

For example, consider a commercial building purchased for $500,000 and depreciated to $300,000 over several years using an accelerated method. If the building is sold for $600,000, the $300,000 difference between the [sale](../s/sale.md) price and the adjusted [basis](../b/basis.md) is the total [gain](../g/gain.md). Under Section 1250, the portion of this [gain](../g/gain.md) attributable to excess [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) would be recaptured and taxed at the higher [ordinary income](../o/ordinary_income.md) rate, while the remainder would generally be treated as long-term [capital gain](../c/capital_gain.md).

## Exclusions and Exemptions

Certain properties and situations may be excluded from Section 1250 recapture rules:

1. **Straight-Line [Depreciation](../d/depreciation.md)**: Properties fully depreciated using the straight-line method do not trigger Section 1250 recapture.
2. **[Principal](../p/principal.md) Residences**: Generally, personal residences are exempt from these rules.
3. **Like-Kind Exchanges**: Under IRC Section 1031, properties exchanged for similar properties may defer recognition of gains, including those subject to Section 1250.

## Implications for Investors and Property Owners

Investors and property owners should be aware of the tax implications under Section 1250 when managing their [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) portfolios. It impacts decisions regarding:

1. **Purchase and [Sale](../s/sale.md) Timing**: Awareness of potential recapture can influence the timing of buying and selling properties.
2. **[Depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) Strategies**: Choosing between straight-line and [accelerated depreciation](../a/accelerated_depreciation.md) methods based on long-term [tax planning](../t/tax_planning.md).
3. **[Estate Planning](../e/estate_planning.md)**: Properly managing [real estate](../r/real_estate.md) assets within an [estate](../e/estate.md) to minimize the impact of [depreciation recapture](../d/depreciation_recapture.md).
4. **Financial Reporting**: Accurate financial and tax records are essential for determining the recaptured [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) at [sale](../s/sale.md).
