# Short Squeeze

A short squeeze is a [market](../m/market.md) event in which short sellers are forced to buy back [shares](../s/shares.md) of a stock they had borrowed and sold short, in [order](../o/order.md) to prevent or limit losses as the stock price rises. The phenomenon can cause a rapid, dramatic increase in the stock's price, volatile trading environments, and significant financial repercussions for traders.

## Understanding Short Selling

To understand a short squeeze, it is crucial first to grasp the concept of [short selling](../s/short_selling.md). [Short selling](../s/short_selling.md) refers to the practice of selling borrowed [stocks](../s/stock.md) with the intention of buying them back at a lower price. In essence, a [trader](../t/trader.md) borrows [shares](../s/shares.md) from a [broker](../b/broker.md) and sells them on the [open market](../o/open_market.md) at the current price, believing that the stock's price [will](../w/will.md) decline. Once the price drops, the [trader](../t/trader.md) buys back the [shares](../s/shares.md) at the lower price, returns them to the [lender](../l/lender.md), and pockets the difference.

- **Short positions**: When a [trader](../t/trader.md) has sold borrowed [shares](../s/shares.md), it's said they have a short position. This is fundamentally different from a long position where the [trader](../t/trader.md) owns the stock hoping its price [will](../w/will.md) rise.
- **[Margin](../m/margin.md) accounts**: [Short selling](../s/short_selling.md) typically requires a [margin account](../m/margin_account.md), wherein the [broker](../b/broker.md) loans the [trader](../t/trader.md) the borrowed [shares](../s/shares.md) and charges [interest](../i/interest.md) on the [margin](../m/margin.md) [loan](../l/loan.md).

## Preconditions for a Short Squeeze

Short squeezes happen when several conditions align, including but not limited to:

1. **Heavy [Short Interest](../s/short_interest.md)**: This refers to a high percentage of [shares](../s/shares.md) that have been sold short but not yet covered or closed out. When a stock has heavy [short interest](../s/short_interest.md), it suggests many traders are betting against it.
   
2. **Favorable News**: Any new information that could potentially increase the stock price triggers panic buying from short sellers.

3. **Limited [Float](../f/float.md)**: When the number of public [shares](../s/shares.md) available for trading is low, any increase in [demand](../d/demand.md) can disproportionately affect the stock price.

4. **Bullish [Market Sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md)**: A general upward [trend](../t/trend.md) in the [market](../m/market.md) or sector can spur buying activity, causing short sellers to feel the heat.

## Mechanics of a Short Squeeze

When the stock price rises contrary to short sellers' expectations, it can set off a chain reaction known as a short squeeze. Here's how this process unfolds:

1. **Rising Stock Price**: Positive news, stronger-than-expected [earnings](../e/earnings.md), or other bullish events cause the stock price to increase.
   
2. **[Margin](../m/margin.md) Calls**: As the stock price rises, short sellers face [unrealized losses](../u/unrealized_losses.md). Brokers may [issue](../i/issue.md) [margin](../m/margin.md) calls, forcing traders to add more [capital](../c/capital.md) to their [margin](../m/margin.md) accounts or buy back the stock to close out their positions.
   
3. **Panic Buying**: To limit losses or avoid further [margin](../m/margin.md) calls, short sellers rush to buy back the stock, adding to the [demand](../d/demand.md). This buying pressure can cause the stock price to escalate further.

4. **Feedback Loop**: The rising stock price attracts additional buyers, some of whom are speculating on the short squeeze itself. This can create a feedback loop of rising prices and escalating buying.

## Historical Examples

### Volkswagen (2008)

One of the most famous short squeezes occurred with Volkswagen in 2008. At that time, [hedge](../h/hedge.md) funds and investors were heavily shorting the stock, expecting it to decline. However, Porsche unveiled that it had secretly gained control of 74% of Volkswagen's [shares](../s/shares.md). With a large portion of [shares](../s/shares.md) now unavailable to buy, short sellers scrambled to cover their positions. Volkswagen's [shares](../s/shares.md) soared from about €200 to over €1,000 in less than two days.

### GameStop (2021)

In early 2021, GameStop became the centerpiece of a legendary short squeeze. Orchestrated mainly by retail investors on the subreddit group `r/WallStreetBets`, they targeted the heavily shorted [shares](../s/shares.md) of GameStop. The [short interest](../s/short_interest.md) was over 100% of the [float](../f/float.md). As [social media](../s/social_media.md) buzz and the "[buy and hold](../b/buy_and_hold.md)" strategy caught fire, GameStop's share price catapulted from less than $20 to over $300 in a matter of weeks, causing colossal losses for [hedge](../h/hedge.md) funds shorting the stock.

### AMC Entertainment (2021)

Coinciding with the GameStop saga, AMC Entertainment also experienced a dramatic short squeeze. Similar strategies were applied, with small investors betting against institutional short sellers. AMC's stock price saw a meteoric rise from around $2 in early 2021 to over $60 by June of the same year.

## Risks and Considerations

### Risks for Short Sellers

1. **Unlimited Loss Potential**: Unlike long positions, where the maximum loss is the initial investment (as the stock price can theoretically drop only to zero), short positions have theoretically [unlimited risk](../u/unlimited_risk.md) because there's no cap on how high a stock price can rise.
   
2. **[Margin](../m/margin.md) Requirements**: [Short selling](../s/short_selling.md) typically involves borrowing [shares](../s/shares.md), requiring a [margin account](../m/margin_account.md). If a stock price rises significantly, brokers might [demand](../d/demand.md) additional [collateral](../c/collateral.md), known as a [margin call](../m/margin_call.md).

3. **[Market](../m/market.md) [Volatility](../v/volatility.md)**: The rapid price increase inherent in short squeezes leads to volatile trading conditions, making it challenging to execute orders at desired prices.

### Risks for Long Positions

1. **[Volatility](../v/volatility.md)**: Long investors may find themselves caught in the turbulence of a short squeeze, experiencing sharp price swings that can lead to forced sales or panic-selling.

2. **[Market Manipulation](../m/market_manipulation.md)**: Short squeezes can sometimes be fueled by coordinated buying by groups of traders. This may lead to allegations of [market manipulation](../m/market_manipulation.md), potentially complicating legal and regulatory scenarios.

### Legal and Ethical Considerations

- **[Market Manipulation](../m/market_manipulation.md)**: Coordinated efforts to induce a short squeeze may border on [market manipulation](../m/market_manipulation.md), which can attract regulatory scrutiny. Platforms and forums used to facilitate such coordination may face regulatory action.
  
- **[Transparency](../t/transparency.md) and [Disclosure](../d/disclosure.md)**: [Hedge](../h/hedge.md) funds and institutional investors must disclose their short positions according to [market](../m/market.md) regulations, but retail investors are not held to the same standard, leading to disparities in [market](../m/market.md) information.

## Technology's Role

### Algorithmic Trading

[Algorithmic trading](../a/algorithmic_trading.md)—computers executing pre-set trading instructions based on variables such as price, timing, and [volume](../v/volume.md)—can amplify the speed and extent of short squeezes. Here are a few ways [algorithmic trading](../a/algorithmic_trading.md) influences short squeezes:

- **Speed**: Algorithms can execute orders at millisecond intervals, contributing to rapid price movements.

- **[Volume](../v/volume.md)**: High-frequency traders (HFTs) provide [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) but can also remove it rapidly, exacerbating price [volatility](../v/volatility.md) during a short squeeze.

Tradebot Systems, one example of a [firm](../f/firm.md) highly involved in [algorithmic trading](../a/algorithmic_trading.md), has been known to [capitalize](../c/capitalize.md) on short squeeze events. More details about their operation can be found on their [website](http://www.tradebotsystems.com/).

### Social Media and Online Forums

Platforms like Reddit, Twitter, and Discord have become instrumental in coordinating retail [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md). The GameStop saga is a prime example of the power of [social media](../s/social_media.md) in driving [market](../m/market.md) phenomena like short squeezes. This democratization of [market](../m/market.md) participation has led to both opportunities and challenges.

## Conclusion

Short squeezes are intricate [market](../m/market.md) events that entail significant financial risks and rewards. They highlight the dynamic interplay between [market sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md), regulatory frameworks, and technological advancements. While they can result in substantial gains for some participants, the inherent [volatility](../v/volatility.md) and potential for unlimited losses make them perilous for the unprepared [trader](../t/trader.md). Therefore, a thorough understanding of the mechanics, risks, and ethical considerations is crucial for anyone looking to navigate or [capitalize](../c/capitalize.md) on a short squeeze.
