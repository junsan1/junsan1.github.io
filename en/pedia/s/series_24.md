# Series 24

The Series 24, also known as the General Securities [Principal](../p/principal.md) Qualification Examination, is a license and exam administered by the Financial [Industry](../i/industry.md) Regulatory Authority (FINRA) in the United States. The Series 24 examination evaluates the competency of candidates aiming to become principals in the securities [industry](../i/industry.md). Successfully passing the Series 24 exam authorizes the candidate to manage or supervise the [investment banking](../i/investment_banking.md) or securities [business](../b/business.md) of a [broker-dealer](../b/broker-dealer.md).

## Examination Overview

The Series 24 exam consists of 150 [multiple](../m/multiple.md)-choice questions that cover a comprehensive [range](../r/range.md) of topics necessary for managing a [broker-dealer](../b/broker-dealer.md). Candidates are given three hours and 45 minutes to complete the exam, and a passing score is 70%. The key areas evaluated in the Series 24 exam include:

1. **Supervision of [Investment Banking](../i/investment_banking.md), [Underwriting](../u/underwriting.md) Activities, and Research** 
2. **Supervision of Trading and [Market](../m/market.md) Making Activities**
3. **Supervision of Brokerage Office Operations**
4. **Sales Supervision, General Supervision of Employees, Regulatory Framework of BD**
5. **Compliance with Financial Responsibility Rules**

## Key Topics Covered

### Supervision of Investment Banking, Underwriting Activities, and Research

This section examines the knowledge required to supervise the dissemination of [investment banking](../i/investment_banking.md) and research information. It focuses on compliance with regulations pertaining to [underwriting](../u/underwriting.md) (both primary and secondary offerings), mergers and acquisitions, public offerings, private placements, and research report dissemination. Key regulations include:

- **Regulation M**: Addresses activities that could manipulate [security](../s/security.md) prices in connection with offerings.
- **Regulation S-K**: Provides detailed instructions for [disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) requirements.
- **Regulation S-X**: Outlines the form and content of [financial statements](../f/financial_statements.md) required.

### Supervision of Trading and Market Making Activities

This section assesses candidates' understanding of the rules and regulations governing trading practices. Topics include the supervision of over-the-counter markets, NYSE and [NASDAQ](../n/nasdaq.md) markets, and roles of [market](../m/market.md) makers and specialists. Key areas include:

- **[Order](../o/order.md) Audit Trail System (OATS)**: Records information relating to orders, quotes, and trades.
- **Regulation ATS (Alternative [Trading Systems](../t/trading_systems.md))**: Governs [trading systems](../t/trading_systems.md) that are not registered as exchanges.
- **FINRA Rule 2020**: Focuses on unethical trading practices.

### Supervision of Brokerage Office Operations

Candidates are tested on their abilities to supervise the operational aspects of a brokerage office, including the management of books and records, safeguarding of [customer](../c/customer.md) funds and securities, and proper maintenance of back-office functions. Critical areas include:

- **[Customer](../c/customer.md) Account Management**: Managing and overseeing new accounts, [customer](../c/customer.md) complaints, and record retention.
- **Regulation S-P**: Governs the privacy of consumer financial information.
- **FINRA Rule 4510**: Relates to recordkeeping requirements for [broker](../b/broker.md)-dealers.

### Sales Supervision, General Supervision of Employees, Regulatory Framework of BD

This section delves into the supervision of sales activities and the oversight of [broker-dealer](../b/broker-dealer.md) personnel. Candidates are evaluated on their ability to ensure compliance with sales practices, advertising regulations, and hiring practices. Key regulations include:

- **FINRA Rule 2210**: Governs communication and sales literature.
- **FINRA Rule 3110**: Requires firms to establish and maintain a system for supervising the activities of their personnel.
- **SEC Rule 15c3-3**: Known as the [Customer](../c/customer.md) Protection Rule, requiring [broker](../b/broker.md)-dealers to protect [customer](../c/customer.md) funds and securities.

### Compliance with Financial Responsibility Rules

This section examines the financial responsibility of [broker](../b/broker.md)-dealers, including net [capital](../c/capital.md) requirements, [customer](../c/customer.md) [reserve requirements](../r/reserve_requirements.md), and the segregation of [customer](../c/customer.md) funds. Candidates must understand how to ensure the financial stability and compliance of their [firm](../f/firm.md) with the following:

- **SEC Rule 15c3-1**: Establishes net [capital](../c/capital.md) requirements for [broker](../b/broker.md)-dealers.
- **SEC Rule 17a-5**: Requires [broker](../b/broker.md)-dealers to file regular financial and operational reports with the SEC.
- **SIPC (Securities [Investor](../i/investor.md) Protection [Corporation](../c/corporation.md))**: Provides limited protection to customers in the event of a [broker-dealer](../b/broker-dealer.md)’s [bankruptcy](../b/bankruptcy.md).

## Eligibility and Preparation

### Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the Series 24 exam, candidates must first pass the [Series 7](../s/series_7.md) (General Securities Representative) or Series 62 (Corporate Securities Limited Representative) exam unless they [hold](../h/hold.md) another valid qualification that meets FINRA’s prerequisites. Candidates must be associated with a FINRA member [firm](../f/firm.md) and must obtain the relevant sponsorship from their employer.

### Preparation Resources

Given the breadth of the material covered in the Series 24 exam, thorough preparation is essential. FINRA provides a content outline to guide candidates through the key areas needed for the test. Several third-party providers also [offer](../o/offer.md) preparatory courses, practice exams, and study guides designed specifically for the Series 24 exam. Popular providers include:

- **Kaplan Financial Education**: Offers comprehensive study packages, practice exams, and live or on-[demand](../d/demand.md) courses. [Kaplan Financial Education](https://www.kaplanfinancial.com/finra)
- **STC (Securities Training [Corporation](../c/corporation.md))**: Provides in-depth guides, practice tests, and classroom instruction [options](../o/options.md). [Securities Training Corporation](https://www.stcusa.com)
- **ExamFX**: Features online courses, interactive learning tools, and simulated exams. [ExamFX](https://www.examfx.com)

## Importance of the Series 24 License

### Professional Pathways

Obtaining the Series 24 license is often a critical step for individuals seeking senior management positions within the securities [industry](../i/industry.md). It not only empowers the individual to oversee compliance and regulatory aspects but also positions them as central figures in maintaining the ethical and financial integrity of their [firm](../f/firm.md). The license is crucial for roles such as:

- **Compliance Officers**: Ensuring that firms adhere to applicable laws and regulations.
- **Branch Managers**: Overseeing operations and sales activities at local branch offices.
- **Regulatory Supervisors**: Managing the [firm](../f/firm.md)'s adherence to both internal policies and external regulatory requirements.

### Regulatory Compliance

The Series 24 exam ensures that principals possess the necessary expertise to implement [robust](../r/robust.md) supervisory systems that protect investors and [uphold](../u/uphold.md) [market](../m/market.md) integrity. With an increasing focus on compliance and regulatory oversight, the license underscores the [principal](../p/principal.md)'s commitment to maintaining a fair and transparent marketplace. Through this credential, FINRA aims to enhance [investor](../i/investor.md) confidence and [trust](../t/trust.md) in the [financial system](../f/financial_system.md).

### Continuous Education

After obtaining the Series 24 license, principals must comply with continuing education requirements to stay informed of regulatory updates and [industry](../i/industry.md) [best practices](../b/best_practices.md). The continuing education program is comprised of:

- **Regulatory Element**: Conducted by FINRA, this includes mandatory training sessions focusing on changes in regulatory requirements or [industry](../i/industry.md) policies.
- **[Firm](../f/firm.md) Element**: Managed by the individual’s [firm](../f/firm.md), this tailored program addresses specific needs and [business](../b/business.md) practices of the [firm](../f/firm.md).

## Series 24 Exam Structure and Scoring

The questions on the Series 24 exam are [weighted](../w/weighted.md) according to their importance and difficulty. The exam includes both scored and unscored (pretest) questions, although candidates [will](../w/will.md) not know which questions are unscored during the test. The major sections and the number of questions allocated to each are as follows:

1. **Supervision of [Investment Banking](../i/investment_banking.md), [Underwriting](../u/underwriting.md) Activities, and Research (31 questions)**
2. **Supervision of Trading and [Market](../m/market.md) Making Activities (41 questions)**
3. **Supervision of Brokerage Office Operations (15 questions)**
4. **Sales Supervision, General Supervision of Employees, Regulatory Framework of BD (49 questions)**
5. **Compliance with Financial Responsibility Rules (14 questions)**

Candidates receive an initial pass or [fail](../f/fail.md) status immediately upon completing the exam, followed by a detailed score report that highlights their performance in each section. A minimum score of 70% is required to pass the exam.

## Conclusion

The Series 24 exam plays an essential role in preparing financial professionals for supervisory roles within the securities [industry](../i/industry.md). Its comprehensive coverage of [investment banking](../i/investment_banking.md), trading, brokerage operations, sales supervision, and financial responsibility ensures that candidates are well-equipped to maintain the integrity and compliance of their firms. As the landscape of the financial [industry](../i/industry.md) continues to evolve, the Series 24 license remains a cornerstone for those striving to manage and lead within this dynamic sector.

To summarize, obtaining the Series 24 license is not just about passing an exam; it's about committing to a higher standard of professional conduct and regulatory compliance, thereby fostering a safer and more transparent investment environment for all participants. For more detailed information and resources, candidates can refer to the FINRA website: [FINRA](https://www.finra.org).
