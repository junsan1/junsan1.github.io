# Straight Line Basis

The straight line [basis](../b/basis.md) is one of the most straightforward and widely used methods of calculating [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) for financial reporting and tax purposes. It involves equally depreciating the [value](../v/value.md) of an [asset](../a/asset.md) over its [useful life](../u/useful_life.md). This method is appreciated for its simplicity and ease of implementation, making it a common choice among businesses and accountants alike.

## What is Depreciation?

[Depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) is the [accounting](../a/accounting.md) process of allocating the cost of a tangible [asset](../a/asset.md) over its [useful life](../u/useful_life.md). It recognizes that assets lose [value](../v/value.md) as they age and generate [revenue](../r/revenue.md). Depreciating an [asset](../a/asset.md) allows a [business](../b/business.md) to match an [expense](../e/expense.md) with the [revenue](../r/revenue.md) generated from that [asset](../a/asset.md). This alignment adheres to the matching principle in [accounting](../a/accounting.md), facilitating more accurate financial reporting.

## Understanding Straight Line Basis

The straight line [basis](../b/basis.md) method calculates [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) with a simple formula:

\[ \text{Annual Depreciation Expense} = \frac{\text{Cost of the Asset} - \text{[Salvage Value](../s/salvage_value.md)}}{\text{[Useful Life](../u/useful_life.md)}} \]

- **Cost of the [Asset](../a/asset.md):** The initial cost incurred to acquire the [asset](../a/asset.md).
- **[Salvage Value](../s/salvage_value.md):** The estimated [residual value](../r/residual_value.md) of the [asset](../a/asset.md) at the end of its [useful life](../u/useful_life.md).
- **[Useful Life](../u/useful_life.md):** The estimated period over which the [asset](../a/asset.md) [will](../w/will.md) generate [revenue](../r/revenue.md).

### Example Calculation

Suppose a company purchases machinery for $100,000. The machinery has an estimated [useful life](../u/useful_life.md) of 10 years and a [salvage value](../s/salvage_value.md) of $10,000. Using the straight line [basis](../b/basis.md) method, the annual [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) [expense](../e/expense.md) would be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Annual [Depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) [Expense](../e/expense.md)} = \frac{\$100,000 - \$10,000}{10} = \$9,000 \]

Each year, the company would record a [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) [expense](../e/expense.md) of $9,000 over the 10-year [useful life](../u/useful_life.md) of the machinery.

## Advantages of Straight Line Basis

1. **Simplicity:** The method is easy to understand and implement, requiring minimal calculations.
2. **Consistency:** [Depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) [expense](../e/expense.md) remains consistent annually, facilitating stable financial projections and reporting.
3. **Predictability:** Fixed annual expenses aid in budgeting and long-term planning.

## Disadvantages of Straight Line Basis

1. **Ignoring [Asset](../a/asset.md) Usage:** The method assumes equal use and benefit from the [asset](../a/asset.md) each year, which may not reflect the actual usage or wear and tear.
2. **Inaccurate [Expense](../e/expense.md) Matching:** For assets that depreciate faster in the initial years, the straight line [basis](../b/basis.md) may not accurately match the [expense](../e/expense.md) with [revenue](../r/revenue.md) generations.
3. **Neglects Technological Obsolescence:** Rapid technological advancements can render an [asset](../a/asset.md) obsolete before the end of its [useful life](../u/useful_life.md).

## Applications in Financial Reporting

### Income Statement Impact

On the [income statement](../i/income_statement.md), the annual [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) [expense](../e/expense.md) is recorded as an [operating expense](../o/operating_expense.md). This reduces the net [income](../i/income.md) of the company, reflecting the cost associated with [asset](../a/asset.md) usage during the reporting period.

### Balance Sheet Impact

On the [balance sheet](../b/balance_sheet.md), the [asset](../a/asset.md)'s [book value](../b/book_value.md) decreases annually by the [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) [expense](../e/expense.md). The [accumulated depreciation](../a/accumulated_depreciation.md) account records the total [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) [expense](../e/expense.md) over time, which is deducted from the [asset](../a/asset.md)'s [original cost](../o/original_cost.md) to reflect its [book value](../b/book_value.md).

### Tax Implications

For tax purposes, the Internal [Revenue](../r/revenue.md) Service (IRS) requires businesses to recover the cost of certain assets through [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md). The straight line [basis](../b/basis.md) is one of the acceptable methods for tax [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md). However, businesses may choose other methods like the Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS) to maximize tax benefits.

## Comparing Straight Line Basis with Other Depreciation Methods

### Declining Balance Method

The [declining balance method](../d/declining_balance_method.md) accelerates [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md), front-loading the [expense](../e/expense.md) in the [asset](../a/asset.md)'s early years. This method may be more suitable for assets that lose [value](../v/value.md) quickly due to heavy initial use or technological advancements.

### Sum-of-the-Years'-Digits Method

This method accelerates [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) similarly to the [declining balance method](../d/declining_balance_method.md) but considers the [asset](../a/asset.md)'s [useful life](../u/useful_life.md) and assigns a fraction of the depreciable amount each year. The annual [expense](../e/expense.md) decreases over time.

### Units of Production Method

The units of production method ties [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) to the actual usage of the [asset](../a/asset.md), ideal for machinery or vehicles that undergo varying levels of use. It calculates [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md) based on output or operational hours.
