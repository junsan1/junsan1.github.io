# Strike Price

In the world of financial trading and investment, particularly in [options](../o/options.md) trading, the concept of the "strike price" is pivotal. The strike price, also known as the [exercise price](../e/excersise_price.md), is the price at which a specific [derivative](../d/derivative.md) contract can be exercised. It is the predetermined price at which the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) (typically [stocks](../s/stock.md), commodities, indices, etc.) can be bought or sold when an [options contract](../o/options_contract.md) is exercised. Understanding the nuances of the strike price is key to executing effective [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md) and managing [financial risk](../f/financial_risk.md).

## Basics of Strike Price

### Definition
The strike price is the fixed price at which the holder of an [options contract](../o/options_contract.md) can purchase ([call option](../c/call_option.md)) or sell ([put option](../p/put.md)) the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md). This price is determined at the outset when the [options contract](../o/options_contract.md) is created and remains constant throughout the contract's life.

### Function
- **[Call Option](../c/call_option.md)**: If the strike price is below the [market price](../m/market_price.md) of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md), the [call option](../c/call_option.md) is said to be "in the [money](../m/money.md)", meaning it is profitable to [exercise](../e/exercise.md). Conversely, if the strike price is above the [market price](../m/market_price.md), the option is "out of the [money](../m/money.md)".
- **[Put Option](../p/put.md)**: If the strike price is above the [market price](../m/market_price.md), the [put option](../p/put.md) is "in the [money](../m/money.md)". If the strike price is below the [market price](../m/market_price.md), the option is "out of the [money](../m/money.md)".

### Importance
The strike price is crucial because it determines the [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md) of an option at any given point and plays a significant role in the decision-making process for traders regarding whether to buy, sell, or [hold](../h/hold.md) an [options contract](../o/options_contract.md).

## How Strike Price Impacts Options Pricing

### Intrinsic Value and Time Value
The price of an [options contract](../o/options_contract.md), also referred to as the [premium](../p/premium.md), is composed of two main elements: [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md) and [time value](../t/time_value.md).

- **[Intrinsic Value](../i/intrinsic_value.md)**: For call [options](../o/options.md), it is the amount by which the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s current [market price](../m/market_price.md) exceeds the strike price. For [put options](../p/put_options.md), it is the amount by which the strike price exceeds the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s current [market price](../m/market_price.md).
- **[Time Value](../t/time_value.md)**: The additional amount paid for the option over its [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md), reflecting the potential for the option to become profitable before expiry due to time remaining.

### At-the-Money, In-the-Money, Out-of-the-Money
- **At-the-[Money](../m/money.md) (ATM)**: When the strike price is approximately equal to the [market price](../m/market_price.md).
- **In-the-[Money](../m/money.md) (ITM)**: When an option has [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md) (profitable if exercised immediately). For calls, this is when the [market price](../m/market_price.md) is above the strike price. For puts, it is when the [market price](../m/market_price.md) is below the strike price.
- **Out-of-the-[Money](../m/money.md) (OTM)**: When an option lacks [intrinsic value](../i/intrinsic_value.md) and is not profitable if exercised. For calls, this is when the [market price](../m/market_price.md) is below the strike price, and for puts, it is when the [market price](../m/market_price.md) is above the strike price.

## Factors Influencing the Choice of Strike Price

### Market Strategy
Selecting a strike price depends heavily on the [trader](../t/trader.md)'s [market](../m/market.md) outlook, strategy, and [risk tolerance](../r/risk_tolerance.md). For example:
- **Bullish Traders** might prefer lower strike prices for call [options](../o/options.md) and higher strike prices for [put options](../p/put_options.md) to [capitalize](../c/capitalize.md) on anticipated price increases in the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md).
- **Bearish Traders** often choose higher strike prices for call [options](../o/options.md) and lower strike prices for puts to benefit from expected downward price movements.

### Volatility
High [volatility](../v/volatility.md) in the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) can lead to higher premiums due to increased chances of the [asset](../a/asset.md) price moving significantly, affecting the decision on strike price selection.

### Time to Expiration
The length of time remaining until the option's [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md) also influences strike price selection. Longer durations allow more time for the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s price to move advantageously relative to the strike price.

## Potential Risks and Considerations

### Premium Costs
Choosing a strike price that is [deep in the money](../d/deep_in_the_money.md) can lead to higher premiums, which increases the cost and [risk](../r/risk.md). Conversely, [out-of-the-money options](../o/out-of-the-money_options.md) have lower premiums but a higher [risk](../r/risk.md) of expiring worthless.

### Market Movements
Unexpected [market](../m/market.md) movements and [volatility](../v/volatility.md) can significantly impact the profitability associated with a chosen strike price. Traders must be prepared to adapt their strategies accordingly.

### Expiration Date
The approaching [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md) can also influence the decision to [exercise](../e/exercise.md) an option, impacting both intrinsic and [time value](../t/time_value.md). Traders should be aware of how the passage of time affects their positions.

## Conclusion

The strike price is a fundamental aspect of [options](../o/options.md) trading that directly influences the [valuation](../v/valuation.md), profitability, and strategic planning related to the [execution](../e/execution.md) of [options](../o/options.md) contracts. Whether manually selected or algorithmically determined, understanding and leveraging strike prices effectively is essential for traders seeking to optimize their financial strategies and manage risks in dynamic [market](../m/market.md) conditions.
