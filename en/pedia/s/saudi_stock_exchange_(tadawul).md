# Saudi Stock Exchange (Tadawul)

The Saudi Stock Exchange (Tadawul) is the sole entity authorized in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia to act as the securities exchange, and it falls under the oversight of the Capital Market Authority (CMA). Established in 2007, Tadawul is a key player in the financial markets of the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region. Tadawul provides a platform for buying, selling, and trading of equities, bonds, sukuk, mutual funds, and exchange-traded funds (ETFs). It aims to support the development of the Saudi capital market in line with the objectives set out in the Kingdom's Vision 2030.

## Market Structure

### Equities
Equities or stocks represent ownership in companies listed on Tadawul. Investors can buy or sell shares through licensed brokers. Some prominent companies listed include Saudi Aramco, Saudi Basic Industries Corporation (SABIC), and Al Rajhi Bank.

### Sukuk and Bonds
Tadawul provides a platform for trading Sharia-compliant sukuk and conventional bonds, which offer investment opportunities with varying risk and return profiles.

### ETFs
ETFs or Exchange-Traded Funds are investment funds traded on the stock exchange, designed to track the performance of an index, commodity, or a basket of assets. They offer diversified exposure and are a popular investment vehicle.

### Mutual Funds
Mutual funds are investment programs funded by shareholders that trade in diversified holdings and are professionally managed. Tadawul lists various mutual funds managed by licensed fund managers.

## Market Indices

### TASI (Tadawul All Share Index)
The TASI is the main stock market index, representing the performance of all listed companies on Tadawul. It serves as a benchmark for the overall health and performance of the equity market.

### Nomu - Parallel Market
Nomu is an alternative equity market with lighter listing requirements, aimed at offering growth opportunities to smaller companies and providing investors with more investment options.

## Trading Platform

Tadawul employs sophisticated trading technology to ensure seamless transaction execution, market transparency, and integrity. It has introduced several technology-driven solutions to improve trading efficiency.

### X-Stream INET
Tadawul uses X-Stream INET, a high-performance trading platform provided by Nasdaq, which supports robust market operations with low latency and high throughput.

### Market Information System (MIS)
The Market Information System provides real-time data and analytics to market participants, facilitating informed decision-making.

## Regulatory Framework

### Capital Market Authority (CMA)
The CMA oversees the functioning of Tadawul to ensure market integrity and investor protection. It sets the regulatory framework, enforces market rules, and oversees compliance.

### Market Surveillance
Tadawul employs advanced market surveillance systems to detect and prevent market manipulation, insider trading, and other fraudulent activities. It collaborates with the CMA to enforce market discipline.

## Vision 2030 and Tadawul

Saudi Vision 2030 is a strategic framework aimed at diversifying the Saudi economy and reducing its dependence on oil. As part of Vision 2030, Tadawul plays a crucial role in developing the capital market by:

### Promoting IPOs
Encouraging more companies to go public to drive capital market growth and diversify investment opportunities.

### Developing New Financial Products
Introducing new financial instruments, such as REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) and Derivatives, to attract a broader range of investors.

### Enhancing Market Participation
Improving accessibility for foreign investors through initiatives like the Qualified Foreign Investor (QFI) program, allowing them to trade selected securities.

### Sustainability and Governance
Implementing high standards for Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria among listed companies to attract global sustainable investment inflows.

### Financial Education
Promoting financial literacy among the populace to increase individual participation in the stock market.

## Statistics

### Market Capitalization
As of 2023, the market capitalization of Tadawul exceeds SAR 10 trillion (approximately USD 2.7 trillion), making it one of the largest stock exchanges in the world.

### Daily Trading Volume
The average daily trading volume on Tadawul is around SAR 10 billion (approximately USD 2.7 billion).

### Number of Listed Companies
Over 200 companies are listed on Tadawul, spanning various sectors, including financial, industrial, healthcare, and consumer services.

### Foreign Ownership
The foreign ownership in the Saudi stock market has been steadily increasing, facilitated by initiatives aimed at easing investment restrictions.
