# Standard & Poor's (S&P)

Standard & Poor's (S&P) is a financial services company that provides a range of products and services in the areas of credit ratings, research, and analytics. Established in 1860, S&P is one of the oldest and most influential firms in financial markets, shaping investment decisions, economic policies, and risk assessments worldwide. S&P is most well-known for its stock market indices, such as the S&P 500, and its widely-used credit ratings.

## Credit Ratings

One of the core services offered by S&P is credit rating. Credit ratings assess the creditworthiness of borrowers, including corporations, governments, and financial instruments. Ratings are divided into two main categories: investment grade and speculative grade (or junk). They range from 'AAA' (highest quality) to 'D' (default).

### Importance of Credit Ratings

1. **Investment Decision-Making:** Investors use S&Pâ€™s ratings to determine the risk level of potential investments.
2. **Cost of Borrowing:** Higher-rated entities are usually able to borrow at lower interest rates.
3. **Market Stability:** Ratings contribute to market transparency and stability by providing consistent criteria for evaluating risk.

### Process

S&P follows a rigorous procedure for issuing credit ratings:
1. **Data Collection:** Financial data, operational history, and economic environment are gathered.
2. **Analysis:** A team of analysts rigorously evaluates the data against established criteria.
3. **Committee Review:** A rating committee reviews the analysis to ensure objectivity and accuracy.
4. **Publication:** Ratings are published and monitored continuously.

## S&P Indices

S&P is also renowned for its broad array of market indices. One of the most famous is the S&P 500, which tracks the performance of 500 of the largest listed companies in the U.S.

### S&P 500

The S&P 500 is a benchmark index widely regarded as one of the best representations of the U.S. stock market. It includes companies from multiple sectors, which provides a comprehensive snapshot of market trends and economic health.

### Other Key Indices

- **S&P Global 1200:** A comprehensive, seven-country index that covers major global markets.
- **S&P MidCap 400:** Focuses on mid-sized companies.
- **S&P SmallCap 600:** Targets smaller enterprises.

## S&P Global

S&P operates under the umbrella of S&P Global, a corporation that provides essential intelligence for companies, governments, and individuals to make decisions with conviction.

### Divisions

1. **S&P Global Ratings:** The business unit responsible for issuing credit ratings.
2. **S&P Global Market Intelligence:** Provides data and analytical insights.
3. **S&P Dow Jones Indices:** Manages and licenses market indices.
4. **S&P Global Platts:** Specializes in energy and commodities information.
