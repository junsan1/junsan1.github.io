# Qatar Stock Exchange (QSE)

The Qatar Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (QSE), previously known as the Doha Securities [Market](../m/market.md) (DSM), is the [principal](../p/principal.md) stock [exchange](../e/exchange.md) in the State of Qatar. Officially established in 1995, the [exchange](../e/exchange.md) has grown to become a key financial hub in the Middle East. With its strategic location, rapid [economic growth](../e/economic_growth.md), and substantial [wealth](../w/wealth.md) driven largely by natural gas and [oil reserves](../o/oil_reserves.md), the QSE has risen to prominence in the global [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md).

## History and Development

Established in 1995, the Doha Securities [Market](../m/market.md) opened to trading in May 1997. Over the years, the [exchange](../e/exchange.md) has undergone significant development and rebranding efforts. In June 2009, the Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) partnered with NYSE [Euronext](../e/euronext.md), taking a 20% stake in the bourse, which subsequently underwent a transformation to become the Qatar Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md). This strategic partnership aimed to enhance the [exchange](../e/exchange.md)’s technological capabilities, governance standards, and overall [market](../m/market.md) competitiveness.

The QSE is regulated by the Qatar [Financial Markets](../f/financial_market.md) Authority (QFMA), which ensures that trading activities are conducted in a fair, transparent, and efficient manner. The QFMA also sets the regulatory framework for listing securities, [market](../m/market.md) conduct, and [investor](../i/investor.md) protection.

## Market Structure

The Qatar Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) operates on an electronic [trading platform](../t/trading_platform.md), utilizing the Universal [Trading Platform](../t/trading_platform.md) (UTP) provided by [Euronext](../e/euronext.md). This platform facilitates efficient [trade](../t/trade.md) [execution](../e/execution.md), high [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md), and reduced [trading costs](../t/trading_costs.md).

### Trading Sessions

The QSE operates from Sunday to Thursday, functioning in two main trading sessions:
1. **Pre-opening Session**: 9:00 AM to 9:30 AM (local time)
2. **Continuous [Trading Session](../t/trading_session.md)**: 9:30 AM to 1:15 PM (local time)

### Listed Companies and Indices

As of 2023, the QSE hosts around 50 [listed](../l/listed.md) companies, spanning various sectors including [finance](../f/finance.md), telecommunications, industrials, consumer services, and [real estate](../r/real_estate.md). Key [listed](../l/listed.md) companies include:
- Qatar [National Bank](../n/national_bank.md) (QNB)
- Industries Qatar (IQ)
- Ooredoo
- Mesaieed Petrochemical [Holding Company](../h/holding_company.md) (MPHC)
- Barwa [Real Estate](../r/real_estate.md)

The QSE is [home](../h/home.md) to several indices, with the Qatar [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) [Index](../i/index.md) (QE [Index](../i/index.md)) being the primary [benchmark](../b/benchmark.md). The QE [Index](../i/index.md) comprises the top 20 most [liquid](../l/liquid.md) and significant [stocks](../s/stock.md) [listed](../l/listed.md) on the [market](../m/market.md). Additional indices include the QE Al Rayan Islamic [Index](../i/index.md), QE All Share [Index](../i/index.md), and several sector-specific indices.

## Trading Mechanism

### Order Types

The QSE supports various [order types](../o/order_types_in_trading.md) including [market](../m/market.md) orders, limit orders, stop orders, and [Good-till-cancelled (GTC) orders](../g/good-till-cancelled_(gtc)_orders.md). These [order types](../o/order_types_in_trading.md) provide traders with flexibility in executing trades based on [market](../m/market.md) conditions.

### Clearing and Settlement

The Qatar Central Securities [Depository](../d/depository.md) (QCSD) is responsible for the [clearing and settlement](../c/clearing_and_settlement.md) of trades. The QCSD functions under the oversight of the QFMA and ensures that post-[trade](../t/trade.md) activities comply with international standards. The settlement cycle for [equity](../e/equity.md) trades is T+2, meaning trades are settled two [business](../b/business.md) days after the [transaction](../t/transaction.md) date.

## Regulatory Framework

The QFMA plays a crucial role in maintaining the integrity of the Qatar Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md). The regulatory framework encompasses:
### Market Conduct Regulations
- Prohibitions against [insider trading](../i/insider.md) and [market manipulation](../m/market_manipulation.md)
- [Disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) requirements for [listed](../l/listed.md) companies
- [Corporate governance](../c/corporate_governance.md) standards

### Listing Requirements
To list on the QSE, companies must meet specific criteria including [financial performance](../f/financial_performance.md), [corporate governance](../c/corporate_governance.md) practices, and shareholding structures. The listing process involves the submission of a [prospectus](../p/prospectus.md), undergoing regulatory scrutiny, and meeting continuous [disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) [obligations](../o/obligation.md).

### Investor Protection
The QFMA enforces rules to protect investors, such as requirements for brokers to segregate client funds, conduct [due diligence](../d/due_diligence.md), and adhere to best [execution](../e/execution.md) practices. Additionally, the QSE offers an [investor](../i/investor.md) education program to enhance [market](../m/market.md) literacy.

## Technological Advancements

In recent years, the Qatar Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) has embraced technological advancements to improve trading [efficiency](../e/efficiency.md) and [market](../m/market.md) accessibility. Key initiatives include:
- **Introduction of the Universal [Trading Platform](../t/trading_platform.md) (UTP)**: This state-of-the-art trading system provides high-speed, low-latency [trade](../t/trade.md) [execution](../e/execution.md).
- **[Market](../m/market.md) Surveillance Systems**: Advanced surveillance systems monitor trading activities in real-time to detect and prevent [market](../m/market.md) abuse.
- **Mobile and Online Trading Platforms**: These platforms enable investors to [trade](../t/trade.md) from anywhere, providing greater [market](../m/market.md) access and convenience.

## Foreign Investment

The QSE actively encourages [foreign investment](../f/foreign_investment.md), [offering](../o/offering.md) several incentives to international investors:
### Ownership Limits
Historically, foreign ownership in Qatari companies was restricted to 25%. However, recent regulations have increased the limit to 49%, making the [market](../m/market.md) more attractive to global investors.

### Tax Exemptions
Foreign investors are exempt from [capital gains tax](../c/capital_gains_tax.md) and [dividend](../d/dividend.md) tax, enhancing the net returns on investments.

### Access to Emerging Markets Indices
In 2014, the QSE was classified as an emerging [market](../m/market.md) by MSCI and S&P Dow Jones Indices, boosting its [visibility](../v/visibility.md) and accessibility to international investors. As a result, Qatari [stocks](../s/stock.md) are now included in major emerging markets indices, attracting passive investment flows.

## Sustainable Investing

Qatar Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) has taken significant steps to promote sustainable [investing](../i/investing.md):
### ESG Reporting
In 2015, the QSE launched the “Sustainable Stock Exchanges (SSE)” initiative, encouraging [listed](../l/listed.md) companies to adopt Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting. This initiative aims to enhance corporate sustainability and attract socially responsible investors.

### Green Bonds
The QSE supports the issuance of [green bonds](../g/green_bonds.md), which [finance](../f/finance.md) environmentally-friendly projects. By providing a platform for [green bonds](../g/green_bonds.md), the [exchange](../e/exchange.md) facilitates the flow of [capital](../c/capital.md) towards sustainable development initiatives.

## Challenges and Opportunities

### Challenges
- **[Market](../m/market.md) [Liquidity](../l/liquidity.md)**: Despite its growth, the QSE faces challenges related to [market](../m/market.md) [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) and trading volumes. Efforts are ongoing to attract more listings and increase [investor](../i/investor.md) participation.
- **Geopolitical Tensions**: Regional geopolitical tensions can impact [investor](../i/investor.md) sentiment and [market](../m/market.md) stability.

### Opportunities
- **Economic [Diversification](../d/diversification.md)**: Qatar’s efforts to diversify its [economy](../e/economy.md) away from oil and gas present significant opportunities for new sectors to be represented in the stock [exchange](../e/exchange.md).
- **Technological Innovation**: Continued investment in technology and fintech solutions can enhance trading [efficiency](../e/efficiency.md) and [market](../m/market.md) accessibility.
- **Sustainability**: The focus on sustainable [investing](../i/investing.md) positions the QSE as a leading [market](../m/market.md) for ESG-conscious investors.

## Conclusion

The Qatar Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) has established itself as a key player in the Middle East’s financial landscape. With a [robust](../r/robust.md) regulatory framework, technological advancements, and initiatives to attract [foreign investment](../f/foreign_investment.md), the QSE is well-positioned for future growth. As Qatar continues to diversify its [economy](../e/economy.md) and emphasize sustainable development, the QSE [will](../w/will.md) play a crucial role in facilitating [capital](../c/capital.md) flows and supporting economic progress.
