# National Market System (NMS)

The National [Market](../m/market.md) System (NMS) is a pivotal component of the United States securities [market](../m/market.md) structure. It is a regulatory framework that encompasses a collection of services, regulations, and procedures aimed at ensuring [transparency](../t/transparency.md) and fair competition among the various trading venues. Established by the Securities Acts Amendments of 1975, the NMS was designed to enhance and unify trading practices across the fragmented landscape of U.S. stock exchanges and trading platforms. Here, we [will](../w/will.md) dive into the key aspects and mechanisms of the NMS, its primary components, and their implications for different [market](../m/market.md) participants including retail traders, institutional investors, and trading firms.

## Objectives of the NMS

The National [Market](../m/market.md) System has several core objectives:

1. **[Transparency](../t/transparency.md)**: Ensuring that the best available prices for securities [listed](../l/listed.md) on U.S. exchanges are easily accessible to all [market](../m/market.md) participants.
2. **Fair Competition**: Promoting a competitive environment among exchanges and other trading venues.
3. **[Efficiency](../e/efficiency.md)**: Facilitating efficient trading across the markets.
4. **Accessibility**: Providing equal access to [market](../m/market.md) data for all participants.
5. **Integrity**: Ensuring the integrity of the securities markets.

## Key Components & Relevant Rules

### Regulation NMS

Regulation NMS, adopted by the Securities and [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) [Commission](../c/commission.md) (SEC) in 2005, is the cornerstone of the National [Market](../m/market.md) System. It includes four key rules:

1. **[Order Protection Rule](../o/order_protection_rule.md) (Rule 611)**: This rule ensures that investors receive the best possible price by mandating that trading centers must execute trades at the best available national price across all competing markets.
2. **Access Rule (Rule 610)**: The access rule aims to promote fair and efficient access to quotations for all [market](../m/market.md) participants.
3. **Sub-Penny Rule (Rule 612)**: This rule controls the minimum pricing increment for quotes, prohibiting the display of prices in increments of less than one cent for [stocks](../s/stock.md) priced above $1.00 per share.
4. **[Market](../m/market.md) Data Rules (Rules 601 and 602)**: These rules pertain to the [consolidation](../c/consolidation.md) and dissemination of [market](../m/market.md) data, ensuring the availability of comprehensive and accurate [market](../m/market.md) information.

### The Consolidated Tape Association (CTA)

The CTA oversees the dissemination of real-time [trade](../t/trade.md) and [quote](../q/quote.md) information via two different “tapes”:

1. **Tape A**: This tape includes securities [listed](../l/listed.md) on the New York Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (NYSE).
2. **Tape B**: This tape includes securities [listed](../l/listed.md) on the American Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (AMEX) and regional exchanges.
3. **Tape C**: This tape includes securities [listed](../l/listed.md) on the [Nasdaq](../n/nasdaq.md) [Stock Market](../s/stock_market.md).

### The Intermarket Trading System (ITS)

The ITS is a system that links the various U.S. stock markets, enabling orders to be routed and executed across [multiple](../m/multiple.md) exchanges. This promotes competition among the exchanges and ensures that orders are executed at the best available prices.

### The National Securities Clearing Corporation (NSCC)

The NSCC plays a crucial role in [post-trade processing](../p/post-trade_processing.md) by [netting](../n/netting.md) and guaranteeing the settlement of trades, thereby reducing the number of securities and amount of cash that must be exchanged on the settlement date. This helps in minimizing [counterparty risk](../c/counterparty_risk.md) and increasing [efficiency](../e/efficiency.md).

### The Securities Information Processor (SIP)

SIPs are responsible for consolidating [quote](../q/quote.md) and [trade](../t/trade.md) data from various participant exchanges and disseminating it to the public. There are different SIPs for different tapes (A, B, and C) as mentioned under the CTA.

## Implications for Different Market Participants

### Retail Traders

For retail traders, the NMS ensures that they have access to the best available prices and [market](../m/market.md) data, thereby leveling the playing field. It enhances [market](../m/market.md) [transparency](../t/transparency.md) and helps protect against price manipulation by ensuring that orders are executed at the best available prices across all trading venues.

### Institutional Investors

Institutional investors benefit from the increased [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) and price competitiveness fostered by the NMS. The framework supports complex [order routing](../o/order_routing.md) and [execution](../e/execution.md) strategies by providing access to [multiple](../m/multiple.md) trading venues, which is particularly advantageous for large orders that could otherwise significantly impact the [market price](../m/market_price.md).

### Trading Firms & Algo-Traders

For algorithmic traders and high-frequency trading (HFT) firms, the NMS framework provides the necessary [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md) to deploy sophisticated [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md) by ensuring fast and reliable access to consolidated [market](../m/market.md) data. The competitive environment among exchanges also enables these firms to find the most favourable [execution](../e/execution.md) conditions.

## Advantages and Criticisms

### Advantages

1. **[Market](../m/market.md) [Transparency](../t/transparency.md)**: The NMS promotes [transparency](../t/transparency.md) by making the best prices from all [market](../m/market.md) centers readily available.
2. **Increased Competition**: It fosters a competitive environment, which generally leads to better prices and lower [trading costs](../t/trading_costs.md).
3. **Regulatory Oversight**: The various regulations under the NMS provide a structured framework for [market](../m/market.md) oversight, enhancing the integrity of the markets.

### Criticisms

1. **Fragmentation**: Critics argue that the NMS framework has led to [market](../m/market.md) fragmentation, with [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) spread across numerous trading venues, which can complicate [order](../o/order.md) [execution](../e/execution.md).
2. **Complexity**: The system's complexity can be a barrier to understanding and navigating the [market](../m/market.md), particularly for retail traders.
3. **Latency [Arbitrage](../a/arbitrage.md)**: The nature of the NMS rules has been exploited by some HFT firms through latency [arbitrage](../a/arbitrage.md) strategies, potentially to the detriment of other [market](../m/market.md) participants.

## Future of the NMS

As markets evolve and technology continues to advance, the NMS is likely to undergo further modifications to address emerging challenges. The SEC continually reviews the effectiveness of the NMS framework and proposes updates to better align with the dynamic nature of [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md). Enhancements in [market](../m/market.md) data [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md), changes in the access to [market](../m/market.md) data, and the ongoing debate about [payment](../p/payment.md) for [order](../o/order.md) flow are some areas where we might see significant regulatory developments in the near future.
